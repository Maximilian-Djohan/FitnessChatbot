if you have have weaker legs in proportions to your upper body but enjoy running upper lower there is a simple change you can make. you can simply change the order of your training days from upper lower rest to lower upper rest. but what does this do?

UPPER LOWER

you accumulate post workout muscle damage and fatigue after workout. so your first session which is usually an upper day can actually impact your lower day. so switching the days will allow you to not be hindered by this fatigue and have that full rest day before hand. while this wont make a significant change it is something that can add up over a long period of time.

Sustained fatigue after exercise is comprised of three elements: [1] muscle fiber damage, [2] excitation-contraction coupling failure, and [3] central nervous system (CNS) fatigue. Yet, all of these elements are ultimately caused by the same original mechanism, which is repeated muscle fiber activation and calcium ion release.
