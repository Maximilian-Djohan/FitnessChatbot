# How fast should your reps be?

How fast should your reps be? What is the best tempo for muscle growth?

the tempo of your reps consists of two parts the concentric and the eccentric. its often explained how you should take your reps slow and controlled to maximize muscle growth but what does this actually mean? how fast should your reps actually be?

for the concentric you want to go as fast as possible. you want to be putting your maximum effort into bringing the weight up. why is this? we know for muscle growth you want to have a high degree of effort to recruit as many muscle fibers as possible. if you were to hold back to do a slower rep you wont be recruiting as many muscle fibers as you arent putting out your highest effort. (and if youre worried about being too fast you just need to pick a challenging enough weight that you physically cant move that fast)

Voluntarily increasing central motor command increases perception of effort, and the number of recruited motor units (and the number of activated muscle fibers)

the eccentric time is arbitrary. it doesnt matter whether you have a long or slow control. since it wont really affect muscle growth as muscle fibers are able to produce more force on the eccentric to the concentric due to passive mechanical tension. this means that less muscle fibers will be recruited on the eccentric anyway as they can produce more force less are needed to move the load. so instead just worry about controlling it to avoid injury

Slower lowering (eccentric) phases cause greater gains in muscle size?

When producing the same muscle force, motor unit recruitment is lower in an eccentric contraction than in a concentric contraction (because the force produced by each muscle fiber is higher in the eccentric). When performing a maximal effort, motor unit recruitment is also lower in an eccentric contraction than in a concentric contraction (perhaps because such contractions are very unfamiliar to most individuals)

WHAT DOES THIS MEAN?

so how long should a rep be? the concentric should be as fast as possible (maximum effort in bringing the weight up) while the concentric being standardized and controlled enough to not cause injury rather than an arbitrary amount of time.
