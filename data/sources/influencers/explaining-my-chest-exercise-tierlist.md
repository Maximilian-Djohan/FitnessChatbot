# explaining my chest exercise tierlist

explaining the reasoning behind my chest exercise tier list

seated low to high fly

starting off to preface just because something is lower on the list does not mean its not effective for muscle growth. rather based on different criteria there will be better options if your goal is to "maximize" growth for said muscle.

starting off with F tier I have the free weight push up variants while you can develop a decent chest this way you will be highly limited in progressive overload potential especially as you become more advanced.

next in C tier I have the standing cable fly while it can effectively develop your chest since youre performing the chests main function it is very unstable limiting you as youre getting pulled back by the weight. next I have the dip which is mainly a tricep exercise. while it can work the lower pec your arms would have to be very flared while being upright to train vertical adduction for the costal pec.

next in B tier I have the unconventional barbell presses and dumbbell presses. the different barbell presses provide no extra value compared to the standard ones in term of training horizontal adduction or shoulder flexion for the chest. and for the dumbbell variations people think these are better than a barbell. but in reality the instability will lead to less motor unit recruitment in the chest and some people say you can go deeper but this doesnt provide any benefit for the chest as going that deep is out of its "optimal" working range.

next in A tier I have the barbell presses. these are relatively stable and easy to progressively overload but are below S/S+ due to not having a fixed arm path. alongside this is the dumbbell pullover which might surprise people. but this exercise trains the top ROM of shoulder extension well which trains the costal pecs secondary function very well.

next in S tier I have different machine pressing variations. this will be VERY effective for the chest as they are training the chests main functions of horizontal adduction/shoulder flexion with a high degree of stability, are easily progressible and have a fixed armpath.

lastly in S+ I have different fly variations that perform the chests main functions. these only rank higher than the other presses in S as they eliminate triceps involvement which will increase MUR in the chest (though there will be some bicep involvement in these).

seated low to high fly

so yes most of these exercises on the list can be effective for growing your chest but due to different criteria i ranked some exercises higher than others.
