# Common form mistakes on curls

Common form mistakes during bicep curl variations which can decrease muscle growth.

we know that in our curl variations our goal is to train the biceps through their primary function of elbow flexion but what are things we should look out for during our set to make sure we are getting the most out of them?

the first mistake is how people orient their body during their bicep curls. we want to maintain the same torso posture throughout our reps. one thing you should be avoiding is this hinging movement during your curls this is where people push their upper body back and their hips forward to try to leverage the weight up with their body rather than through elbow flexion.

next is excessive shoulder flexion during your curl. this will be apparent as you get closer to failure. this would happen as you are flexing the shoulder upward towards the harder parts of ROM to get the weight up and would be apparent on something like a preacher curl as your upper arm would come off the pad. instead you should make sure to try to keep your upper arm as planted against the pad as you can.

lastly is rom which is very common on preacher curls. many people will stop at the hardest part of the rep which is a bit above lockout instead you should be performing your reps with a full rom. ego lifting will eventually lead to more form breakdown and plateaus and have a higher risk of injury as lockout will be beneficial for tendon adaptations in that specific rom.

so overall when performing preacher curls make sure to maintain your torso posture, keep your upper arm on the pad, and use a full range of motion.
