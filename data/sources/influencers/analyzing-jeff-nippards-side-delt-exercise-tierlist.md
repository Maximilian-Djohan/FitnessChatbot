# analyzing jeff nippards side delt exercise tierlist

He starts off with a shoulder press which he puts in A tier.

Some people might disagree with this as they see it as a front delt exercise. But when you look at what you're doing you are training shoulder abduction which is the same as in a lateral raise and when you go far down you are essentially doing a lateral raise with bent arm.

Next is the standing dumbbell lateral raise which he puts in B tier which is below the shoulder press. I disagree with this as it is pretty stable and it is purely training the side delt's function where the front delt isn't involved. I would put this above the shoulder press in A tier as it is a good exercise training shoulder abduction with the main fault that it's harder to progress with the dumbbell weight increments.

Next is the incline dumbbell Y raise which he puts in S tier. I would agree with this as it is good lateral raise variation similar to the last dumbbell one but you have more stability. Also this added stability can help you avoid ego lifting/shrugging which many people end up doing on a dumbbell lateral raise.

Next is the upright row which he puts in B tier above the dumbbell lateral raise. I heavily disagree with this. This is the same movement pattern and resistance as the dumbbell lateral raise except you have unneeded loading on the elbow and wrist. There is no reason to do this over a lateral raise. Another downside is that you can unintentionally work scapular elevation which will take away from the side delts.

Next is a cable lateral raise which he places in S tier. I agree with this and would put this above other dumbbell variants. It is very stable and with the cable stack it will be much easier to progress as it has smaller increments.

He also puts the machine lateral raise in S tier which is also great for the same reasons but also has another level of stability as it has a fixed arm path.

Overall I agree with his "best exercises" for the side delts. But the dumbbell lateral raise is over-hated even though it is a great option and if you want to efficiently grow your side delts you should be doing a lateral raise variation. And yes the shoulder press will effectively grow the side delts but it won't be as good as a lateral raise. And lastly the upright row should be at the bottom of the list.
