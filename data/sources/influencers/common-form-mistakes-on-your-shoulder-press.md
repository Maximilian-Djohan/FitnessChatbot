# Common form mistakes on your shoulder press

Common form mistakes on your shoulder press that can decrease muscle growth

we know that in a shoulder press our goal is to train the front delt through its function of shoulder abduction. but what are things we should look out for during our set?

well the main faults comes from setup. if your goal is to bias the front delts your setup should reflect this. for this you want to have a lower seat to start up higher in the range of motion where the side delts wont be contributing as much. and during this you shouldnt arch to avoid any upper pec involvement.

next is your handle choice. you should be using the wide grip handles where you are in the frontal plane. this will allow you to be performing shoulder abduction rather than picking the inner handles where you will have a more bucked arm path and in turn be training your upper pec through shoulder flexion. during this you should have your wrist and elbow stacked.

so overall find what your goal of your shoulder press is. front delts then use wide grip and less rom. side and front delt then wide grip and more rom and for the upper pec use the inner grips.
