# Do muscles actually benefit from the stretch?

Do muscles actually benefit from the stretch?

popular "science based" content creators have promoted training at a deep stretch as it is "more hypertrophic". but is this actually true and should you train your muscles in a deep stretch?

There are only two situations where you would benefit from the stretch. If the muscle you're training has better leverage at length and/or you are a beginner and haven't maxed out your stretch mediated hypertrophy adaptations (only applicable to certain muscles). but what is stretch mediated hypertrophy and what muscles have better leverage at length?

Stretch mediated hypertrophy is muscle growth due to training at a longer length leading to a process called sarcomerogenesis. This happens as the due to that process there will be enough passive tension in the muscle promoting hypertrophy until you max out SMH adaptations (cant benefit from sarcomerogenesis) and wont benefit from it. but what muscles actually benefit from SMH?

(you will need to maintain these adaptations but you should train a muscle where it has best leverage)

Sarcomerogenesis is the process of adding new sarcomeres in series within a muscle fiber, leading to an increase in muscle fiber length

what other muscles should your train at length? muscles such as your calves or biceps have better relative leverage towards the lengthened portion of the movement which is why they have better growth. not because of the stretch.

Elbow flexor moment arm lengths increase with increasing elbow flexion and are maximal in the contracted position. The brachioradialis has very poor leverage when lengthened and the best leverage when shortened.
