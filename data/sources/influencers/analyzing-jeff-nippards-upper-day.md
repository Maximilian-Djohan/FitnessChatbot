# Analyzing Jeff Nippards Upper Day

Analyzing Jeff Nippard's Upper Day to see if it's effective.

We know for a good upper day you want to train all the muscles in the upper body by training their main functions. So I'll be looking at his workout to see if he trains all of these effectively.

He starts off with an incline smith press to bias the upper pec. This is great option but he is not biasing the upper pec as much as he can as his arms aren't as tucked to allow for more shoulder flexion. But regardless your entire (mid/upper pec) will be trained as they still perform horizontal adduction. He then does a pec dec which is the best exercise for biasing the mid/lower pec.

He then follows this up with a unilateral machine lateral raise. This will be a great option for the side delts and since it's unilateral there will be more motor unit recruitment in the delt since there is less overall musculature that needs to be recruited during the set.

He then finishes this with a preacher curl which will train all of the elbow flexors effectively biasing the biceps and with a tricep pushdown which will train the entire tricep effectively with a bias on the longhead.

So overall his upper day is effective. But in his case I would tuck my arms a bit more on the incline press. And I would at least add a set of a top half shoulder press to better train the front delts through shoulder abduction rather than as a secondary function in a chest movement.
