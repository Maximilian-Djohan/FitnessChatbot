# explaining the reasoning behind my quad exercise tierlist

explaining the reasoning behind my quad exercise tier list

starting off to preface just because something is lower on the list does not mean its not effective for muscle growth. rather based on different criteria there will be better options if your goal is to "maximize" growth for said muscle.

starting off the F tier exercises these exercises have no progressibility since their free weight body movements.

in C tier these exercises lack stability and while they are loaded they still lack progression especially as you are more advanced compared to exercises higher on the list.

in B tier these exercises are much better in terms of stability and progression and will be effective for quad growth but there are still improvements to be made.

In A tier i have more stable variations which lack a lot of the unnecessary instability the other exercises have.

in S tier i have the very stable and easy to progress machine squat variations that have a "fixed path". if wanting to include a squat pattern it should be from here if possible. (if there was a pendulum squat it would be here)

in S+ tier i have the leg extension due to the fact that it will train all parts of the quad including the rectus femoris which is biarticular and will suffer from antagonistic inhibition during a squat pattern causing it to not be trained effectively. meaning a leg extension is REQUIRED if you want to grow all parts of the quad.

so yes most of these exercises can be effective for growing your quads but due to different criteria i ranked some exercises higher than others.
