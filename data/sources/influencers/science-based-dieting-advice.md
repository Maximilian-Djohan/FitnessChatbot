Science Based Dieting Advice

many people at some point in their life try to lose weight we know the sole way to lose weight regardless of person or circumstance is via a calorie deficit. but knowing this what are effective ways that can help you adhere to your diet?

A calorie deficit occurs when you consume fewer calories than your body burns, forcing it to use stored energy (fat) for fuel, resulting in weight loss.

the first thing to consider is being able to adhere to your plan. you can theorize the fastest way to lose weight you should instead do something that is sustainable and healthy (hit macros/micros/feel relatively satiated). the best way to do this is by being flexible with your rules as we can see that restriction often leads to nonadherence. avoiding restriction and starting and ending your day off with a food you enjoy is a great way to do this.

Self-set dieting rules: adherence and prediction of weight loss success

ema, Marjorie Rabiau, Odelia Borter

next are the food choices in your diet. using alternative food choices that are more satiating (filling) this is seen with something such as potatoes / sweet potatoes over something like rice. also introducing more voluminous and fibrous foods such as vegetables will be a great help.

next are more "tricks". using smaller utensils to slow eating, actively thinking about chewing your food more (sounds dumb but helps a lot), drinking more water during your meals, have an environment devoid of foods that make you feel to cheat, planning your proportions beforehand. all of these tips will be able to help either keep you to your diet or help with more mindful eating.

Effects of chewing on appetite, food intake and gut hormones: A systematic review and meta-analysis

DOI: 10.1016/physbeh.2015.07.017

lastly is a tip that might seem counterintuitive to some of the advice I gave earlier. but trying to stay relatively consistent with what you eat and not have an over reliance on zero sugar foods can help with cravings to a degree. having more "bland" food can help deter you away from over consumption or stop binging urges. diet sodas and sauces are fine used sparingly but meals that rely on an overconsumption of their more artificial sources can be harmful.

so overall when dieting make sure its something that you can actually stick to, alter some food choices to help you be better satiated, using tips/tricks to make the diet easier and be mindful of your overall eating habits and choices.
