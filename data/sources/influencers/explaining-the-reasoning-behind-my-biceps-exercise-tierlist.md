# explaining the reasoning behind my biceps exercise tierlist

explaining the reasoning behind my biceps exercise tier list

starting off to preface just because something is lower on the list does not mean its not effective for muscle growth. rather based on different criteria there will be better options if your goal is to "maximize" growth for said muscle.

starting off with C tier is the free weight dumbbell hammer curl. while it will train the biceps it will be sub optimal compared to higher tiers as it uses a neutral grip which disadvantages the biceps. alongside this since its hardest at 90 degrees which is also worse for the biceps as you want a descending resistance profile hardest at the bottom

next is B tier these will also be effective bicep exercises. but they are better than C tier as they all use a supinated grip but they still are hardest at 90 degrees and lack upper arm stability compared to higher tiers on the list

next is A tier these will be great for the biceps. they are relatively stable and can be altered to have a descending resistance profile. but starting with the bayesian curl it will load the shoulder and have constant instability being pulled backwards. the cable curl and concentration curl also have similar problems with instability of the body.

next is S tier with the seated bilateral preacher curl. this is one of the best bicep exercises you can do as its very stable from your torso to your upper arm. it also has a great resistance profile that will bias the biceps over the elbow flexors but compared to S+ tier there are a few adjustments that can be made to better train the biceps

lastly is S+ tier. the machine preacher curl will be very easy to progress and will have the benefit of a fixed arm path. next the unilateral preacher curl which will have the benefit of being unilateral which will result in more muscle fibers being recruited as there is less total musculature being recruited at once. and lastly is the recline curl which can theoretically have some benefit over a preacher curl as there is data that your biceps gains leverage when your arm is more into shoulder extension.

so yes all of these exercises on the list will be effective for growing your biceps but due to different criteria i ranked some exercises higher than others.
