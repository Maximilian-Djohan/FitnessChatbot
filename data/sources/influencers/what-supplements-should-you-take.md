# What supplements should you take?

What supplements should you be taking for overall health / performance?

starting off supplements are not essential. your main priority is to get in your micronutrients through your diet. then the usage of supplements can be used to either cover the deficiencies in your diet or to supplement a high amount of a specific micronutrient that would be hit with food.

starting off are three supplements that a majority of people are statistically shown to be deficient in. starting off is your omega 3s which can be hit via a high quality fish oil/algae oil (EPA/DHA not ALA) if you don't consume fatty fish (even more essential if you're cutting). Then D3 + K2 which is the no.4 micronutrient deficiency and no.2 magnesium which you can also take in the form of magnesium glycinate which can help with sleep quality.

next are supplements which can help but aren't as essential. caffeine / nootropics (alpha gpc) can be taken separately or via preworkout and can help with exercise performance. creatine is a useful cognitive supplement and will make you visually bigger but won't have any statistically significant muscle growth increase compared to any other supplement (it's overhyped). and a electrolyte supplement but not as essential as you can get adequate sodium/potassium etc through your diet.

so overall i'd recommend supplementing as you need which you can do by logging your food via cronometer and see what you're deficient in. besides the stuff i recommended a multi vitamin can be used if you're not consuming a diet which covers it / or have an inconsistent diet. then the supplementation of protein via any protein powder if it helps you reach your daily goal.
