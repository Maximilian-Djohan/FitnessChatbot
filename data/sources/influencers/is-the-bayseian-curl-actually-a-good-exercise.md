# Is the bayseian curl actually a good exercise?

Is the bayesian curl actually a good exercise?

the bayesian curl is a biceps exercise popularized by jeff nippard. he does this saying it helps train the biceps function of elbow flexion with a big stretch. but is this actually an effective way to train the biceps?

when training your biceps you want to pick an exercise that will advantage it over the other elbow flexors. how do we do this? we use a supinated grip (palms up) and a descending resistance profile (hardest at the bottom). the bayesian curl does both of these so wouldn't that mean it's a great exercise?

Biceps brachii moment arm lengths are longer when the wrist is supinated (although only in the middle of the elbow joint angle range of motion). This means that we can therefore target the biceps brachii with supinated grip biceps curls, and the brachioradialis with pronated grip biceps curls.

Elbow flexor moment arm lengths increase with increasing elbow flexion and are maximal in the contracted position. The brachioradialis has very poor leverage when lengthened and the best leverage when shortened.
