# How should you bring up your legs

How should you bring up your legs muscles if you have a leg imbalance compared to your upper body?

some people neglect their lower body for long periods of time but then realize later that they want to train it. by this time their upper body is already far ahead of their lower body. or some people just want to prioritize their lower body. so how should you go about growing your legs effectively?

the first thing is your split. you want a split where you are training your legs as frequently as possible. this can be done through a two day split like UL/AntPos or something with a higher frequency like Full Body. for UL you can switch it to LU to further prioritize your lower, and for Ant/Pos or full body you can have legs first then upper body. frequency is the most important thing when trying to prioritize muscle growth.

Non-linear dose-response relationships of hypertrophy to workout volume vary substantially between meta-analyses, perhaps due to inclusion criteria. When muscle fibers are not in a post-workout stimulus period, they display atrophy. Maintenance studies allow us to determine how fast this happens.

next is to determine exercise selection so what would this look like? you want to target all the main muscles in the legs. you can do this by training all the joint actions that the legs are responsible for. and if you want to take it a step further you should stick to exercises that are only training one joint action to maximize motor unit recruitment for that muscle. so what does this look like?

MUSCLE GROUP
Quads Knee extension
Hamstrings Knee flexion
Add/abductors Adduction/abduction
Glutes Hip extension
Calf Plantar flexion

for quads you will do a leg extension, hamstrings a hinge and hamstring curl, ab/adductor do ab/adduction, glutes do a hip thrust and calves you do a straight leg calf raise. doing all of these exercises or a majority of these exercises youll be able to make great leg growth.

for volume youll train these muscles like you do your other muscles 5-10 reps. for sets you want to be on the higher end of volume that you progress well on which will probably be around 1-2 sets. another thing that people ask is if you need a squat pattern. you dont if you train all the functions that a squat pattern does individually. doing all of this will be great to make your legs catch up
