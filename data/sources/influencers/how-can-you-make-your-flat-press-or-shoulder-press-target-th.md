# how can you make your flat press or shoulder press target the upper pec?

How can you make your flat press or shoulder press bias the upper chest?

your flat press or shoulder press will normally be training horizontal adduction for the chest or abduction for the delts but you can actually alter the setup to make it bias the upper chest.

horizontal adduction: abduction:

its actually quite simple to bias the upper chest in these exercises. you want to take a neutral grip rather than pronated as it will make your arms more tucked. this will allow you to get more shoulder flexion in your press which will train the upper pec.

shoulder flexion:

another smaller change that you can make is raising the seat height this will position your arms in a way that makes them more tucked by your side and will allow for even more shoulder flexion over any other actions occurring. and in terms of how you should position your body keeping yourself upright / slight arch will be good.

so if you want to turn your pressing into more upper pec biased press you can take a closer neutral grip and alter the seat height higher to allow for more shoulder flexion which will bias the upper pec
