# Ultimate Cutting Guide

Ultimate Cutting Guide

- How to setup your dieting/training

in this ill be going over how you should structure your macros and training when dieting. but starting off when should you cut? ideally you should try to be around 10-15% body fat so you should try to cut down to this and maintain this.

first you need to determine your maintenance calories. first measure your height and bodyweight and get your age. then go to do a tdee calculator like www.calculator.net and enter your information. this will give you a general ESTIMATE of your maintenance calories. next we will decide your deficit to apply to this.

first get your weight. next to estimate how much fat you can lose per week you want to estimate your bodyfat percentage. next multiply that percentage by your bodyweight to find your fat mass. then you want to multiply this fat mass by 31 to get deficit needed per day (based on alpert study). then if you want to know how much fat youre losing per week you want to multiply that daily amount by 7 (weekly amount) then divide it by 3500 (~1lb of fat) to see how much you can lose per week.

Weight = 170 lbs

Body Fat = 20% — Fat Mass = 170 x 0.20 = 34 lbs
Daily Fat Calories = 34 x 31 = 1054 kcal/day

Weekly Fat Loss = (1054 x 7) ÷ 3500 = 2.1 lbs/week
Maintenance Calories = 2500 kcal/day

Calories for Fat Loss = 2500 - 1054 = 1446 kcal/day

next is to determine your macros. the biggest over estimation is how much protein you need. all you need is .7-.8g of protein per lb of bodyweight no more is needed regardless if your in maintenance or in a cut. then you want to determine how many fats you will consume which would be around 20-30% of your daily calories. then the rest of your calories should go to carbs.

next is training. some people think your training should change. but realistically it shouldn't. you should try to keep exercises the same and keep training to failure with a high frequency to minimize time in atrophy + all other benefits of high frequency. if your energy levels are suffering make sure youre eating enough carbs pre workout (60-90g) but if it continues you can slightly lower volume.

lastly what most people concern themselves with is cardio. cardio isnt needed but it can be used to help reach your deficit/allow you to consume more calories while being in your deficit. you should do something low intensity like getting your steps, treadmill, bicycle, elliptical. (getting steps in is relatively easy and each step is 0.04-0.05 kcal/step)

So as a summary - find maintenance calories, estimate body fat, estimate deficit, calculate macros, keep training same, optionally do cardio then apply everything and get down to 10-15% bodyfat.
