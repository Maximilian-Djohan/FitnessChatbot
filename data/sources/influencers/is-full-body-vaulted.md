# is full body vaulted

Is fullbody vaulted?

in a recent video TNF claimed that you cant maximize growth with three sets per week. people in response bring up whether full body is actually usefull as it uses low volumes as there is a lot to get through in a session. but we know that when doing something like full body where you are training with a higher frequency it can outweigh more volume. but can you actually maximize growth doing 3 sets on a muscle group per week?

Non-linear dose-response relationships of hypertrophy to workout volume vary substantially between meta-analyses, perhaps due to inclusion criteria.

the answer is simple. you wont maximize growth doing 3 sets per week. but you will still make great progress with a high frequency. so what should you do if you want to get closer to maximizing growth? this is also simple just do more volume while having a high frequency. doing 3 sets will still have good growth but you should be trying to hit as many muscle regions as you can and train close to your max recoverable volume if you want to maximize growth.

When workout training volume is set at a level that permits full recovery before the next workout, a higher training frequency seems to be better for hypertrophy. Increases in maximum isometric strength were significantly greater in the high frequency group.

but what if we compare this to something like upper lower or ppl which have ~2x frequency? you will obviously be able to handle more volume as you have more time to recover training with a lower frequency. but the same concept still applies. train as many muscle regions as you can and train close to your max recoverable volume if you want to maximize growth. so then what is better something like full body or something like upper lower or ppl?

(but keep in mind more subsequent sets of an exercise will have diminishing return)

this depends on your view on mechanisms and data. and where you hold the importance of the extent in which frequency outweighs volume, atrophy data where training more frequently which is minimized with higher frequencies, and things like more stimulating first sets and diminishing returns on sets.

When muscle fibers are not in a post-workout stimulus period, they display atrophy. Maintenance studies allow us to determine how fast this happens.

so what split is the best? this will depend on a lot of things. your lifestyle choices, your programming, your enjoyment all will play a factor in this. and whichever split you pick you will make great progress on. i wont tell you what split is the best it will be something you have to decide for yourself.
