# Is the RDL a good hamstring exercise?

Is the RDL a good hamstring exercise?

one of the most recommended exercises to train your hamstring is the RDL. to train the hamstring we need to perform their function to extend the hip and flex the knee which are both present in an RDL. would this make the RDL a great exercise?

no. the hamstring will not get effectively trained. similar to the hamstring in a squat pattern due to antagonistic inhibition as the hamstring is a biarticular muscle crossing both the hip and knee joint during the movement it will be shortening at one joint and lengthening at the other so it won't be able to produce force and get trained

Antagonistic inhibition, in the context of muscle physiology, refers to the automatic relaxation or lengthening of one muscle (the antagonist) when the other muscle in a pair (the agonist) contracts

instead if you want to train the hip extension function of the hamstring you should keep your knee locked out and perform a straight leg deadlift or hyperextension
