# Does slowing your reps increase muscle growth?

Going slowed and controlled increases mechanical tension? should you be slowing your reps for the most muscle growth?

Mechanical tension is the pulling force on the muscle which signals muscle growth and is the main driver of hypertrophy. and a slower contraction speed will increase mechanical tension on the muscle so should you be slowing your reps for muscle growth?

Mechanical tension in muscle tissue refers to the pulling force exerted on muscle fibers during contraction and stretching.

no you shouldnt slow your reps. for muscle growth you want a high degree of effort and mechanical tension. so when you slow your reps youre holding back effort. so while there will be mechanical tension on the muscle there wont be max effort meaning less muscle fibers will experience it as they arent recruited due to low effort.
