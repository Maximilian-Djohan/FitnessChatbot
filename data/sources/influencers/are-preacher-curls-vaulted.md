# Are preacher curls vaulted?

Are preacher curls vaulted? New study showing preacher curls disadvantage the bicep?

A March 2025 study researchers compared how shoulder position affected elbow flexion resistance training. this was done using two exercises being the preacher curl and bayesian curl. the bayesian curl having an extended shoulder and a preacher having a flexed shoulder. the study was conducted over 10 weeks. what were the results of the study?

Training was performed twice weekly on nonconsecutive days (48 h apart) in the afternoon hours for 10 weeks, with progression in the number of sets over the weeks (week: 1-3: 3 sets; 4-7: 4 sets, weeks 8-10: 5 sets). Training intensities of load were initially set relative to 70% 1RM and were adjusted over the sessions so that the subjects could perform the sets in 8-12 RM to (or very close to) failure. The rest between sets was set to 120 s. The PREA and BAYE biceps curls were performed unilaterally, at a tempo of 1:1 s (concentric and eccentric phases, respectively), with shoulder and arm positions and ranges of motion the same as defined for the 1RM tests (Figure 1).

the biceps long head crosses the shoulder so having a flexed shoulder will shorten it at the shoulder and having an extended shoulder it will lengthen. but how does this influence the results? a potential issue that comes from this is muscle swelling. in an exercise which is more lengthened will cause more calcium ion related fatigue. this would cause more muscle swelling in which it would cause these insignificant differences when measuring through the ultrasound.

but say after all this information you are still wary and want to change your biceps training what should you do? the most important things will still be a descending RP and stability. but the change you will make is by putting your shoulder at a better angle by having it not be flexed (infront of you) you can do this by being closer to the preacher bench or by standing to the side of it.
