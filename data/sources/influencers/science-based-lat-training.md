# Science Based Lat Training

Science Based Lat
Training Explained

the lat is one muscle made up of the thoracic, lumbar, and iliac part but it is commonly referred to as two regions being the upper and lower lat. the entirety of the lat has the function of both shoulder extension and shoulder adduction.

shoulder extension & shoulder adduction

the lats are best trained through these functions throughout a majority of the range of motion. with the exception of the very high degrees of shoulder extension/adduction and very low degrees of adduction where other muscles contribute.

many people ask whether or not you can bias the different lat regions based on the exercise. in theory you would be able to bias different lat regions due to the fact that they aren't FULLY activated during a single exercise as they suffer from the voluntary activation deficit. meaning training one function with one exercise could train some muscle fibers not active in another exercise.

inability of the brain to fully recruit all their motor units during maximal effort

so to train the lats function of shoulder adduction you should perform a wide grip pulldown trying to stay in the frontal plane. (being slightly in the scapular plane is fine)

Frontal

next to train the function of shoulder extension you should perform a pull in the sagittal plane. this would be something like a pullover or some type of close grip row.

so you can get away with just training one function of the lats. but I would recommend splitting the volume and training both functions of shoulder adduction and extension.
