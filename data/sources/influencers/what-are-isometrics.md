# What are isometrics?

What are Isometrics? Are they better then normal reps for muscle growth?

Isometrics are when you contract the muscle while there is no movement at the joint. you can think of this as pushing or pulling on a stationary object where your arm doesnt move. but can this actually be good for muscle growth?

An example of isometric triceps training is pushing your fist against a table while keeping your elbow at a 90-degree angle. You can also perform an isometric triceps extension by pushing down against an immovable object with your arm

first we need to see if isometrics fit the requirements for muscle growth which is a high degree of effort and mechanical tension. these two are both present when doing a max effort isometric. so should you implement them?

Voluntarily increasing central motor command increases perception of effort, and the number of recruited motor units (and the number of activated muscle fibers)

there is a main flaw of isometrics which is progression. the way you would progress an isometric is through producing more force but you cant really track that without specialized equipment. but they still can have their use. you can do them on machines youve maxed out on or use them to train without equipment. so how should you perform them?

its simple you will have normal sets and reps. structure sets the same and have the same rest times but for reps you will do three ~3 second reps with a pause in between. each rep you will be done at max effort. exercise setup is the same as if you were to do on a machine.
