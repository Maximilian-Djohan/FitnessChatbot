# How long should you rest in between sets?

how long should you rest in between sets? (for better muscle growth)

resting in between your sets has a large impact. many people have a short rest time to catch their breath before going into their next set but this isnt adequate enough rest. having a higher rest time around 3-4 minutes is actually shown to result in better muscle growth

LONG REST PERIODS FOR HYPERTROPHY?

SCHOENFELD ET AL, (2016). LONGER INTERSET REST PERIODS ENHANCE MUSCLE STRENGTH AND HYPERTROPHY IN RESISTANCE-TRAINED MEN, J STRENGTH COND RES.

when you hit failure on an exercise this happens due to you hitting your maximum tolerable perception of effort. then if you arent recovered enough from inadequate rest time it increases your perception of effort for the next set causing you to fail quicker from less muscle activation (less growth as less fibers are activated) meaning you want supraspinal cns fatigue to diminish before your next set

The maximum tolerable perception of effort is the limiting factor for a strength training set, because reaching this point prevents further increases in effort (and therefore in motor unit recruitment) from happening.

Supraspinal CNS fatigue, a component of central fatigue, refers to a decrease in motor output from the brain's motor cortex, leading to reduced muscle force production.

so how long should you rest? rest times arent absolute you just want to make sure are completely recovered for your next set. rest times can vary between muscles as you might need to rest more between squats compared to a curl. but you should at least be resting 3 minutes.
