# Is PPL x UL a good split?

Is Push Pull Legs x Upper Lower a good split?

some people like to run PPL x UL compared to a standard PPL or UL split but is this actually an effective approach?

well compared to push pull legs repeat where you have a 1.75x frequency per muscle group you will be bringing that to 2x while still maintaining two rest days. you also wont be having to sacrifice much if any volume. because of this I would "rank" it higher than push pull legs. but it does have a caveat.

with the PPL sessions being shorter you will be able to handle more volume in the given day so while you can more sets to your exercises progression will be harder to track as you will have more intrasession fatigue. and since you have more subsequent training days there will be a higher amount of intersession fatigue and CNS fatigue present during your workouts compared to UL itself.

so what does this mean? overall it will be a great split. and based of frequency and rest days i would say its better than PPL. but I would say its very similar if not worse to a UL rest repeat as sessions are the same, you have a slightly higher frequency, less intrasession fatigue, and less intersession fatigue between workouts. but another consideration if you want to be in the gym more would be UL4x or UL6x.
