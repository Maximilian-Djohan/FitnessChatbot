# How to warmup for your working set

How to warmup for your working set. Does warming up increase muscle growth?

is warming up important will it actually help with performance? doing warmups will help with exercise execution and help induce the post activation potentiation affect to help performance on your working set.

Warming up before exercise is important for performance enhancement.

your warmup should start with a lighter set to help warmup your coordination for your later sets. to do this you can perform a lighter set around 50% of your working weight for 3-5 reps. if your working set is very heavy you can also optionally do a ~75% warmup for 2-3 reps.

Warming up with coordination exercises helps prepare your body for more intense physical activity by improving the communication between your brain and muscles. These exercises enhance your ability to move smoothly and efficiently.

then you should have a heavier warmup set to induce PAP before your working set this should be around 90% of your working weight for 1 rep then rest 1-2 minutes. after you optionally you can a maximum effort schzio rep to peak PAP then go right into your working set.

Post-activation potentiation (PAP) refers to the short-term increase in muscle force production following a conditioning exercise. Essentially, a muscle can generate more force after it has been voluntarily activated by a prior, high-intensity contraction.

SO as a quick summary. do a 50% warmup for 3-5. rest 60-90 sec. optionally a 75% warmup for 2-3. rest 60-90 sec. a ~90% warmup for 1. rest 1-2 minutes. then optionally a schzio rep right into your working weight.
