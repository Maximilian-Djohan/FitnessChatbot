# Why do science based lifters stray away from 4x12

Why do science based lifters stray away from higher volumes? is 4x12 actually bad?

Sample 4x12 Routine Structure
- Structure: 4 sets, 12 reps, 30-90 seconds rest between sets.
- Goal: Hypertrophy (muscle growth) and muscle endurance.
- Intensity: Use a weight where the last few reps of the 4th set are challenging.

we know that more sets leads to more stimulus via mechanical tension to contributing muscle fibers and in turn more growth. so higher volumes aren't inherently bad and can even be beneficial so then why do people stick to lower sets?

Mechanical tension is the force exerted on muscles when they contract against an external load (active) or are stretched (passive), acting as the primary driver of muscle hypertrophy. It involves the physical stretching of muscle fibers during exercise, which mechanoreceptors convert into chemical signals for growth.

well there are two reasons why the first being recovery just because you can do a lot of volume in one session doing too much can hinder you to the point where you won't recover next session or even regress. but what's the second reason?

Being recovered in resistance training means your body has repaired the muscle tissue damage, replenished energy stores (glycogen), and rested the nervous system enough to return to or exceed your previous baseline performances.

the second reason is due to the fatigue mechanisms that hinder you from set to set. as you keep performing subsequent sets you will develop CNS fatigue at a brain level and peripheral fatigue at a physical level that will hinder you from recruiting and training the higher threshold motor units which is the purpose of your training. this can physically be tested as with each subsequent set if you have a decrease in performance via reps/weight as you are recruiting less muscle fibers and with paired with very high volume the end sets won't be very productive.
