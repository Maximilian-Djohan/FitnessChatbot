# Traditional vs Aggressive Cutting

Traditional vs. Aggressive Cutting

What is the difference between a slower cut and a more aggressive cut? Well its simple. Its the rate at which you lose fat by increasing the deficit. But what is better to do? And will muscle growth be slower on one of them?

Deficit size: Maintenance, Small, Moderate, Large
Calories eaten: 2000, 1700-1800, 1500-1600, 1000-1300

Well first many people think that a larger calorie deficit will be detrimental towards muscle growth and associate it with "major" strength loss. But this isn't exactly the case with a lower amount of calories and with people thinking they need to push protein high they end up with very little carbs and is further worsened by bad carb timing. This in turn leads to bad gym performance which they associate with muscle loss. So should you aggressive cut?

Example: of excessive protein vs enough protein

Calories 1500 1500
Protein 180g 130g
Fat 50g 50g
Carbs ~83g ~133g

My recommendation is to do what you think you can stick to. My preference is to opt for a higher deficit especially if you have a higher body fat as you can oxidize more fat for energy. And a general guideline to see how much fat you can lose in a week is to get 1-1.5% of your weight. This is a good estimation of how much fat you can lose in lbs. Then you can multiply this by 3500 to see how much of a deficit you'll need per pound of fat then subtract that off your maintenance

Example: 180 lb person, 2700 kcal maintenance

Step 1: Weekly fat loss estimate
- 1% of 180 = 1.8 lb
- 1.5% of 180 = 2.7 lb

Step 2: Weekly calorie deficit
- 1.8 x 3,500 = 6,300 kcal/week
- 2.7 x 3,500 = 9,450 kcal/week

Step 3: Daily deficit
- 6,300 ÷ 7 = ~900 kcal/day
- 9,450 ÷ 7 = ~1,350 kcal/day

Step 4: Daily calorie target
- 2700 - 900 = ~1,800 kcal/day
- 2700 - 1,350 = ~1,350 kcal/day
