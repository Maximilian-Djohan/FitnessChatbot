well first we need to know what mechanical tension is. mechanical tension is the pulling force on a muscle where the mechanosensors will be detecting this tension. then through mechanotransduction this force will be converted into biochemical signals for muscle growth. but how does the weight you are using effect this?

Mechanical tension is the force exerted on muscle fibers when they contract against resistance

well the weight doesnt inherently determine the degree of mechanical tension you are facing. a better metric would be your contraction speed. a slower contraction speed will lead to a higher degree of mechanical tension. rather the a higher degree of resistance during this contraction would lead to a higher degree of effort which means more muscle fibers will be experiencing this mechanical tension
