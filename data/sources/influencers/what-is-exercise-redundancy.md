# What is exercise redundancy?

What is exercise redundancy? Should you avoid it for most muscle growth?

exercise redundancy is where you have multiple exercises that train the same joint action in the same way i.e same armpath/resistance profile an example of this would be something like a press and a fly training horizontal adduction. but is this redundancy bad and should you avoid it?

redundancy can be bad and good. you could even consider multiple sets of something redundant. but this isn't bad. its fine to do multiple sets of something whether it be through one exercise or multiple as long as you are accounting for the volume. more volume even if the sets are "diminishing" will result in more growth if you're progressing faster.

redundancy will only get bad when you are doing multiple sets spread across multiple to the point where you aren't recovering/progressing.

so exercise redundancy is fine if you're managing volume and progressing. as long as you aren't doing an excessive amount of sets/exercise variations of the same thing where you aren't progressing you'll be fine.
