# How to setup and perform the perfect SLDL

How to setup and perform the perfect SLDL

in a stiff leg deadlift you are training the hip extensor muscles being the hamstrings, adductor magnus and glutes. to do so you are training them through their role of hip extension

first is how you should set it up. you want your knees to be as straight as possible and to maintain that throughout the rep. next for your spine you want it to be consistent throughout the rep. if its rounded and stays rounded thats fine or if its a bit straighter that is also fine but you should avoid going through active spinal extensions as you pull.

next is how you perform the movement. its simple you should have the bar close to your shins/legs while pushing your butt back. your spine should maintain its posture as you are not bending at the spine you are only hinging at the hips. and you should try your best to maintain your knee angle throughout the rep. depth will be dependent on your structure you can stop around knee height or go down all the way if you want.
