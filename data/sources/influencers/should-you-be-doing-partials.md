# Should you be doing partials?

Are partials vaulted? Partials actually decrease muscle growth?

first lets define what a partial is. a partial is where you cant complete a full rep but continue doing partial rom reps and some people like to do these until they cant anymore to reach what they believe is "true failure" opposed to stopping when you cant complete a full rep. why does this happen and is it actually detrimental.

SHORT-LENGTH PARTIALS LONG-LENGTH PARTIALS

when you reach failure/0 rir where you cant complete another full rep your brain has reached its maximum tolerable perception of efforts when your brain says you cant go anymore. but if you decide to with partials it wont be productive as your brain wont be able to recruit many muscle fibers and you will be generating exponentially more fatigue with very little benefit.

There are multiple types of fatigue mechanism present during a strength training set. Each type of fatigue mechanism accumulates at a different rate, and has different effects. Some fatigue mechanisms mainly affect single muscle fiber shortening velocity, others reduce motor unit recruitment, and others reduce single muscle fiber force.

Proximity to failure (measured by reps in reserve)

this basically means that you wont be stimulating as many muscle fibers during the partials but accumulating exponentially more fatigue compared to your normal reps. so what should you do? instead of doing partials stop your set when you know you cant get another full rep and if you want more volume do another straight set instead.
