# Science Based Hamstring Training

Science Based
Hamstring Training

the hamstring is a group of muscles that all work to flex the knee. all of them are biarticular crossing the hip and knee meaning alongside knee flexion also work to extend the hip except for the biceps femoris short head which doesn't cross the hip.

the first factor that affects training is antagonistic inhibition since the majority of the hamstring muscles are biarticular crossing both the hip and the knee. in an exercise where you are lengthening at the hip and the knee these muscles won't get worked "effectively". this is seen in a squat pattern where the is a high degree of antagonistic joint torque. this will apply to something like an RDL as well but since there is a lower degree of antagonistic joint torque you will still train the hamstrings.

one of the main questions is do you need both a hinge training hip extension and a curl training knee flexion? well you NEED a curl to train the entirety of the hamstring since the entire hamstring doesn't perform hip extension. so do you need a hinge?

well it is beneficial for the hamstrings to have both a hip extension (hinge) and a knee flexion (curl) exercise. this is as the hamstring curl will give more regional growth distally i.e towards the knee and the hinge will give more regional growth proximally towards the hip.

another question is whether or not its better to pick a seated or lying hamstring curl. well in theory when you are seated you will be more flexed at the hip lengthening the hamstrings giving them "better leverage" which can benefit the biarticular hamstring muscles. but overall the degree of difference it will have won't make much of a difference and you should just pick a hamstring curl you like and stick to it. (this concept is further seen when people lean forward on their hamstring curl to further lengthen the hamstrings)

so when training your hamstrings you should have a hamstring curl to train the entire hamstring with more distal growth. then alongside this you should also have a hinge to train the biarticular hamstring muscles with more distal growth.
