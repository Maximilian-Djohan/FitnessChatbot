# Tiktok science based lifting

Ryan Jewers says that "tiktok science based lifting" isn't effective. Is this true?

His first point is that "maximalist 40 set" workouts are dumb. This is true. The credible SBL don't promote this. Pick muscle regions you want to grow and do exercises to train the joint action for it. For most people this will be around 8-15 functions. He also states to do 5-8 exercises. But this is not enough to cover most important muscle regions especially on something like full body.

You're not a "maximalist." You would need 42-47 exercises per workout, excluding the forearms, and use exercise selection to prioritize muscles (you'd be training everything 2-3 times per week).

Next is his statement about enjoyment. He is correct having more enjoyment and in turn motivation in your workout you will reach your maximum tolerable perceived effort later in your set. But this all comes with the assumption that people don't like the way they train. A majority of people enjoy training optimally so this point is irrelevant.

Furthermore, personal enjoyment can affect perception of effort, which is directly correlated to motor unit recruitment (MUR). If you're unmotivated and/or doing something you don't enjoy, you reach your maximum tolerable perception of effort faster, thus limiting MUR

Strive to be optimal, and strive to make the fastest muscle gains, but that will look different for everyone..and guess what? It's not race, so why are we acting like it? Your training should be enjoyable, always. Who's to tell you it shouldn't?

Besides, 99% of you will not make a career out of bodybuilding, you need the 0.01% genetics in terms of shape and size, then you also need the 0.01% genetics in terms of response to PEDs,

Next he explains how full body isn't needed and 3x+ frequency is overrated. He uses anecdote to prove this. But you can also say this from the opposite side. Many people who do run full body do it for the reason that they have made great progress on it. And also to insinuate that data shows a worse outcome is disingenuous as frequency studies have fullbody done with irrecoverable volumes. But yes whether there is a significance going from 2x -> 3x frequency can be argued.

This is only 3 years of consistent training with very strict dieting. My genetics are not an anomaly, and yes I'm 100% natural here. Lots of sub-180 pound FB dweebs like to cope by saying all my progress is from steroids (they won't look like this after 8 years of training).

Yes rage bait isn't productive and sets SBL back and saying "genetics" is indeed cope. And on his anecdote of arm training maybe if he did keenan flaps his biceps would be able to progress better on a higher frequency. And yes calling people "dweebs" is cringe this isn't an episode of ben 10

At some point we also have to stop blaming genetics. There is a reason why those who train like a dweeb stay looking like a dweeb.

Being science-based isn't just about using exercise science literature—it's also about using anecdote. And some things aren't even fully concrete, mind you. The rage bait content and treating things so black and white needs to stop.

For example, I have vigorously tested 2x vs 3x frequency with varying volumes for my arms over the years, as they have always been a weak point. For me, training them twice per week has resulted in better progress. I've seen the same pattern with other muscle groups in clients I have worked with
