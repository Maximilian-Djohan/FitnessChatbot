# Science Based Side Delt Training

Science Based Side Delt
Training Explained

the side delt is one of the three main divisions of the deltoid muscle. its the very middle part of the deltoid between the front and rear delt. its primary function is to raise the arm through shoulder abduction.

the side delt is best trained effectively throughout 0 to ~95 degrees of shoulder abduction regardless of resistance profile. so you should aim to train through a "full" range of motion when performing shoulder abduction.

something to keep in mind is there actually is 7 sub divisions in the deltoid amongst the "main" 3 divisions. by performing your shoulder exercises / lateral raise in different planes of motions you can theoretically bias different sub divisions of the delt with different lines of pull in different planes. though in practice this won't make a significant difference. (if you want to do it go for it)

another thing to keep in mind before I get into exercise selection is the "fear" of your traps taking over. this is a non issue as long as you maintain posture to not shrug the trap upwards through scapular elevation this won't be a problem. regardless of what you do the traps will be involved to some degree as they also aid in shoulder abduction bringing the arm upwards.

now for exercise selection you should just perform a machine or cable lateral raise as they are very stable and easy to progress I would recommend sticking close to the frontal plane when performing your lateral raise. and afterwards if you want to dedicate additional volume towards performing it in another plane you can if you want (imo insignificant)
