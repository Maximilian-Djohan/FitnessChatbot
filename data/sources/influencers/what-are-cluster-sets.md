# What are cluster sets?

What are clusters sets? Are they important to implement into your training?

a cluster set is where you have periods of rest in between your reps usually performing the set within or around your 5 rep max. this can look like 1 rep followed by say ~30 second rest repeated until your 5 reps are done. this is essentially performing a straight set of 5 reps but with rest in-between them. this rest has the purpose of having metabolite fatigue dissipate which would make it "better" then a straight set.

Metabolic fatigue during resistance training occurs from depleted energy (ATP) and buildup of byproducts (lactate, phosphate, etc.), causing a "burning" sensation

for a majority of people there will be no need to perform clusters over straight sets. but I have implemented it for some under 3 scenarios. the first being they are plateaued on a specific exercise for a prolonged time. the second being they are more focused on an increase of strength. or lastly that they don't want to / cannot perform an additional set due to time or recovery issues. so how can clusters help with this?

"Straight sets" means performing all repetitions (reps) for one set of an exercise consecutively, resting fully, and then repeating for the next set

rather than a "dragged" out cluster I would make them perform 1-2 reps using a heavier weight then their working set at 2-3 RIR before/alongside their working set. this will be very quick to perform and utilizing a higher weight with RIR can increase volume and help improve coordination/neural adaptations faster. this can help with plateaus, strength goals on an exercise or add volume that wont be very fatiguing/hard to recover from.

ex. 50LB Preacher 1-2 reps @ 2-3 RIR into their normal preacher set

so clusters can be used in certain scenarios trying to achieve a certain goal. but in my opinion its not worth the time to perform over a straight set just because you can as the differences in growth arent significant. and as a side note these are different then dropsets/myo reps as those are performed AFTER failure where youre already impaired by fatigue (CNS fatigue).
