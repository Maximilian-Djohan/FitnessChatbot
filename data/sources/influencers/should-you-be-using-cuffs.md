recently some "coach" creators made videos explaining how cuffs aren't actually beneficial for increasing MUR. their argument is that by using a cuff moving the force from the hand to the forearm decreases the moment arm. as a result from this you will increase the weight as it becomes easier when you decrease the moment arm. say as you are now moving a heavier weight there will be more stability demand which will make the exercise worse and decrease motor unit recruitment but is this true?

using their example of a tricep extension they say that weight increase will cause instability on the shoulder causing you to shrug. first off decreasing the moment arm by an inch wont cause a drastic weight increase and you shouldn't need to worry about any stability issues. regardless having the weight at the hand causes even more stability issues as you will have to be doing an isometric at the wrist through all of its motions

but what if it does cause some stability issues? realistically it wont be anything significant you can argue the extra motor unit recruitment will outweigh the stability demand. but what if you still think its a problem? there are other ways to fix stability without involving your forearms. you can fix your arm against something or use something like a bench to increase your stability.

(these are the same type of people who argue that science based lifters are trying to be too stable then say cuffs are bad because of their supposed instability)

yes you should still opt for cuffs on most exercises. they are overexaggerating the stability demand of decreasing the moment arm. yes can some setups can cause more of a stability demand but you should try to fix it without having to make the exercise worse by involving other muscle groups. yes for a majority of people and a majority of exercises cuffing will be better.
