# How to "Maximize" Chest growth by training all of its functions.

How to maximize chest growth by training all of its functions.

we know that to train a muscle you want to train all of its actions. and most people already train some of these functions in their presses or flys but if you want to truly maximize your chest growth what actions can you perform?

to start off the first and most important joint action of the chest is horizontal shoulder adduction for the sternocostal pec and this is best trained through something like a pec dec

next for the clavicular or upper pec its joint action is shoulder flexion this will be best trained through a front raise/low to high fly from 20-120 degrees.

now to more uncommon exercises for the chest is to train shoulder adduction in something like a bottom rom keenan flap for the sternocostal pec as within the last 40 degrees of the rom as it will have better leverage over the lats here.

next is to train shoulder extension through something like a top rom pullover for the costal pec as the arms are overhead the lats wont have good leverage

Free weight (EZ bar) pull over exercise involves greater pectoralis major and triceps brachii (long head) muscle activation, compared to latissimus dorsi muscle activation

lastly something to keep in mind is that the pec minor will be active in some movements in the scapula such as depression and protraction but other muscles share these functions like the serratus anterior and traps

so do you need to train all of these? of course not. you will have great training only training shoulder flexion and horizontal adduction but if you want to train other functions no one is stopping you.
