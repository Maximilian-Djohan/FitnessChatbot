# why can your first reps be harder then the rest?

Why can the first rep be more challenging then the next reps? does this matter for muscle growth?

after the first rep the stretch reflex will be present. as you go down on the eccentric your tendons will store elastic energy which will help you on the concentric. also the post activation potentiation effect where the muscle will be able to produce more force after a concentric might not be present if your warmup wasn't adequate allowing it to be present only after your first rep.

The stretch reflex and stored elastic energy

During the eccentric (lowering) phase of a repetition, the muscle-tendon unit is stretched, initiating the stretch-shortening cycle (SSC).

so what does this mean? its normal as you're starting from a dead stop so you wouldn't have this "momentum". though if its due to PAP it could be due to poor warmup. another factor could be that you start from a rom that you dont use on your next reps e.g. starting below 90 degrees on shoulder press but then stopping at 90 degrees on the next reps.

so what should you do? standardize your reps and have a good warmup and you will be good.
