How much weight would you gain if you binged during thanksgiving?

many people like to eat a lot during thanksgiving and some people see this as their one opportunity to go off their "plan". but how much would this actually affect you afterwards?

What Are Thanksgiving foods?

much would it take to actually gain weight? well first we need to differentiate fat gain and scale weight. first going over fat gain we know that around 3500 excess cals will be ~1lb in fat. an average maintenance amount would be 2000 cals and having an excess of 3500 on top of what you would have to eat around 1.5x pumpkin pies or 1/3 of a turkey or 1.3x of a batch of macaroni. for most people this would be higher than the upper limit being around ~5k calories for the entire day at the most

some people say they gain even more than this up to 5-10+ lbs of weight the next day. but this shouldnt be confused with fat. you will have water retention from carbs and sodium. when you consume more carbs you will retain multiple grams of water for each gram of carb (as glycogen). this could also be even more excessive if you were depleted. then this is also the case for sodium but it will extracellular which will give you more of a "bloated" look i.e face puffiness.

Carbohydrate (glycogen): ~3g water per 1g carb. Water is stored intracellularly in muscles and liver along with glycogen. Range: 2-4g water per g glycogen depending on individual.

Sodium: ~100-200 mL water per 1g sodium (~0.22-0.44 lbs). Water is retained extracellularly, often causing bloating. Highly individual; salt-sensitive people retain more.

Quick takeaway:
- 100g of carbs ~300g (0.66 lbs) of water retained.
- 1g of sodium ~100-200 mL (0.22-0.44 lbs) of water retained

so what does this mean? at most youd gain a pound of fat and retain up to 5-10lbs of water. but this is fine as its only a day out of the year. if you arent competing you shouldnt worry about being off track for one day as you will easily get rid of a lb of fat and the water retention. so you shouldnt worry much and enjoy your thanksgiving.
