# what is rib compression?

What is rib compression? Can your exercise execution be holding you back?

rib compression is the orientation of your ribcage during the lift.

this happens through expansion and compression. this happens as your body is smart and when you are focusing on a task i.e lifting from point A to point B your body will make sacrifices in form to complete it if you're unaware. this will impact you as you can restrict the movement of your scapula/shoulder and on chest, shoulder, back training end up not effectively working what you're trying to target.

this is very often seen in pressing movements. this is as people expand and compress their ribcage which restricts scapular movement and distributes force throughout the ribcage and through supporting muscles in order to move the weight. this also is used to decrease the rom needed for the lift.

another example of this can be in your abduction work. in something like a lateral raise some people end up pushing their chest upward in order to help them abduct their arms in a higher degree of rom. this is due to constant compression over time that will restrict scapular movement. in this case he needs to rely on moving his torso to finish the lift as he lacks the mobility to raise his arms that high without it.

another factor is that it can change the plane you are working in. this can be seen in a shoulder press as some people compensate by shifting their body away from the frontal plane. shifting it to include some horizontal adduction requiring the chest to get involved. this is also seen in pulldowns as people stray from the frontal plane into the scapular/sagittal plane training a different function of the lats or even their upper back.

so what should you be doing? you should avoid having ACTIVE torso movement during your reps. ideally you should be keeping your ribcage stacked over your pelvis during the lift but a slight arch if it is constant throughout the exercise will also be fine.
