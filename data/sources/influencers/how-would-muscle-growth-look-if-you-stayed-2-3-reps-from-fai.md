# How would muscle growth look if you stayed 2-3 reps from failure?

How would muscle growth look if you didnt train to failure and left 2-3 reps in the tank?

we know that we need to train close to failure to grow muscle. this is because you will be training with an intensity where you have a high degree of effort where more muscle fibers will be under mechanical tension meaning more muscle fibers will be growing. these most effective reps would be the last ~5 reps in a set but what if you stopped 2-3 reps before failure?

well in data leaving up to 2-3 reps to failure can produce very similar if not the same muscle growth as going to failure. it would also have the benefit of reducing CNS/Calcium ion related fatigue as they are exponentially with each rep as you get closer to failure. so what should you do?

Training to failure and stopping short of failure cause similar hypertrophy?

in theory these benefits do seem good. but in practice i would just recommend training to 0 rir (cant complete another full rep) or at most 1 rir. these fatigue benefits statically are there but in practice the added fatigue of another rep wont limit you (in most cases). another factor is many people cant gauge rir and THINK they are training harder then they actually are
