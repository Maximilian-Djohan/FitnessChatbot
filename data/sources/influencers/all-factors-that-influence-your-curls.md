# All factors that influence your curls

What are all factors that influence your bicep curl? What makes one better than another?

There are many different factors that will influence a curl and can make one variation "better" than another but what are these factors and what is the "best" curl? Well there are many factors such as stability/setup, wrist position, resistance profile, and length changes via shoulder angle. But what are examples of these and should you implement them?

Well first is the setup and stability. Being seated will help decrease perception of effort compared to standing variants as well as machines with a fixed arm path can also do the same via less coordination demand. And one of the more important things you can implement is an upper arm support that acts as a counter force to your curling motion. All of these things can help increase motor unit recruitment.

Next is wrist position which will be the main factor to consider when trying to bias a certain elbow flexor. Being supinated will better train the biceps and we see as we start to switch to a neutral to pronated grip we are disadvantaging the biceps in favor of the other elbow flexors.

Next is the resistance profile. Based on the length tension relationship while also considering internal moment arm lengths we are able to see that the biceps favor a descending resistance profile hardest at the bottom. This would be via something like a recline or preacher curl.

Lastly is your shoulder angle which recently has become more popular. The biceps also have a role at the shoulder and their length will change with your shoulder angle. Having your arm in front of you can shorten it and having your arm behind you can lengthen it. And based on the LTR we can infer that they will be trained better at a longer length.

Lastly you can even consider whether something is single limb or uses both limbs. The idea behind this is that a unilateral exercise will use less total musculature during your set which will allow you to recruit more muscle fibers in the side that you are training.

So all the factors that you can look for is being stable, using a supinated grip, a descending resistance profile, and possibly having your arm to your side/behind you and using a unilateral variation. But we also have to consider that the biceps are a small muscle meaning "optimizations" won't make a significant difference as they have a low voluntary activation deficit meaning it will be easy to get close to fully recruiting the muscle. So what does this mean?

Voluntary activation deficit is the inability of the central nervous system to fully activate muscles during a maximum effort.

We know all of the different factors that we can implement but we also know that trying to be super specific isn't needed and won't be "important". So I would recommend doing something easy that fulfills a lot of these factors that doesn't require much of a setup. And great options for this are DB/Machine Preacher or Recline curls.
