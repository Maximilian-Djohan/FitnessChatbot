# Should you do 5 sets of calf raises?

TNF does 5 Sets for calves should YOU be doing 5 sets for your calves?

some people have seen how many sets TNF does on his calves and asked me if they should do the same. well first what does volume do and how can we determine how much we should do? its simple volume is stimulus and more stimulus will lead to more muscle growth. so should you be doing more sets?

well first you have to consider: can you recover from more sets and are these sets actually speeding up your rate of progression? the way to do this is just by testing/adding sets week to week making sure youre recovered then see if your rate of progression actually increases via reps/weight. volume is diminishing meaning each subsequent set will be less effective as well as more sets will increase intrasession fatigue which will also affect other muscles/exercises so volume isnt always the answer.

Recovered training status is best identified by a return to baseline performance (matching or exceeding previous weight/reps)

so does this mean you should raise your sets specifically on calf raises? realistically most people can easily do 2+ sets per session for them while recovering/progressing faster. but another thing you have to consider is that your training is goal specific. do you care to allocate the time to do say 5 sets just for calves? for most people this will be no. but if this is something youd consider go for it. though i would recommend splitting calf volume between different variations i.e internally/externally rotated ankles and even seated for the soleus.
