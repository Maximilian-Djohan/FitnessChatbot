# Is the preacher curl vaulted?

Is the preacher curl vaulted for the biceps? Are recline curls better for the biceps?

first what is the argument behind the preacher vs recline curl? well in your curl you are training elbow flexion. but we also know that the biceps have a secondary role at the shoulder for shoulder flexion. this will also mean that how you position your upper arm will impact the length of the biceps. having it in front of you will shorten it and having it farther back will lengthen it. so what does all this mean?

well first lets look at what the studies say about this. there are two main studies which people reference an Incline vs. Preacher curl study and Bayesian curl vs. Preacher curl study. both of these studies showed a statistically insignificant outcomes. meaning that biceps growth was similar.

then why do people say that a recline curl can theoretically be better? well in data we can still see an trend (albeit insignificant) that the preacher curl might train the brachialis better relatively. as well as when looking at the length tension relationship of the biceps we can infer that they will be trained better at a longer length in which you are doing having your arm behind you. so what does all of this mean?

W.M. Murray et al. / Journal of Biomechanics 33 (2000) 943-952

so if there is all this theoretical benefit why don't we see a significance in outcomes? well first the biceps are a small muscle and because of this they have a low voluntary activation deficit.

this means it's easy to recruit a majority of the muscle already

So any optimizations will only make a small difference. another reason is due to the flawed studies with the participants, small time frame and no standardization of the exercises.

Voluntary activation deficit is the inability of the central nervous system to fully activate muscles during a maximum effort, resulting in reduced strength.

so what should you do? if you have a good stable setup and enjoy doing a recline curl go for it there is no harm and only a possible benefit there are even common curl machines that serve this purpose. but is this something to stress about in comparison to a preacher curl? no the preacher curl is still a great option for all the elbow flexors just like a recline curl. the main factor will just be having a supinated (palms up) grip as you curl.
