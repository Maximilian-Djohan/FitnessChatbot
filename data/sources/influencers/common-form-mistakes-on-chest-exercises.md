# Common form mistakes on chest exercises

Common form mistakes during your chest press that can decrease muscle growth

we know that in a chest press our goal is to train the chest through its function of horizontal adduction. but what are things we should look out for during our set?

the first issue that many people have is how they orient themselves on the machine. many people have an arch and in turn use a lot of rib movement/contort their body to push the weight up. instead you should try to keep a stacked unmoving ribcage.

next is your grip width when pressing. you should try to have your wrists and elbows stacked throughout the movement to eliminate torque at the elbow and in turn limit tricep involvement.

so the things you should be looking for is keeping posture in your torso avoiding any unnecessary movement and stacking your wrist and elbow to limit tricep involvement.
