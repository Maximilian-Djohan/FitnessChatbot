# Your upper back row is more of an rear delt exercise then upper back?

Your upper back row is more of a rear delt exercise than an upper back exercise.

Many people do an upper back row in order to train their upper back muscles, the traps/rhomboids. We know that to train a muscle you want to perform its function. The upper back muscles' function is to retract the scapula. We know that this happens in an upper back row, so is it a good movement for the upper back?

Retracting the scapula is only a tiny part of the exercise. The majority of the exercise comes from bringing your arms back through horizontal extension. This means that through a majority of the movement you are just training your rear delts.

(Also if your arms are more tucked you will be training your lats alongside your rear delts instead of your upper back.)

Pectoralis major and anterior deltoid both contribute to horizontal flexion (as in the pec deck) but the posterior deltoid is the main contributor to horizontal extension.

So is an upper back row a bad exercise for the traps/rhomboids? Of course not. It is a good exercise but something like a shrug will be better as you will purely be training scapular retraction, which allows you to have more MUR and in turn train more muscle fibers in your upper back.
