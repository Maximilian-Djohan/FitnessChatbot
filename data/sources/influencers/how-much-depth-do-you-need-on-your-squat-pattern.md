# How much depth do you need on your squat pattern?

How much depth do you need on your squat patterns for best muscle growth?

first we need to determine what is the purpose of your squat pattern. there are two main purposes for a squat pattern. the first being to primarily train the quads through a compound or second wanting an overall leg compound to train multiple muscle groups. so how much depth do you need depending on your goal?

how does depth affect the exercise? we know you when youre squatting you do so to challenge knee extension. but as you get more depth you get more knee flexion but also your hips start to flex meaning you start to also challenge the muscles that perform hip extension. so how should you squat?

for a quad biased squat going to 90 degrees is sufficient as when you go down more you will get more hip flexion alongside your knee flexion. and if you want to train other muscles in your squat going further allowing for more hip flexion will allow you to challenge more hip extension which would involve a lot more adductor magnus a bit glutes (for this go as deep as you are able to maintain stability).

The proportional contribution of the hip muscles to hip extension torque changes with squat depth and load (percentage of 1RM)

so basically in a quad biased squat around 90 degrees for knee flexion will be is sufficient and if you want to challenge your hip extension muscles you can go deeper. but keep in mind even when going deeper your quads will still be the primary muscle being trained.

(this can be slightly different based on the squat pattern but a majority of squat patterns are the same being descending - leg press, hacksquat, smith/barbell squat.)
