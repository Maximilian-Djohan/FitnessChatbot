# Is there a benefit to strapping yourself down like a science based lifter?

Is there actually a benefit in tying yourself down like a science based lifter?

We know that being more stable on exercises is important as it will make the task "easier" meaning it will decrease your perception of effort which will allow for more motor unit recruitment and in turn train more muscle fibers. so should you be belting yourself down like a science based lifter?

using things like seatbelts WILL help increase stability of the exercise allowing you to get more out of it. except if it is uncomfortable. if it's uncomfortable it can have the opposite effect as that sensation will increase your perception of effort. but this concept of "stability maxxing" is also seen in isokinetic testing for strength in athletes
