# does tracking your lifts matter?

Does tracking your lifts matter? Tracking your lifts can actually increase muscle growth?

some people like to go to the gym and just go through the motions of their workout. some people make sure they are doing the same exercises in the same order. and after that some people like to make sure they log everything with their sets, weight and reps for each exercise. but does logging actually matter?

Tracking your lifts means systematically recording the weights, sets, and repetitions you perform during your weight training sessions. It involves keeping a detailed log of your workouts, whether through a journal, app, or other method.

besides helping you keep track of what you should be doing each session which is already a good enough positive does it help with anything else? being able to visually see what you did before can help increase motivation as you try to surpass it. this can be helpful as when your motivation is increased your perception of effort is lower which will increase motor unit recruitment.

Motor unit recruitment is a measure of how many motor neurons are activated in a particular muscle, and therefore is a measure of how many muscle fibers of that muscle are activated. The higher the recruitment the stronger the muscle contraction will be.
