# How does hand grip affect shoulder pressing?

Does hand grip affect shoulder pressing? what grip is better for muscle growth?

we know that the front delt performs shoulder adduction and shoulder flexion. looking at a shoulder press we can see that this will be occurring so does grip placement matter? the difference mainly comes from what function youre training and what other muscles are involved.

flexion: adduction:

when holding the wider grip your arms will be by your side performing shoulder abduction. in shoulder abduction your side delt will be contributing with the front delt having best leverage above 100 degrees

The internal moment arm lengths of the shoulder abductors change relative to one another with increasing shoulder elevation angle

when holding the neutral closer grip your arms will be in front of you performing shoulder flexion. in shoulder flexion your upper chest will be contributing with the front delt having best leverage above 120 degrees.

The internal moment arm lengths of the shoulder flexors change relative to one another with increasing shoulder elevation angle

if wanting to press for your front delts id recommend just pressing in the frontal plane (horizontal grips to your side) above 100 degrees. or if you want do an incline pressing "compound" to train your upper pec and the front delt at the same time then you can use the closer grips.
