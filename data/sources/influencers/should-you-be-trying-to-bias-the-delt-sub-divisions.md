commonly when referring to the deltoid we refer to it with the three main divisions being the front, side, and rear delt. they are responsible for shoulder flexion, extension, and vertical/horizontal abduction. but if we can further divide these three divisions into 7 subdivisions can we be able to bias them?

well simply explained you can "bias" these different subregions with the use of internal/external rotation and using different arm paths. here are some examples of this by coach aaron (go check out his videos if you want to see how to setup/perform them)

but is it worth it to train all of them? personally i would just program a top half shoulder press and a lateral raise variation. depending on frequency and volume if you end up on the higher side doing two sets of each and still want to add more then id implement them. if not id personally just divide your two to three sets between the shoulder press and lateral raise variation. regardless of whether or not you choose to bias regions the deltoid as a whole will be trained well with these
