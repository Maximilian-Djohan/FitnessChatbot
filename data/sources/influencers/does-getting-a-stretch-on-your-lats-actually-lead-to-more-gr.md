# does getting a stretch on your lats actually lead to more growth?

Does getting a "stretch" on your lats in a row actually lead to more muscle growth?

some people say that you want to get a "stretch" on your lats during the eccentric. but is this something that you want to do and can it beneficial?

there are two reasons why it isnt good. first off there is no benefit of even trying to stretch your lats that far as during shoulder extension your lats have better leverage towards the end of the movement. when you stretch this far out it puts emphasis on the start of the movement where muscles like your chest/triceps have better leverage for shoulder extension.

Free weight (EZ bar) pull over exercise involves greater pectoralis major and triceps brachii (long head) muscle activation, compared to latissimus dorsi

also when these people are trying to get a "stretch" on their lats they arent even stretching them. all they are doing is flexing at the hip. so that means on the concentric they performing hip extension which means they are doing a hinge for the adductors/hamstrings and erectors into their lat row.

so no there wont be a benefit of getting a "stretch" on the eccentric. and if you wanted to train your chest/triceps in shoulder extension you should do it in another exercise and if you wanted to train hip extension you should also do that in another exercise.
