# How to setup your incline smith press

How to setup your Incline Smith Press (bench, armpath etc.)

to setup your incline smith there are a few things you want to make sure you have setup correctly the bench and your armpathing. this will have to do with your bench setup relative to the smith and your armpath will be a combination of this alongside your grip width.

first we need to setup the bench. to start off we want to make sure the bench is aligned in the center of the smith. to do this drop the bar on the bench and look at the bar. it will have a smooth part (middle) and textured parts on either side. you want to position the bench so its centered with the smooth part. then raise the bench to a ~30 degree incline.

next is to setup your arm path and execution. since we have fixed the horizontal aspect of the bench now we have to fix the vertical (forward/backward). lay on the bench and then let the bar fall onto your chest. ideally you want the bar to be around your mid to lower pec. moving the bench backwards will make it go lower down to get to here.

next is your grip width/execution. you want to have relatively tucked elbow with around a 30-45 degree flare. during this you also want to make sure your arms and wrist are completely stacked. another tip is to not start with stacked wrists if unracking to start as the unracking will change your wrist angle.

so putting it all together you want to align the bench, set the bar relative to your body, have a good grip width, and have stacked joints. putting it all together you will have a "perfect" incline smith press. and another tip is to avoid arching your back as you execute the movement.
