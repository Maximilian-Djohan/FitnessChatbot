# How to find your maintenance calories

how to find your maintenance calories

Maintenance calories refer to the number of calories an individual needs to consume daily to maintain their current weight.

in order to put on weight or lose weight you have to be in either a calorie surplus or calorie deficit. to do any of this you will have to know your maintenance calories so you can add or subtract on this. so how should you do this?

Calorie Deficit vs. Calorie Surplus
Definition:
- Calorie deficit: When you consume fewer calories than you burn.
- Calorie surplus: When you consume more calories than you burn.

first you will need to find a general estimate. to do this measure your height, bodyweight and get your age. then go to a tdee calculator like www.calculator.net and enter in your information. This will give you a general estimate on what your maintenance calories should be around. but what should you do to refine this estimate?

eat at these calories and weigh yourself everyday in the morning before you eat and track how your weight fluctuates (its normal for it to fluctuate around ~1lb from day to day). see if it has a average trend upwards or downwards and adjust it till its around the same throughout the week.
