Does the longhead of the tricep get worked in a dip?

We know that the triceps do elbow extension. but the long head is biarticular meaning it crosses the shoulder joint and elbow joint. during the dip as you press shoulder flexion is occurring alongside elbow extension which results in antagonistic inhibition. but what does this mean?

Antagonistic inhibition, in the context of muscle physiology, refers to the automatic relaxation or lengthening of one muscle (the antagonist) when the other muscle in a pair (the agonist) contracts.
