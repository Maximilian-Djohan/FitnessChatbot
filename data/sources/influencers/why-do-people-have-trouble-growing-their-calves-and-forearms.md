# Why do people have trouble growing their calves and forearms?

Why do people have trouble growing their forearms and calves? Should you even train them?

some people genetically have a weak point in their forearms or calves and they train it to fix that. but many people say that they don't grow and even give up on training them. is this true and why does this happen?

forearms and calves typically are made up of a higher proportion of type 1 slow twitch muscle fibers. type 1 fibers are smaller, produce less force and have a low growth potential.

Slow-twitch muscle fibers, also known as Type I fibers, are muscle cells that are specialized for endurance activities and sustained contractions.

also forearms and calves visually are heavily impacted by your genetic insertions which can make them look smaller in general. (e.g. high vs low inserting calves)

so does this mean you shouldn't train them? no. like any other muscle you should train them if you want to grow. use the same principles as you do with other muscles - heavy loads, lower rep range, 1-2 sets, higher frequencies.
