# Common form mistakes during your tricep pushdown

Common form mistakes during your tricep pushdown/extension that can decrease muscle growth

we know that in our tricep pushdown/extension variation our goal is to train the tricep through its primary function of elbow extension. but what are things we should look out for during our set to make sure we are getting the most out of it?

Extension

the first problem is moving the upper arm. you want your upper arm to be as stationary as possible during your pushdown. the weight will be pulling your arm into shoulder flexion and if you have simultaneous shoulder flexion and elbow extension it will cause antagonistic inhibition in the triceps longhead causing it

next is for if you perform them with a cuff. ideally you will want the cuff to be as close to the wrist as possible while avoiding any sliding up the forearm during the rep. this is as the higher up the cuff is the more you are decreasing the moment arm length which you will need to use a heavier load and in turn cause more instability at the scapula.

so when performing your pushdown just make sure to have a stationary upper arm and when using a cuff make sure its as close to your wrist as possible.
