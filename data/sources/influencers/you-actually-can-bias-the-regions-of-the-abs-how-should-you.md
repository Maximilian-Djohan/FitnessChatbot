# You actually CAN bias the regions of the abs? How should you be training the abs

You actually CAN bias the regions of the abs? How should you be training the abs?

It's commonly stated that you only need to do one crunch variation for the abs as they only perform spinal flexion. Because of this you should perform a stable crunch over doing some unstable exercise trying to "bias" the abs. We can even see this in data as different ab exercises don't show any statistical significance in activation between different exercises. So does this mean you can't bias the regions of the abs?

Quantification of the differences in electromyographic activity magnitude between the upper and lower portions of the rectus abdominis muscle during selected trunk exercises

GJ Lehman, SM McGill

PMID: 11319934

Well this is true, all you need to do is perform spinal flexion. But rather than focusing on that we should look at how its origin is at the pelvis and inserts around the ribs. This means when with anterior or posterior pelvic tilt the length of the abs can change. So what does this mean?

Posterior Pelvic Tilt Posture, Neutral Spine Posture, Anterior Pelvic Tilt Posture

Well, with a posterior tilt the abs will be more shortened and with an anterior tilt the abs will be more lengthened. And there is some data that supports this idea with a posterior tilt showing greater activation in the lower region and anterior tilt in the upper region of the abs. So should you try to bias the abs?

Diagnostic Ultrasound Shows Preferential Activation of Rectus Abdominis Segments with Exercises Targeting Upper Versus Lower Segments

AARON P GOMIRATO, SYLVAIN G GRENIER

PMCID: PMC10824285 PMID: 38288259

Well, there is still data that contradicts this showing that there is no statistical difference, but personally I believe this is due to the fact that these exercises aren't directly testing the influence of the pelvis tilt or just have the usage of bad unstable exercises.

Figure 1: Initial (upper) and final (lower) positions of analyzed exercises. From left to right: curl up (C), reverse (R), reverse with pelvic tilt (P)

Well, first, is it even worth it to directly train the abs? In my opinion for a majority of people it's not important as it has a relatively small physiological cross sectional area similar/smaller compared to the upper arm muscles, meaning it won't be hard to maximally activate them to the point where trying to bias them won't show significance. As well as the fact that the abs are shown to be adequately trained in other compounds such as hinges.

Trunk muscle activation during dynamic weight-training exercises and isometric instability activities

Nicolle Hamlyn, David G Behm, Warren B Young

PMID: 18076231 DOI: 10.1519/R-20366.13
