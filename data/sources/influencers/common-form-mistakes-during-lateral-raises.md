# Common form mistakes during lateral raises

Common form mistakes during lateral raises which can decrease muscle growth.

performing lateral raises are very simple as all you are doing is performing the side delts function of shoulder abduction. but its common for people to lose form either due to ego lifting, mobility or just because of an unstable variation. but how can you spot this?

starting with the first most common mistake is movement in the torso via thoracic extension/expansion. many people either from lacking shoulder mobility in higher degrees of shoulder abduction or due to ego lifting. this is commonly seen arching your back and pushing your chest up thinking you are raising the weight but rather you are just moving your torso. instead you should maintain neutral/correct posture throughout.

Anterior pelvic tilt
Rib flare

next is due to an unstable variation or ego lifting. this is is as people go into lateral flexion (sideways crunch) to raise the arm as they perform the lateral raise. to avoid this you should make sure you are standing straight throughout your reps.

so how are these bad? with bad torso posture instead of actually challenging shoulder abduction you are instead just contorting the torso to raise the weight instead of actually training the joint action. so during your reps you should focus on maintaining your posture of your body/ribcage eliminating any body movement besides raising the arm.
