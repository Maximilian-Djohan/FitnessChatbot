# Science Based Tricep Training

Science Based Tricep
Training Explained

starting off with the first factor is antagonistic inhibition. since the longhead has the function of shoulder extension and elbow extension if you are performing an exercise where you are lengthening it at one end and shortening it at the other end it won't get worked effectively as seen in different tricep pressing variations through dips or JM Press but these will be great for the medial/lateral head.

the tricep is made up of three heads being the longhead, lateral head and medial head. they all perform elbow extension but as the longhead is biarticular crossing the shoulder it also is involved in shoulder extension.

the next factor is how differing shoulder angles will affect the triceps many people make the claim that the longhead is trained worse overhead as you go higher into higher degrees of shoulder elevation. this would mean that the longhead is best trained when the arm is by your side in a pushdown. but there are problems with the measurements/accuracy with the testing so we can't make a definite claim.

The different role of each head of the triceps brachii muscle in elbow extension

we also have data that the overhead extension grew the longhead better. but in the study since the longhead is more lengthened into shoulder extension this lengthened training in untrained lifters will result in muscle swelling skewing the measurements as growth. so what should you do?

Triceps brachii hypertrophy is substantially greater after elbow extension training performed in the overhead versus neutral arm position

since all of the triceps heads perform elbow extension no matter what you do as long as you are extending the elbow with a stationary shoulder you will be training all three heads effectively. so you can perform your triceps exercises with a "full rom" without an emphasis on the "stretch" and you will work all heads. but there are other factors that can change this.

another factor is seen with wrist position. the longhead is also responsible for adducting the humerus (upper arm bone to torso) as it originates on the scapula. so when you are supinated (only recommended if cuffed) your arm is put into a more naturally adducted shoulder position which can increase activation (proximal to distal joint coupling). but overall you should pick what is comfortable and try to maintain your posture during your exercise.

A Comparison of EMG Activity for Long and Lateral Heads of Triceps Brachii Muscles According to Exercise and Forearm Positions During Triceps Strengthening Exercises

so overall you should perform a pushdown variation to train all three heads of the tricep. then if you want another exercise you can do an overhead extension or pressing pattern like a JM press.
