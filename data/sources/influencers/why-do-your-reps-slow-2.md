# Why do your reps slow?

Why do your reps slow towards the end of your set? What does it have to do with muscle growth?

We know that when training to failure your reps will involuntarily slow and this is important as those last 5 reps will be the most stimulating reps. but what causes this involuntary slowing of contraction speed?

(involuntary slowing = rep on the concentric is slower even though you are maintaining max effort every rep)

Strength training volume can probably be best measured by recording the number of reps done with full motor unit recruitment and at slow bar speeds.

when you hit failure this happens due to you hitting your maximum tolerable perception of effort (your brain says you can't go anymore). this is also important as this shows the importance of resting between sets. as when you aren't recovered enough due to inadequate rest time it increases your perception of effort for the next set causing you to fail quicker from less muscle activation (less growth as less fibers are activated).

Supraspinal CNS fatigue, a component of central fatigue, refers to a decrease in motor output from the brain's motor cortex, leading to reduced muscle force production.
