# How can we determine how fast a carb will digest?

How can we determine how fast or slow a carb will digest?

we know that we need to consume carbs throughout the day and have carbs before you lift but how can you determine if the carbs you eat digest fast or slow?

You can determine if carbs digest fast or slow by checking their type

a simple explanation is that the digestion of a carb is determined by the glycemic index of the carb. it is a measurement of how fast a carb is digested and absorbed to raise blood glucose (blood sugar). A higher GI means it will digest faster and vice versa. but what determines this?

The glycemic index (GI) is a measure of how quickly a carbohydrate-containing food raises blood sugar levels after consumption. It is a scale from 0 to 100, where:

* 0-55: Low glycemic index (GI)
* 56-69: Medium GI
* 70-100: High GI

the glycemic index is determined by the type of carb. this can be looking at simple sugars vs complex carbs, content of fiber in a food, form of carb e.g white vs whole wheat and how the food is prepared. also protein/fats will also slow down the digestion of carbs.

so how can you utilize this information? low GI carbs can be more satiating and used to keep full while you could utilize a higher GI carb when you need something to digest faster. e.g a fast digesting carb meal would consist of something with a high GI carb, lower in fats/protein/fiber (as they slow down digestion).
