# How does grip affect chest pressing

How does grip placement affect chest pressing? Is one better for chest growth?

we know that the chest performs horizontal adduction while the upper chest performs shoulder flexion (alongside adduction). looking at a chest press we can see horizontal adduction will be happening so does the grip placement matter?

adduction: flexion:

usually when doing the press with a horizontal grip it will make your arms more flared putting you in a good position to primarily do horizontal adduction for your mid/lower chest.

then when we compare it to doing a press with a vertical grip your arms are more tucked. this allows you to get more shoulder flexion during the pec and depending on the degree of shoulder flexion (more = better) it will be a good upper pec exercise.

so grip determines arm path. a more tucked arm path is good for the upper pec. while a more flared arm path is better for your mid/lower pec.
