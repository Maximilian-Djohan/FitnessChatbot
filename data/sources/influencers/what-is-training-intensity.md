# what is training intensity?

What is training intensity? How much does training intensity matter for muscle growth?

Training intensity is how hard you are pushing yourself during your exercises. This can be measured by how close you are to failure, but how much does training intensity actually matter?

Training intensity during resistance is the level of effort relative to your maximum capacity.

Training intensity is the most important thing when it comes to your training over exercise selection and workout split. This is because training intensity will reflect how much effort you are putting in. In order to train as many muscle fibers as possible you want to have the highest degree of effort you can and take it to a close proximity to failure.

Voluntarily increasing central motor command increases perception of effort, and the number of recruited motor units (and the number of activated muscle fibers).

So what should your training look like? You should be training to at the very least 0-2 reps in reserve (full reps before you lose form). And to make sure you have a high degree of effort you should be moving the weight as fast as you can on the concentric.

Eccentric resistance while lowering, concentric pushing while raising.
