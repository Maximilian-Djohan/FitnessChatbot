Yes a "body recomp" is essentially just describing a small deficit while gaining muscle. calories arent a stimulus for muscle growth. you will be able gain muscle while in a deficit albeit slightly slower then if you were maintaining.

Body recomposition refers to the process of simultaneously losing body fat and gaining muscle mass. It's a fitness goal that focuses on improving body composition, the ratio of fat to lean muscle, rather than solely on weight loss.

Some people say its only possible in beginners this is not true. the mechanisms for losing fat and gaining muscle dont change based on experience. a beginner will have an easier time "recomping" but thats just because its easier for a beginner to develop muscle.

keep in mind that to help aid in this process your other factors should be in check to help progression. sleep, diet and training are important.
