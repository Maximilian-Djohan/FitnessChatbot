# explaining my reasoning behind my side delt exercise tierlist

explaining the reasoning behind my side delt exercise tier list

starting off to preface just because something is lower on the list does not mean its not effective for muscle growth. rather based on different criteria there will be better options if your goal is to "maximize" growth for said muscle.

Starting off with F tier these exercises are very unstable/impractical and there is no reason to implement them over any of the exercises on the tierlist even if they do train shoulder abduction which is the side delts function and will train the side delt.

next in C tier i have more "viable" options. the arnold press will be better as you are stable but there is still a lot of unnecessary movement. the standing shoulder press will be better in terms of the actual movement being performed but its still unstable and harder to progress.

next in B tier is the dumbbell shoulder press which will be a pretty effective option if taken into a deeper rom. but it still lacks some stability and will primarily be a front delt exercise. the upright row also will train the side delts function but there is only detriment to bending the elbows compared to a lateral raise.

in A tier I have the machine shoulder press. it will be very effective for the side delt if performed in a deep rom. but it will still primarily be a front delt exercise and will be worse compared to any lateral raise variation.

in S tier I have the dumbbell lateral raise. many people discredit the exercise in exchange for more "science" based exercises but it is very effective at training the side delts function of shoulder abduction. the only thing holding it back is some stability and the harder progression with dumbbell increments.

in S+ i have the cable lateral raise and machine lateral raise. these will be very easy to progress and will be relatively more stable than other choices. the machine also has the benefit of having a fixed arm path which makes it the "best"

so yes most of these exercises can be effective for growing your side delt but due to different criteria i ranked some exercises higher than others.
