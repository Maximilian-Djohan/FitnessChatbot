# Does time under tension actually matter?

Does time under tension actually matter?

many older people like to say that time under tension causes muscle growth. this causes people to rep out something lighter then they could normally do as they can spend more time lifting it. but is this actually true and does it matter?

Time Under Tension (TUT) refers to the duration a muscle is actively engaged and under load during a set of exercise.

this is actually a misconception as time under tension doesnt matter for muscle growth. if you are focusing on just lifting under a time frame you are missing out on the requirements to actually build muscle. but then what actually builds muscle?
(TIME IS NOT STIMULUS)

To build muscle you need a high degree of effort and mechanical tension to grow muscle. you can do this by training to failure or close to failure with something challenging to actually recruit the motor units required to build muscle. comparing this to Gut you can be lifting something for a long time but not recruit enough motor units as there isnt the degree of effort to call for it.

This means your body starts by using smaller, low-force motor units for light tasks and progressively recruits larger, high-force units as effort increases.
