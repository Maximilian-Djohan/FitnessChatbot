# What is insulin and insulin sensitivity?

What is Insulin and Insulin Sensitivity? Is it the secret to maximizing muscle growth?

Insulin is the hormone in your body that regulates your blood glucose (sugar) and moves glucose into cells from the bloodstream used for energy. Insulin sensitivity is how "effectively" your body's cells respond to insulin through this process. But what does this have to do with muscle growth?

Well there is the idea that by increasing your insulin sensitivity you will be able to move glucose to the muscle more effectively to store it as glycogen/help with nutrition partitioning. If this is the case shouldnt we be trying to maximize it as much as possible?

Nutrient partitioning is the metabolic process determining how ingested calories are distributed between muscle growth, energy expenditure, or fat storage. Optimizing this process aims to drive nutrients into muscle tissue (glycogen replenishment) while minimizing fat storage.

While this idea isnt "wrong" but its importance is overplayed as people try to use this "secret" knowledge for monetary value via coaching or views. This is because we know in scenarios where people are more insulin resistant there is no significant difference in the outcome of muscle growth. So what should you do?

Well the main contributing factors will be your body composition where you want to be at a healthy bodyfat percentage from 10-15% and having a high physical activity. Also things like better sleep, timing meals post workout, and walking after meals will also help.

Visceral fat (fat surrounding abdominal organs) actively drives insulin resistance by releasing inflammatory cytokines and free fatty acids, directly impairing insulin function.

So what does this mean? Trying to maximize insulin sensitivity isnt important for muscle growth to an extent. This is because you should already be in a good state for this as you should already be aiming to have a good body composition and high activity.
