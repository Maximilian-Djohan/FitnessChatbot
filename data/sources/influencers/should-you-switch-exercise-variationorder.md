# should you switch exercise variation/order?

Should you switch exercise
variation/order for different training
days or keep them the same?

Fullbody A Fullbody B

some people ask if they should keep exercise
selection the same or change it by having

different training days (e.g. FB a FB b FB c). is it a
problem if you are training all the same muscle
groups? while yes you will progress while doing
something like that i am against it as switching
exercises there will be some muscle fibers that
wont get worked because of variation and wont
benefit from a higher frequency even if the overall

muscle group gets trained.

now for exercise order it doesnt have the
problem of missing muscle fibers but instead
changing order can make it hard to track your
progressive overload. if you were to curl at
the start of your session one day and at the
end of your session another day you will get
less reps on that second day. its good to
keep order the same to make sure you are
progressing everything incase you need to
make any changes.

(occasionally it is fine if someone is hogging a machine and you
would have to wait an excessive amount of time)

overall its best to keep variation to a
minimum to have better progression
on your certain exercises and be able
to easily track it. but if you enjoy
variation it is comepletly fine and you
wont impact you much.
