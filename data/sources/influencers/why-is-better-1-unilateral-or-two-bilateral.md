# Why is better 1 Unilateral or two bilateral?

What is better 1 Unilateral Set each side or 2 Bilateral Sets?

some people ask what is better to do for muscle growth one unilateral set for each side or two bilateral sets. well first what is the difference between these? a unilateral set where you are only working one limb will have a higher degree of motor unit recruitment as you don't have to split central motor command to each side rather focusing on one and in turn recruiting more muscle fibers in that side. so what does this mean?

Motor Unit Recruitment

Recruited MUs Non-recruited MUs

in a unilateral set you will have more muscle fibers active in each side. but if in comparison to doing a multiple bilateral sets you will have less total muscle fibers active but in return since you have more volume you will be giving more stimulus to the muscle fibers that are active and in turn making them grow more. so which is better?

| Single Unilateral | Multiple Bilateral |
|---|---|
| Less Stimulus, More Active Fibers | More Stimulus, Less Active Fibers |

well if you're only performing one set you can perform it unilaterally to get more out of it as long as you don't suffer from the bilateral force deficit. but should you do it over multiple bilateral sets? well that shouldn't be the breaking point. you can just do multiple unilateral sets and if you don't want to do that due to time then a question of time not a question of whats better.

Bilateral force deficit (BFD) is a phenomenon where the sum of force produced by limbs contracting individually (unilaterally) exceeds the maximum force produced by both limbs contracting simultaneously (bilaterally).

so we can use unilateral and bilateral exercises in our training. I would spare unilateral choices for exercise where its convenient/a muscle is a priority. there will be a greater degree of CNS fatigue by performing more unilateral sets as your brain is working additional for each side so to avoid that you don't need to do everything unilateral. so one isn't inherently better it depends on your total programming and priorities.
