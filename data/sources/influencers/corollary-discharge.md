# Corollary Discharge

Corollary Discharge Explained

How does it relate to resistance training?

a corollary discharge/efference copy is the process of a set of action potentials where they travel along nerve pathways to certain parts of the brain carrying a copy of a motor command with the purpose of predicting and coordinating movements/actions. but what does this have to do with resistance training?

Auditory re-afference

now how does this relate to resistance training? this corollary discharge signal reaches the brain to predict and coordinate for your training where it will sense how much effort is needed/given. when a small signal is sent there is a small perception of effort and vice versa. so what does this effect?

Perception of effort is the subjective feeling of how hard a task is, and it is directly influenced by a neural signal called corollary discharge.

your perception of effort will correspond with how many motor units you are recruiting. we know that we want to be training with maximum effort to recruit as many motor units. but as you train with a high effort your perception of effort will rise until you reach your maximum tolerable perception of effort due to this corollary discharge signaling. this is where you would reach failure in your exercise.

The "maximum tolerable perception of effort" is the point of fatigue where a person stops an activity because they cannot psychologically or physiologically tolerate the feeling of exertion any longer.

so as summary the corollary discharge is a signal that goes to the sensory parts of the brain. this signal corresponds to your effort levels and will determine when you reach failure.
