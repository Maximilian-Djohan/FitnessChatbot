# What is the TNF/Braedon Squat?

What is the TNF/Braedon Squat? Is it an effective squat pattern or even better than normal squat patterns?

There are two ways to perform this version of the squat pattern, one where you have a fixed hip and one with a non fixed hip. They both have the same idea of disadvantaging hip extension, but with the fixed hip you ideally won't have hip extension at all, while non fixed one the hip angle changes but it's disadvantaged. But they both have the purpose to limit glute and adductor involvement.

In the context of exercise, "fixed hip" refers to a hip joint that has no movement. A "non-fixed hip," conversely, has movement at the hip.

Also with the weight loaded on your femurs instead of your back or coming from in front of you through your legs, will also mechanically disadvantage hip extension, which decreases glute and adductor contribution. So is this a good squat pattern?
