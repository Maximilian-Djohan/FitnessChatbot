# What is RIR?

What is repetitions in reserve (RIR)? Should you utilize it in your training?

RIR can refer to "Reps in Reserve," a concept used in strength training to gauge the intensity of a set.

Repetitions in reserve is how many full reps you can do prior to failure. 0 RIR is where you can't complete another full rep while something like 2 RIR means that you stopped 2 reps away from failure (0 RIR). But should you use rir in your training?

Using RIR is not some way to "take the easy way" its good for managing fatigue in session or from session to session. So utilizing RIR can help prevent fatigue as within that 0-2 rir the fatigue of each rep is exponential while the growth stimulus is very similar/non significant.

Training to failure and stopping short of failure cause similar hypertrophy?

so should you use rir? i would recommend training to 0 rir (stop when you can't complete a full rep) and if you are having trouble progressing from session to session you can try implementing rir to help with it.
