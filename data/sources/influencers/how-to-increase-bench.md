# how to increase bench

How to increase your bench based on science based lifting

the first and most obvious thing you want to do is include benching in your sessions over something like a pec dec. your goal to increase your bench is to develop neural/coordination adaptations to improve your skill of benching as well as strength over "optimal" muscle growth.

Neural adaptations are improvements in the nervous system's efficiency at controlling muscles, leading to strength gains, better coordination, and improved motor control

so how would you go about doing this? bench with as high of a frequency as you can with as much volume as you can. this would look like benching every other day (FBEOD or UL6x). for volume you want to do as many sets as you can ~3 sets in a lower rep range 3-5 reps with 1-2 rir to allow you to do more of these sets.

Non-linear dose-response relationships of hypertrophy to workout volume vary substantially between meta-analyses, perhaps due to inclusion criteria.

so basically it will be very similar to a powerlifters approach to training being train it as much as you can as frequently as you can. and if you question how much "hypertrophy" you will be missing it wont be much of a problem as you will be getting a good amount of stimulating reps during it.
