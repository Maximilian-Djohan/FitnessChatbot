# so smaller muscles need more volume?

Do smaller muscles need more volume/reps? should your training change depending on the muscle?

some people like jeff nippard have promoted doing higher reps for muscle groups such as your rear delts or calves. they promote this idea saying itll help maintain form to keep bigger muscles out. and just that they are better trained with a lower weight. but is this actually true?

starting with his example of a rear delt fly he says do use up to something like 20-30 reps to make sure you arent using your upper back. well what does the upper back do? it retracts the scapula. in a rear delt fly when you are moving your arms back through horizontal extension no where in this range will you be retracting your scapula. your rep range wont dictate your form. and this concept can be applied to any muscle group.

but do muscles actually benefit from higher reps? no. rep range wont change anything dependent on the muscle. whether you are training at 5 reps or 30. if you are training to failure where you have a high degree of effort and mechanical tension those last 5 reps to failure will be the most stimulating.

so what should you do? train in a rep range you are comfortable in. your form isnt dictated by the weight that is being used as long as you dont ego lift. and lower reps will be better to avoid any unnecessary fatigue of going higher but if you want to do high reps do what you want but its not better.
