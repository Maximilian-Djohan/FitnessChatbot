# How to fix your postural imbalance

How to fix your postural imbalance - Scoliosis, Anterior Pelvic Tilt, Flared Ribcage, Winged Scapula etc.

first what actually is a posture imbalance? your body is a connected system with the pelvis, spine, ribcage, and shoulders. then these postural/musculoskeletal differences can make you stray away from the "ideal" straight up posture which people can classify into things like anterior tilt etc.

Good posture, Anterior pelvic tilt, Posterior pelvic tilt, Lumbar lordosis, Thoracic kyphosis, Forward head

well first is the misconception that these are things you need to fix. first the body isnt always ideally symmetrical and its normal to stray away from a "perfect" stance. it can just be how you are or due the physical conditions you put your body through. but these things arent inherently harmful. if they arent causing you pain or limit your range of motion then you shouldnt worry about them.

Anterior pelvic tilt (APT) in athletes is a common, often advantageous posture characterized by the pelvis tipping forward, creating increased lower back curve (lordosis). While often deemed "bad" posture, in elite athletes, it facilitates explosive performance by lengthening hamstrings for greater force production and increasing rearward leg travel for speed, famously seen in runners like Usain Bolt.

but what if it is actually harming you? the first thing you should do is get it diagnosed by a medical professional. dont try to self diagnose or ask me or any other fitness influencer what to do. the cause can be from a multitude of reasons from your daily posture, muscle imbalances, anatomy, injuries, environment, your growth/development and many other factors that we cant help you with.

identify imbalances, create strengthening/stretching exercises, and may offer techniques like manual therapy, ergonomic adjustments, and in severe cases, pain management (e.g., injections).

so if you have what you think is a postural imbalance if it doesnt cause pain or limit you then dont stress. if it does then consult a specialist that can actually determine if you have one and how to fix it. but the most basic non medical advice that i can give you that can possibly check off some boxes is to train all major muscle groups especially the ones that can impact posture i.e glutes, shoulders, back, erectors, hip extensors etc.
