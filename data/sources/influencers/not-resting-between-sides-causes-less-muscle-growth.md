# Not resting between sides causes less muscle growth

Not resting between sides causes less muscle growth?

some people ask whether they should rest between their left and right sides during an exercise. some people go right into the opposite side without resting while some people wait longer. but what is the best for muscle growth? does it matter since the muscle on the other side of your body isnt "tired"?

A "unilateral set" is a fitness term for a set of exercises that uses only one limb (arm or leg) at a time to perform a movement.

you should rest between sides. but why is this? while your other side isnt physically affected there will be CNS fatigue throughout your body from the set. this CNS fatigue will affect your entire body and decrease performance unless you have rest. but how long should you rest in between sides?

SUMMARY

rest time will differ on muscle group/exercise and vary from person to person based on their cardiovascular endurance. but something like a tricep pushdown can take something like 1 minute between sides as it wont be very fatiguing compared to something like a leg extension which might take you 3 minutes. but you should base it on when YOU feel recovered and if you want an objective way to track it you can base it off your heart rate.
