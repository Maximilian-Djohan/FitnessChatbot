# Should you be off above or below the elbow?

Should you cuff above or below the elbow on your lateral raises? Which is better for muscle growth?

we know that by cuffing above the elbow on your lateral raises will help eliminate any movement at the elbow that could decrease motor unit recruitment so does this mean that you should always be cuffing above the elbow?

Motor unit recruitment is a measure of how many motor neurons are activated in a particular muscle, and therefore is a measure of how many muscle fibers of that muscle are activated.

well you also have to consider what else is happening when you cuff above the elbow. in the case of a lateral raise you are significantly decreasing the moment arm length. this will result in you needing around double the weight for the same challenge. while this isnt inherently an issue it the increased weight can cause instability/ego lifting via movement of the spine or through lateral flexion.

so what does this mean? cuffing above the elbow will be great but if you find yourself losing form with torso movement you wont have the benefits of eliminating the joint as youre just adding unnecessary/more harmful movement elsewhere. so in these cases it would be better to do a lateral raise that requires less load and stability demand like cuffing at the wrist.
