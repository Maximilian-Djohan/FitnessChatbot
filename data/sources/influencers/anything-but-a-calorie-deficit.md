# anything but a calorie deficit

The keto diet is NOT effective for weight loss. Should you really be avoiding carbs when trying to lose weight?

some people claim that they lose a lot of weight when cutting out carbs from their diet. they use this as evidence that they are losing weight. in reality this is because they are losing water weight. carbs hold water as glycogen so when you cut out carbs you are just eliminating the water that it retains which is not fat.

300g of carbohydrates can lead to approximately 900g to 1200g (2 to 2.6 pounds) of water weight because each gram of carbohydrate stores 3 to 4 grams of water as glycogen.

so why does this matter? first this water weight will not make you look better as fat is still there. carbs are an important source for energy and are important to incorporate into your diet. when you end up eating carbs again you will gain the water weight back and if you cut out carbs for a prolonged amount of time you will be at risk for ketosis which can increase heart/cardiovascular disease. Ketosis can have both potential benefits and risks for heart health, with some studies suggesting a higher risk of cardiovascular events due to increased LDL cholesterol and other factors.

so if youre looking to lose actual fat you should just be in a calorie deficit for a prolonged amount of time until you reach your goal.

What is a calorie deficit? A calorie deficit occurs when you burn more calories than you consume.
