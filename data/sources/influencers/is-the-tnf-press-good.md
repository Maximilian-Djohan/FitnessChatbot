# Is the TNF Press good?

Is the TNF Supinated Incline Press a good exercise? What about it makes it effective?

this variation of chest pressing has the purpose of biasing the upper pec but is this a good option? well we know that we want to be performing shoulder flexion for the upper chest which this does very well so it will be effective. but is there anything that makes this specific variation special?

shoulder flexion:

well the main factor that makes this different is the supinated grip. this will allow you to be more naturally internally rotated which gives you the perfect arm path for shoulder flexion. also during this press your forearm will be relatively straight with your wrist and elbow being stacked which will help reduce triceps involvement that could limit you.

so if you have a smith machine or just don't have any other good upper chest options this will be a great option for the upper chest
