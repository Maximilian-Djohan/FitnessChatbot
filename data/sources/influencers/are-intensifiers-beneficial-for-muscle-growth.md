# Are intensifiers beneficial for muscle growth?

Are intensifiers like forced reps, dropsets and supersets beneficial for muscle growth?

Drop Set

Definition: After reaching failure with a certain weight, you immediately reduce the weight and continue doing more reps without resting,

Superset

Definition: Two exercises performed back-to-back with no rest in between. They can target the same muscle (for intensity) or opposite muscles (for efficiency)

Forced Reps

Definition: After you reach failure, a spotter helps you complete a few extra reps by slightly assisting you.

we know for muscle growth we need a high degree of effort and mechanical tension. you can achieve this by training to failure/close to failure. we know this would be present when doing these intensifiers so are they beneficial?

Mechanical Tension Defined:
Mechanical tension is the force exerted by a muscle when it contracts under resistance. It's the primary stimulus for muscle hypertrophy (growth).

When looking at dropsets/forced reps you perform them after can no longer complete a full rep on your own (0 RIR). At this point youve already hit your maximum tolerable perceived effort. so when you push through additional reps using intensifiers, youre already fatigued and can't put in the same level of effort per rep. because of this fatigue youre not able to recruit as many muscle fibers as you would if you rested and did another set.

Supraspinal CNS fatigue, a component of central fatigue, refers to a decrease in motor output from the brain's motor cortex, leading to reduced muscle force production.

when looking at supersets the same concept is applied whether you are doing a superset within the same muscle group or an antagonistic superset between two muscle groups (ex. bicep/tricep). after reaching failure on one exercise the fatigue will limit how much effort which lowers motor unit recruitment for the following set.
