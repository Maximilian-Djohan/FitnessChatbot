# is eccentric overloading training good?

Is eccentric overloading training actually good? is it better than normal training for muscle growth?

the eccentric is the part of your rep where you are lowering your weight. during your eccentric you are able to produce more force than your concentric. but you are using the same weight throughout both parts less muscle fibers will be active during the eccentric they arent required to handle the weight. this means less muscle fibers will be trained during the eccentric. so what does this have to do with the eccentric overload training?

When producing the same muscle force, motor unit recruitment is lower in an eccentric contraction than in a concentric contraction (because the force produced by each muscle fiber is higher in the eccentric). When performing a maximal effort, motor unit recruitment is also lower in an eccentric contraction than in a concentric contraction (perhaps because such contractions are very unfamiliar to most individuals).

eccentric overloading training is where you have more resistance during the eccentric to compensate for the more force you have. this will make sure you have maximum effort by utilizing all the force you can produce during the eccentric. this makes it like concentric training where you are able to recruit more muscle fibers as you are able to have high degrees of effort. so can it be useful?
