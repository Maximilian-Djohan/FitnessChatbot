# How much should your progressive overload be?

How much should your progressive overload be?

we know that progressive overload is important for muscle growth and you should be progressing often (vid few posts before this goes over this). but how much should you be progressing be it weight or reps?

How Progressive Overload Works:
- Increased Weight: Gradually increase the weight lifted during exercises.
- Increased Repetitions: Perform more repetitions with the same weight.

some people think they should be progressing weight every session or they are doing something wrong. this is not true. its normal to not have noticeable progression from session to session as strength adaptations will develop with time. this doesnt mean you arent progressing its just that you havent made enough adaptations to make this physical difference in weight or reps.

Strength adaptations are the body's long-term improvements in response to strength training, which include both neural and structural changes.

when you visualize how slow progression is it can help. a SLDL which is a heavier movement a 1lb jump will be a lot lower of a percentage increase compared to 1lb jump on a preacher curl. improving your SLDL by 1lb a week is realistic going from something like 250lbs -> 300lb in a year. but if you were looking at a curl going from a 40lbs to a 90lbs in a year it isnt realistic. not everything will have the same linear progression which is normal. progression over time is slow and normal through reps or weight.

1lb is 0.4% of 250, 1lb is 2.5% of 40
