# Why might you be able to get another rep after failing bilaterally?

Well first we need to know what causes you to fail during your set. Each rep in your set requires a certain amount of effort to complete the rep. But due to the fatigue you accumulate during the set your perception of effort rises. So at the end of your set, as you have reached your maximum tolerable perception of effort, you aren't able to produce enough effort required to finish another rep. So what does this have to do with single vs two limb exercises?

Perception of effort (RPE) in resistance training is your brain's conscious feeling of how hard a lift is, based on body signals (muscle fatigue, heart rate) and mental factors.

Well, simply explained, when using more musculature by using two limbs, each bilateral rep will require more effort to finish. So when failing bilaterally, while you might not be able to produce enough effort to complete another rep, you might be able to get another unilateral rep as it requires less effort to complete. So what does this mean?

So yes, after you fail bilaterally you might be able to get more unilateral reps, but this isn't something you should care to go for as these reps won't be very productive as there will already be a high degree of CNS/Metabolite fatigue which will hinder recruitment.
