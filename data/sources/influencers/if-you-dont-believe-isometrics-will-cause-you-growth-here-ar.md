Isometric exercises are a type of strength training that involves contracting muscles without changing their length or the angle of the joint. Essentially, you're holding a static position

first the most common example is through back exercises. these exercises have a big demand on your grip strength and will in turn grow your forearm muscles that are responsible for finger flexion.

next is your curl variations. you will be training your wrist and finger flexors as you stabilize your wrist and grip the weight.

another common one is your abs. many exercises require core stability which will be putting your abs through an isometric

and most frequently are your erectors as many people do not train their function of spinal extension. rather they train them through a stabilizing isometric during a hinge where they are moving at the hips
