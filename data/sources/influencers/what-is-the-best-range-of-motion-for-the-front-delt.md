if wanting to do a single joint exercise such as a cable front raise (performing function of shoulder flexion) you want to stay within 0-20 degrees as any higher the upper chest will be contributing more to the exercise

why do we train in this range? the front delt in 0-20 degrees has better leverage for shoulder flexion compared to the upper pec in this range

Based on: Ackland, D.C., Pak, P., Richardson, M., & Pandy, M.G. (2008). Moment arms of the muscles crossing the anatomical shoulder. Journal of Anatomy, 233(4), 363-390.

does that mean you should only train in these ranges? yes and no. if you want to maximize your front delt you can stick in these ranges but if you want to increase rom just keep in mind that there are other muscles in the movement that will be the primary muscle trained (ex. front raise will be upper pec exercise above 20 degrees)
