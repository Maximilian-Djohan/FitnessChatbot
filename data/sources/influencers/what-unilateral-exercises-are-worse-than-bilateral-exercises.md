# What unilateral exercises are worse than bilateral exercises?

What are unilateral exercises that are worse than their normal counterpart? Can some unilateral exercises be worse for muscle growth?

we know that unilateral exercises will result in more motor unit recruitment as only one side is receiving central motor command but sometimes they will be worse then their bilateral counterpart. this is because of the bilateral force deficit where the instability from the unilateral exercise will outweigh any benefit. so what exercises would be worse unilaterally?

The bilateral force deficit is a phenomenon where the total force produced when using both limbs at the same time is less than the sum of the forces each limb could produce individually.

the main exercises that suffer from the bilateral force deficit are presses. without the other arm acting as a counter force your body will shift towards the working side. so presses will be better bilateral unless you can anchor yourself (most scenarios regardless you should be doing them bilaterally).

the next most common one would be leg exercises. for hinges its hard to balance yourself unilaterally and usually there isn't a need to do it unilaterally. next would be leg machines where they have the same problem where you wouldn't have the other leg to provide counter force. but if you're able to stay stable and lift more than half unilaterally then you will be fine.

for arm exercises the weight isnt heavy enough where if you have arm support or use your other arm to brace against something/hold something it will be enough to maintain stability for unilateral variations.

for back exercises if you have good machines with chest/leg support using your other arm to brace will be enough to allow yourself to do unilateral variations. if you don't have support then it can be something to worry about.

so overall for heavier exercises where your producing a lot of force relying on your other side for counterforce rather than the stability of the machine/setup it will be better to do it unilaterally.
