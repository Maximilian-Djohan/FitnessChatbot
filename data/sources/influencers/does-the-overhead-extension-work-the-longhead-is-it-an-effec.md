# does the overhead extension work the longhead? is it an effective tricep exercise?

Does the overhead extension work the longhead? Is it an effective tricep exercise?

its often debated whether the overhead extension is a good or bad exercise. specifically people argue whether or not the longhead of the tricep gets worked in it. but why is this?

what are possible reasons for this? there is antagonistic inhibition for biarticular muscles like the longhead which has two functions: shoulder extension and elbow extension where it can experience antagonistic inhibition when it is lengthening at one end and shortening at the other causing the muscle to not get worked effectively. e.g a dip/jm press where you are going through shoulder flexion and elbow extension at the same time. but the overhead extension doesnt have this problem as its stationary so what does this mean?

SBL's make this longhead claim based on this study where they test the triceps at differing degrees of shoulder elevation. the study showed that the longhead is best trained when the arm is by your side and loses leverage as your go into higher degrees of shoulder elevation i.e overhead. but there are problems with the measurements and accuracy of testing so we cant use it as a definite claim.

The different role of each head of the triceps brachii muscle in elbow extension
Rizki Fejar Zulkernair, Yu Cheng Sun, Sungloan Lim, Jae-Myeung Chun
DOI: 10,1016/,20tt 2018.02.05

well in response people reference this study where the longhead is shown to grow better in an overhead extension. SBL's dismiss this study due to the measurements of the study. when your arm is overhead the longhead will be more lengthened and training the longhead like this in untrained lifters in a short period of time will result in muscle swelling in the longhead skewing the measurements as "growth". but this doesnt mean it should completely dismissed due to the one flaw. so what does this mean?

Triceps brachii hypertrophy is substantially greater after elbow extension training performed in the overhead versus neutral arm position
Meng Hi, Hikaru Sakurai, Yuki Kusagawa, Aki Kanehisa
PMID: 35819335 DOI: 10.1080/17461391.2022.2100279

well we dont have conclusive data on how effective the overhead extension will train the longhead compared to a pushdown. but we can say that the overhead extension WILL train the longhead. so my recommendation is to have a pushdown with your arm by your side for the longhead. whether you believe the overhead extension will better train the longhead or will disadvantage it due to the shoulder elevation you can decide and choose that as your secondary movement.
