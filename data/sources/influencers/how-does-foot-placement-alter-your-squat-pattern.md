# How does foot placement alter your squat pattern?

How does foot placement alter your squat pattern? Is there a "best" foot placement for muscle growth?

many people say that foot placement when doing something like a leg press can determine what muscles you are targeting. no matter what you do your muscles responsible for hip extension and knee extension will be active during the movement but foot position can help bias certain muscles.

taking a low foot stance will allow for more knee flexion compared to hip flexion which will result in more of a quad bias (choose width that is comfortable)

(more flexion through the eccentric allows for more extension on the concentric)

taking a high foot stance will allow for more hip flexion compared to knee flexion which will result with more of an adductors/bit of glute bias (choose width that is comfortable though more width can benefit the adductors)

Antagonistic inhibition, in the context of muscle physiology, refers to the automatic relaxation or lengthening of one muscle (the antagonist) when the other muscle in a pair (the agonist) contracts
