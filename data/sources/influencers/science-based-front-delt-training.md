Training Explained

the front delt in shoulder abduction is trained effectively above ~95 degrees of shoulder abduction and in shoulder flexion through hyperextension to ~20 degrees in shoulder flexion as in other ranges other muscles will be contributing more (side delt/chest).

one of the main questions people have is what grip to use on their shoulder press. well if your goal is to grow your front delts you should use the pronated (wide) grip to train shoulder abduction. using the neutral (inner) grips you will train through shoulder flexion where the upper chest will be contributing more.

so for your shoulder abduction exercise you should just do a wider grip top half shoulder press. but what about shoulder flexion?

while you can do a front raise partial to train shoulder flexion directly it won't be very important. rather you should just focus on your top half shoulder press and let your upper pec exercise where you're already going through shoulder flexion count towards training the front delts function of shoulder flexion.

so basically just do a top half shoulder press.

the front delt is one of the main three divisions of the deltoid muscle. its the front/anterior part of the deltoid in front of the rear and side delt. it has the function of raising the arm through shoulder abduction and shoulder flexion.
