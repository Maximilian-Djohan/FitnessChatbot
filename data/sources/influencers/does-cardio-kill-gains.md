# Does cardio kill gains?

cardio does NOT harm muscle growth it actually can INCREASE muscle growth

some people say that cardio kills gains. this is true and false. it could have a negative effect as it will cause muscle damage which could impact your training sessions. so should you avoid cardio?

Examples of cardio exercise include walking, running, cycling, swimming.

i would not recommend higher impact cardio as it will more than likely be detrimental. but low intensity e.g walking/cycling will be easily recoverable. you can test this by incorporating cardio ideally post workout or whenever you have time and see if your progression in training changes. if youre progressing like normal it means youre recovering from this added damage/fatigue.

but can this cardio actually improve muscle growth? it can as it will improve your cardiovascular endurance. this is important as you perform a set you reach failure when you reach your maximum tolerable perception of effort where your brain reaches its limit. reaching this limit includes cardiovascular sensations during your set. so by increasing your endurance you can tolerate more/are less effected by the sensations during the set.

The relationship between cardiovascular sensations and central nervous system (CNS) fatigue is complex and can manifest in symptoms like a racing or fluttering heart, shortness of breath, or a feeling of weakness and dizziness.

so what does this mean? cardio can be helpful to increase your tdee, increase performance during your set and have shorter rest times as your heart rate will recover to baseline quicker. so as long as youre recovering from your cardio it will be beneficial.
