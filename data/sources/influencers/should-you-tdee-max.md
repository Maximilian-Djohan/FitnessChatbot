# Should you tdee max?

Should you be TDEE Maxxing? How does this differ between different dieting phases?

well first what is the purpose of increasing your TDEE? your TDEE is the amount of energy you expend which will in turn be how many calories you will need to maintain this expenditure of energy. many different factors contribute to your tdee but the main factor that you can manipulate is your intentional physical activity through cardio. but what happens when we increase that?

Total Daily Energy Expenditure (TDEE) is the total number of calories your body burns in a 24-hour period, accounting for all bodily functions, daily movement, and exercise.

well by increasing activity through cardio it can help increase your cardiovascular fitness which can have some positive carryover to your lifting as well as the increased expenditure raising your maintenance calories. but how does this affect you?

Maintenance calories are the total amount of energy (calories) your body requires daily to maintain its current weight, covering all bodily functions, digestion, and physical activity.

well during your cut raising activity can help maintain the same deficit amount without having to cut as many calories from your eating. this can help maintain some energy and allow you to better hit your macros/micros during your deficit allowing you to eat more.
