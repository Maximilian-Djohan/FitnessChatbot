# Isolations vs Compounds

Why do people do isolation exercises when compound exercises allow you to train more muscle groups?

There are compound exercises like bench for example in which while you are primarily training your chest but you are also training your triceps and shoulders alongside it. Would it not be better for muscle growth to work multiple muscles to train them with more overall volume?

When doing an exercise you train a muscle by performing its function. But when you perform an exercise which has multiple functions being trained like a bench press your central motor command will be divided which will divide motor unit recruitment between the muscles. This will result in less muscle fibers worked in the specific muscle you are trying to train.

Central motor command, originating in the brain, dictates motor unit recruitment, which is the activation of different numbers of muscle fibers to produce varying levels of force.

The sensation of effort is a cognitive feeling of work associated with voluntary actions, and is likely caused by a corollary discharge from the central motor command.

So what should you do? Doing compounds and isolations both have their use. Isolations can be used to further prioritize something while compounds will be more time/volume efficient while still providing great growth.
