# Analyzing Jeff Nippards Shoulder/Arm Day

Analyzing Jeff Nippard's Arm and Shoulder Day to see if it's optimal.

Jeff Nippard recently released his full shoulder and arm day, he does to prioritize his arms. Starting off this can be seen as counterintuitive when bringing up a weak point. While you may think it's beneficial to separate it from other muscle groups by giving it a day to prioritize, adding another day to your split will in turn lessen the frequency you are training them which is counterintuitive. Instead you should just do it at the start of your sessions over the other muscle groups as frequency is important. But besides this is his workout good?

For his biceps/arms he does 3 exercises. His bayesian curl will be descending and hardest at the bottom which is good as the biceps have best leverage there. He then does a hammer preacher curl for his brachioradialis but this is counterintuitive as the brachioradialis has best leverage towards the top but because of the preacher setup it will be hardest at the bottom making your biceps/brachialis contribute a lot more. And lastly he does a db curl which is fine as an overall elbow flexor curl which isn't the most "optimal" which he acknowledges and it's fine to do.

For triceps he does an overhead extension and machine dips. These are both great triceps exercises but they will both be primarily medial/lateral head exercises as the longhead loses leverage overhead and in the dip the long head suffers from antagonistic inhibition. It would be better to replace one of these for a pushdown movement with a stationary upper arm to have a longhead biased exercise.

For his shoulders all he does a lying lateral raise. This is a great option for the side delts as it's stable and easily progressible training it where it has good leverage 0-90 degrees. But this is the only shoulder exercise he does and it would be beneficial to have a front delt exercise. He probably counts his chest pressing towards it but if you want to maximize your front delt growth you should directly train them.

And lastly he does a wrist curl and extension. The exercises he chose will be good as they are stable and easy to progress and they will be effective at growing your forearms by training flexion and extension of the wrist.

Overall his exercise selection and execution are great but it would be beneficial to have a tricep pushdown for the longhead and direct exercise to train the front delt. And his intensity is good training to failure with a good amount of volume doing 2 sets in around the 6-8 rep range. His workout is great but there are some things that can be improved.
