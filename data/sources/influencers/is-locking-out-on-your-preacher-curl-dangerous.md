# is locking out on your preacher curl dangerous?

Is locking out on preacher curls actually dangerous?

many people have the fear of tearing their bicep when fully locking out. this mentality is reinforced by videos online of people tearing their bicep on a preacher curl. but in reality this is from them using momentum, excessive weight that they arent used to, and them not normally training in that range. but is it even beneficial to be locking out?

locking out is actually beneficial to your tendons/joints as they can adapt to load and develop tendon stiffness adaptations which will strengthen them and make them less susceptible to injury. locking out will also give the benefit of standardizing your form as well as training the bicep where they have better leverage towards the bottom of the rep.

Human tendon adaptation in response to mechanical loading: a systematic review and meta-analysis of exercise intervention studies on healthy adults
Sebastian Bohm, Falk Mersmann, Adamantios Arampatzis

Patellar tendon adaptation in relation to load-intensity and contraction type
Peter Malliaras, Beenish Kamal, Alastair Nowell, Theo Farley, Hardev Dhamu, Victoria Simpson, Dylan Morrissey, Henning Langberg, Nicola Maffulli, Neil D Reeves

so perform your preacher curl in a full rom. dont make drastic jumps in weight. and train it as heavy as you would any other muscle within the 4-8 rep range.
