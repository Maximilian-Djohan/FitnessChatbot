# is a more enjoyable split better than a more optimal one?

Is a more enjoyable split better than a more optimal one?

some people like to argue that a split that is more enjoyable will be more "optimal". they often state that doing something they enjoy can help them stay consistent which is more important. they can also say that the increased motivation can help increase motor unit recruitment making it more optimal. so is this all true?

well its true but only under the pretense that you cant stay consistent or enjoy the gym without it. using an analogy you can say eating unhealthy is better than not eating at all. but this wouldn't mean that eating unhealthier is better than eating healthy. but there is a medium between being "optimal" and the diminishing return of more and more optimizations so what does this mean in the context of workout splits?

A false dilemma (or false dichotomy) is a logical fallacy that presents only two options—often extremes—as the only possibilities, when in fact more alternatives or a middle ground exist. This tactic oversimplifies complex situations to force a specific choice, commonly using "either/or" language to pressure decisions.

well in reality its very simple in the context in training. no your bro split wont be "optimal" but yes it will lead to results albeit slower than a "better split". and yes you can find outliers as other factors like genetics can expedite your progression timeline. but as long as you have a split at least training every muscle 2x every week youll be good. and for this i would recommend a two day split like UL/AP or a 3-3.5 day split like FB.
