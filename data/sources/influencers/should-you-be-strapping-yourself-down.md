# Should you be strapping yourself down?

Is there actually a benefit in tying yourself down like a science based lifter?

We know that being more stable on exercises is important as it will make the task "easier" meaning it will decrease your perception of effort which will allow for more motor unit recruitment and in turn train more muscle fibers. so should you be belting yourself down like a science based lifter?

using things like seatbelts WILL help increase stability of the exercise allowing you to get more out of it. except if it is uncomfortable. if its uncomfortable it can have the opposite effect as that sensation will increase your perception of effort. but this concept of "stability maxxing" is also seen in isokinetic testing for strength in athletes

Isokinetic testing uses specialized equipment to measure muscle strength by applying constant velocity movement with variable resistance.

so what should you do? be as stable as you can if you want to get the most out of your exercise. if you dont want to do that then dont. and if you dont feel comfortable in the setup then dont.
