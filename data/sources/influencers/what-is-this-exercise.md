# What is this exercise?

What is this exercise? What does it train and is it something you should be doing?

First we have to see what the exercise is training. Looking at the movement we can see that the exercise is training shoulder adduction. Usually we perform this when training our lats in something like a pulldown, so is this a back exercise?

Looking at the leverages of the different shoulder adductors, this exercise is actually a sternal costal chest exercise. This is because in that bottom 40 degrees of shoulder adduction the chest has better leverage over the lats. But is this something you should do?

Shoulder adduction internal moment arm lengths (IMALs) of the various prime movers vary over the frontal plane ROM. The lumbar latissimus dorsi has the longest IMAL for most of the exercise ROM. However, the sternal and costal pectoralis major have better leverage in the contracted position. The thoracic latissimus dorsi never had best leverage.
