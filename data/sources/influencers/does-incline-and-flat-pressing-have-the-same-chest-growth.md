# Does Incline and Flat pressing have the same chest growth

Is the Jeff Nippard study saying that incline and flat pressing movements actually provide the same chest muscle growth true? is there a point in doing flat and incline movements?

first a disclaimer: this isnt jeff nippards study rather he just popularized using it to fit his content. in his video he references this study where the results show that there was no statistical significance in muscle growth in the upper or mid/lower pec in either the incline or flat press. if the study came out to this conclusion is there even a reason to do both a flat and incline movement?

Effects of Horizontal and Incline Bench Press on Neuromuscular Adaptations in Untrained Young Men
SUENE F N CHAVES, VALDINAR A ROCHA-JUNIOR, IRISMAR G A ENCARNACAO, HUGO C MARTINS-COSTA, EDUARDO D S FREITAS, DANIEL B COELHO, FREDERICO S C FRANCO, JEREMY P LOENNEKE, MARTIM BOTTARO, JOAO B FERREIRA-JUNIOR
PMCID: PMC7449336 PMID: 32922646

well first we can look at the study. the study had three groups of untrained lifters a group training either incline or flat and a group training both once a week for 8 weeks. first we know that training once a week isnt good for muscle growth but lets continue. they also warmed up doing 10 pushups. the average untrained person would probably get good stimulus from that alone as they arent trained and this would impact muscle growth. but what about how the presses were conducted?

When muscle fibers are not in a post-workout stimulus period, they display atrophy, Maintenance studies allow us to determine how fast this happens.

the presses were done with the same variables changing the bench incline to 44 degrees for the incline press. isnt this good? something that jeff nippard didnt cover was their elbow flare. both presses had the same 90° flare. this would make sense in the outcomes from the study. as when you do an incline press you are supposed to tuck your arms to allow for shoulder flexion which is the upper pecs function. as arm path is the most important thing over the bench incline. so essentially they are just doing two sternalcostal chest exercises with a different bench incline.

so as a quick overview of the study. they use untrained lifters, have a warmup that is more than likely a lot more challenging than a warmup and will impact the training outcome, use a volume that will barely make any growth, and most importantly turn their incline press into basically a flat press with the way they position their arms. but if you still believe that type of exercise will effectively train both the clavicular and sternalcostal pec than go for it.
