# Does exercise enjoyment actually matter for muscle growth?

Does exercise enjoyment actually matter for muscle growth?

there are many exercises which are considered "optimal" in which people say you should replace more traditional exercises with them. but many people dont want to make the switch because it isnt enjoyable for them. does enjoyment impact muscle growth?

enjoyment is important in training as it will correlate with motivation. if you are doing an exercise you dont like you will have less motivation to do so. with less motivation you will have an higher perception of effort which will decrease motor unit recruitment. this will in turn lead to less muscle growth.

Perceived effort limits the level of motor unit recruitment that can be achieved in any maximal effort contraction, through a feedback loop.

but some exercises are objectively significantly better. enjoyment wont make up for the benefits of leverages and resistance profiles for a muscle. there should be a middle ground. try your best to pick a stable exercise that fits the strength profile of the muscle you are trying to train. but if you enjoy a slightly worse variation its fine to do that instead.

Neuromechanical matching refers to the principle where muscles or muscle regions are activated in proportion to their leverage for a specific joint action. Essentially, the nervous system preferentially recruits motor units and muscle fibers that have the best mechanical advantage for a given movement.

so enjoyment AND optimization go hand in hand. dont force yourself to do something if you truly are against it. there is a balance to it. something like a pulldown vs a flap there will be a difference but you will still be training the muscle effectively regardless of what you pick.

if its something that would create more of a difference like something like a barbell row vs a chest supported machine the difference is bigger. it would always be recommended to avoid the barbell row but if you enjoy it and dont care about being optimal it doesnt matter just do what you want.

so basically just try to be as optimal as you can but its ok to sacrifice a bit for enjoyment. it depends on your motivation and goals.
