# explaining the reasoning behind my back exercise tierlist

explaining the reasoning behind my back exercise tier list

Keenan Flaps, Kelso Shrug, Machine Pullover

starting off to preface just because something is lower on the list does not mean its not effective for muscle growth. rather based on different criteria there will be better options if your goal is to "maximize" growth for said muscle.

starting off with F tier these exercises either have no progressibility or just barely if at all train the backs functions.

next in C tier i have the standing heavy row variations that have no chest support and rely on a lot erector stabilization and or usually use a lot of momentum during the exercise.

in B tier i have more machine/stable exercises that are easier to load and actually perform without heavy stabilization requirements. but these still lack chest support so your erectors will still have to stabilize.

in A tier starting with the unilateral cable row and single arm DB row you still have the stabilization requirement without a chest support but this demand will be much less since youre only loading one arm at a time. the neutral grip pullup will be great at training shoulder extension but you will training through a high degree of shoulder elevation where other muscles are active.

next in S tier we have the machine rows/pulls with chest support/leg support and have very easy progression. these exercises will be very effective for the lats/upper back and a majority of peoples exercises should come from here.

lastly in S+ tier I have the flaps/kelso shrugs. I believe that most people should run a kelso shrug over their upper back if they have the proper setup and loading isnt an issue as its great at isolating the traps/rhomboids function of scapular retraction. then I have keenan flaps which are a single joint version of pulldowns and rows which can be used to "optimize" lat training. though it will be very setup specific with a suboptimal setup being worse than just traditional rows/pulls.

so yes most of these exercises can be effective for growing your back but due to different criteria i ranked some exercises higher than others.

Keenan Flaps, Kelso Shrug, Machine Pullover
