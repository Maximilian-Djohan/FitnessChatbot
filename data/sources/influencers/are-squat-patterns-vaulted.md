# Are squat patterns vaulted?

Are Squat Patterns vaulted? Do you need a squat pattern to maximize quad development?

We know to train the quads we need to perform their function of knee extension. This is present in a squat and a leg extension and most people use both alongside each other in their session. We also know that a leg extension is important as it trains all of the quad including the rectus femoris which a squat pattern doesnt train. So is there even a need to do a squat pattern?

against popular belief you do not "NEED" a squat pattern to maximize quad development. you will train the entire quad in a leg extension with the benefit of it being a single joint movement not having any hip extension like a squat allowing better motor unit recruitment towards the quad. so does this mean squat patterns are bad?

a squat pattern can still have their place. whether you are using them to give stimulus towards your adductors/glutes alongside your quads. another reason could to preferentially grow the vastus muscles over the rectus femoris for visual purposes (though you can do this in a leg extension).

so basically squat if you need it for your program. but if you already have specific training for all the other functions there is no "need" for it.
