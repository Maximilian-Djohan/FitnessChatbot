# What actually is fatigue?

What actually is fatigue? Is it something you should actually be managing to maximize muscle growth?

When someone is talking about fatigue in relation to resistance training there are two main types they are talking about. There is central fatigue which affects your entire body and peripheral fatigue which is localized to the muscle.

Central and peripheral fatigue are two types of muscle fatigue with distinct origins. Central fatigue originates in the brain and central nervous system, affecting the ability to initiate and sustain voluntary movements, while peripheral fatigue arises from factors within the muscles themselves, hindering their ability to contract.

under central fatigue is where supraspinal central nervous system fatigue would fall under. this fatigue happens when you have an uncomfortable "feeling" which increases your perception of effort which results in less motor unit recruitment. examples:

- Cardiovascular Sensations:
- Contribute to increased perception of effort.
- Can reduce motor unit recruitment due to discomfort from increased heart rate and breathing.
- Longer Sets:
- Lead to metabolite accumulation (e.g., lactate).
- More common with light weights and higher repetitions.
- Increasing Muscle Damage:
- Contributes to fatigue, especially from eccentric contractions
