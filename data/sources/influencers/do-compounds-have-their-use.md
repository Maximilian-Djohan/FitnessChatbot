# Do compounds have their use?

Do compound movements still have their use or are isolations always better?

the popularity of single joint movements like flies over pressing for chest and leg extensions over squats for legs are rising as they will allow you to have more motor unit recruitment towards the muscles you are training. but does this mean compound lifts dont have a purpose?

Motor unit recruitment is a measure of how many motor neurons are activated in a particular muscle, and therefore is a measure of how many muscle fibers of that muscle are activated. The higher the recruitment the stronger the muscle contraction will be.

well first is enjoyment and goals. if you enjoy an exercise you should do it as you will have a lower perception of effort when you are motivated to do something which will in turn increase MUR. and if you want to progress a certain exercise you should as you will build up neurological adaptations that will help you get stronger on that specific lift.

A lower perception of effort can lead to greater motor unit recruitment during exercise, maximizing gains.

Neural adaptations to exercise refer to changes in the nervous system that improve movement and force production

compounds will also allow you to be more time efficient as you will have less exercises in a session as you will hit multiple muscle groups in one exercise. one can also argue that beginners wont benefit from isolations as they will be in a motor unit recruitment deficit and in turn should stick to compounds.
