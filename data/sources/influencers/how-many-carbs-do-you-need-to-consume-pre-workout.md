# How many carbs do you need to consume pre workout?

How many carbs do you need to be consuming pre-workout? Are preworkout carbs overrated?

Weight training does not deplete enough glycogen to impair performance, new study finds

Acute effects of resistance exercise on skeletal muscle glycogen depletion: A systematic review and meta-anal

first why are people even arguing that preworkout carbs are useless? well its because of post from menno (keto biased) going over this meta analysis saying that muscle glycogen isnt depleted enough during your workout to make any difference in performance and thus there is no need of pre workout carbs. but is this actually true?

Acute effects of resistance exercise on skeletal muscle glycogen depletion: A systematic review and meta-analysis

Amin Hamidvand, Slaheddine Delleli, Jeffrey A Rothschild, Farzaneh Chenaghchi, Afshar Jafari, Alireza Naderi

PMCID: PMC12717450 PMID: 41420384

well the findings are actually true. during resistance training you wont deplete your muscle glycogen much at all especially in the way we train using lower rep ranges and lower volumes. and if youre already eating throughout the day you will have more than enough carbs for muscle glycogen without having the need of a specfic preworkout meal. so are carbs "vaulted"?

Muscle glycogen is the stored form of glucose in skeletal muscle, acting as a crucial, localized energy reservoir for high-intensity exercise and muscle contraction.

well we actually have carbs for a different purpose than muscle glycogen being your liver glycogen to maintain your blood glucose/sugar. a drop in your blood glucose levels will lead to supra spinal central nervous system fatigue which will impair your performance in the gym. we can even see this to a greater degree when consuming carbs intra workout we are able to see that it can even add reps.

Effect of carbohydrate mouth rinse on muscle strength and muscular endurance: A systematic review with meta-analysis

so then what should you be doing? well if youre already eating throughout the day and are at maintenance calories then youll already have carbs in your system its not something to stress to much about. but having some quick digesting carb before your workout can be used 30-60 minutes (digestion time) prior to your training if youd like.

but it becomes more important if youre either in a deficit or fasted as you wont have much carbs in your system and will be more reliant on the energy or more prone to the fatigue mechanisms from low carbs. in this case you can time more carbs pre workout having something like 60g of carbs 30-60min prior to your workout and sip on some intra workout.

High oxidation rates from combined carbohydrates ingested during exercise

Roy LPG Jentjens, Juul Achten, Asker E Jeukendrup
PMID: 15354037 DOI: 10.1249/01.mss.0000139796.07843.1d

so as a conclusion. no preworkout carbs arent vaulted but yes they are "overrated". making sure youre consuming carbs throughout the day as well as having some type of carb prior to your workout can be benefical. and if youre dieting or fasted it will be more important to do so.
