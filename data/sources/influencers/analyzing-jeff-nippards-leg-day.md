# Analyzing Jeff Nippards Leg Day

Analyzing Jeff Nippard's Leg Day to see if it's effective.

We know for a good leg day you want to train all muscles in the leg being the quads, hamstrings, adductors, glutes and calves. So I'll be looking at his leg workout and see if he trains all of these effectively.

He starts off with a leg extension which is great as it is the best quad exercise that will train all parts of quad effectively. It is also important to have this before a squat pattern if you do one as it will be less taxing. This will help with being able to recruit as much as the quad as possible without being pre-fatigued as the quads suffer from a voluntary activation deficit meaning they can't fully get recruited.

Next he does a barbell rdl. Through this he will work his glutes and adductor magnus to a good degree alongside the hamstrings though they will suffer from antagonistic inhibition due to the knee angle changing. But this is an overall good exercise for the hip extensors.

Next he does a machine hip thrust which is the best exercise for the glutes which will be very effective. He then follows this with a leg press. This will be a good stable squat pattern that will give more volume to the quads and hip extensors.

He then finishes his workout with a straight leg calf raise. This is good for the calves and his execution is perfect making sure his knees don't bend while focusing on the bottom rom to better train the gastrocnemius.

Overall this is good but he misses out on one thing. His only hamstring work is through the hinge which will train the hamstring through hip extension. But since he doesn't include a hamstring curl the biceps femoris short head won't be trained as it only performs knee flexion since it doesn't cross the hip.

So overall his workout will be very effective but it would be important to incorporate some type of hamstring curl to make sure you are training the entirety of the hamstring. Another benefit of a hamstring curl would be more distal growth (growth towards the knee).
