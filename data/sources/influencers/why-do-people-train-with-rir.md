# Why do people train with RIR?

Why do people train with RIR / Reps in the tank?

we know that we want to train to failure or very close to failure to maximize muscle growth. but if this is the case why do people train stopping a few reps before failure wouldn't that mean that they are missing out on muscle growth?

Reps in Reserve (RIR) is a method used in strength training to gauge the intensity of a set by estimating how many more repetitions could be performed before reaching technical failure.

training keeping 1-2 reps from failure can be used as a good tool to limit fatigue or any performance decrease that might hinder your workout without any significant difference in hypertrophy. so leaving 1-2 reps will provide very similar muscle growth with a bigger decrease in fatigue which could make your later sets less effective.
