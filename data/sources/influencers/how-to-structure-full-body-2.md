# How to Structure Full Body

How to structure your workout split (full body edition)

The first thing you should do is make sure youre working out all of the main muscle groups. You do this by performing the joint actions of each muscle.

MUSCLE GROUP JOINT ACTIONS
Lats: Shoulder extension
Upper Back: Scapular retraction
Front/Side Delt: Shoulder flexion, Shoulder abduction
Biceps/arms: Elbow flexion
Triceps: Elbow extension
Rear Delt: Shoulder extension, Horizontal abduction
Quads: Knee extension
Hamstring: Knee flexion
Add/abductors: Adduction/abduction
Glutes: Hip extension
Calf: Plantar flexion
Abs: Trunk flexion

next is to decide how much volume you should do. you should do ~2 exercises per muscle group with 1 set per exercise. with weak points at the start for priority. also use unilateral sets to further prioritize your weak points. you can add additional sets and test if you can progress and recover from them until you find your max recoverable volume (do what you can recover from everyone is different)

next thing to keep in mind is exercise selection. you should order your exercises based on priority to bring up any weak points. people say that with so many exercises the ones towards the end of the session will suffer which is not true. you can switch exercise order every ~4-6 months to keep everything balanced

(if no weak points run your split for a few months until then then change exercise order to fix it)

Example Full Body Day

(~x = do if you can handle the volume / change order according to you)

chest - 1x low to high fly 1x fly

lats - 1x wide grip pulldown 1x pullover

upper back - 1x upper back row

front/side delt - 1x shoulder press 1x lateral raise

biceps/arms - 1x descending preacher curl ~x reverse curl (free-weight)

triceps - 1x pushdown ~x overhead extension
rear delt - (row/pullover will be good)

quads - 1x leg extension ~x leg press
hamstring - 1x ham curl ~x hyper extension
adductor - 1x adductor

glutes - 1x hip thrust

Calf - 1x calf raise

abs - 1x
