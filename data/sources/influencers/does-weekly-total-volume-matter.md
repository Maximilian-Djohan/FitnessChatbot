# Does weekly total volume matter?

Is total weekly volume vaulted? Does your total weekly sets actually matter?

its commonly told that you need a certain amount sets per muscle group in a week to grow. is this true is there a volume quota you need for hypertrophy?

how many sets do you need per muscle group per week

Referring to a more recent systematic review, it found: The best hypertrophy results came from 30-40 sets per muscle per week. A minimum effective dose of 4 sets per muscle group per week. Somewhere around 10-20 sets per week still delivered good hypertrophy results. Nov 22, 2024

this is false. looking at this in a more extreme view many people agree that doing 12 sets 1 day per week will not be good for muscle growth. they would say splitting up throughout the week will be better. this is the concept of frequency. we know that each set in a session will have diminishing returns. so how much volume should you have per week?

Non-linear dose-response relationships of hypertrophy to workout volume vary substantially between meta-analyses, perhaps due to inclusion criteria.

(dont use the values as units to determine what you should do rather just showing the trend of diminishing return)

volume will be dependent on frequency. frequency taking priority to have more stimulating first sets. anything with a 2x or higher frequency will be sufficient. whether it is PPL, UL or FB. for example with a frequency closer to 2x per you can do ~2 exercises per muscle group with ~2 sets per exercise for something on the higher end like full body 3x you can do ~2 exercises per muscle group with ~1 set per exercise.

what should you take away from this? you will grow with lower volumes if you have good frequency. the recommendations are just a baseline. do as much as you can recover and progress from.
