Ryan Jewers says that "tiktok scienced based lifting" isnt effective. Is this true?

his first point is that "maximalist 40 set" workouts are dumb. this is true. the credible SBL dont promote this. pick muscle regions you want to grow and do exercises to train the joint action for it. for most people this will be around 8-15 functions. he also states to do 5-8 exercises. but this is not enough to cover most important muscle regions especially on something like full body.

PRIMARY JOINT ACTIONS / MUSCLE GROUP

- Chest: Horizontal adduction, Shoulder flexion
- Lats: Shoulder adduction, Shoulder extension
- Upper Back: Scapular retraction
- Front/Side Delt: Shoulder flexion, Shoulder abduction
- Biceps/arms: Elbow flexion
- Abs: Trunk flexion
- Triceps: Elbow extension
- Rear Delt: Shoulder extension/Horizontal abduction
- Quads: Knee extension
- Hamstring: Knee flexion
- Add/abductors: Adduction/abduction
- Glutes: Hip extension
- Calf: Plantar flexion

You're not a "maximalist." You would need 42-47 exercises per workout, excluding the forearms, besides, fatigue exists. It's literally impossible... volume, and exercise selection to prioritize muscles (you should already be training).

next is his statement about enjoyment. he is correct having more enjoyment and in turn motivation in your workout you will reach your maximum tolerable perceived effort later in your set. but this all comes with the assumption that people dont like the way they train. a majority of people enjoy training optimally so this point is irrelevant.

Furthermore, personal enjoyment can affect perception of effort, which is directly correlated to motor unit recruitment (MUR). If you're unmotivated and/or doing something you don't enjoy, you reach your maximum tolerable perception of effort faster, thus limiting MUR.

Strive to be optimal, and strive to make the fastest muscle gains, but that will look different for everyone..and guess what? It's not race, so why are we acting like it? Your training should be enjoyable, always. Who's to tell you it shouldn't?

besides, 99% of you will not make a career out of bodybuilding, you need the 0.01% genetics in terms of shape and size, then you also need the 0.01% genetics in terms of response to PEDs.

next he explains how full body isnt needed and 3x+ frequency is overrated. he uses anecdote to prove this. but you can also say this from the opposite side. many people who do run full body do it for the reason that they have made great progress on it. and also to insinuate that data shows a worse outcome is disingenuous as frequency studies have fullbody done with irrecoverable volumes. but yes whether there is a significance going from 2x -> 3x frequency can be argued.
