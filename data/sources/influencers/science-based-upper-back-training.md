# Science Based Upper Back Training

Science Based Upper Back
Training Explained

The upper back consists of the trap and rhomboids. split further you can divide the trap into three regions: upper, middle, lower. these muscles perform different functions with moving the scapula. another function for the upper trap is shoulder abduction (above shoulder height)

starting with the upper traps there is a misconception with how they are best trained. many people claim that it is best trained through scapular elevation (shrugging upwards) but in reality the levator scapulae is better trained in scapular elevation (muscle along neck). and the upper trap is actually better trained through abduction in something like a lu raise above 90 degrees.

abduction:

overall for the entirety of the trap and rhomboids you will just need to perform scapular retraction as the "primary" function of the lower/middle traps and rhomboids are to retract the scapula. also keep in mind your upper trap will still contribute to this. this can be an upper back row or shrug.

Adduction (retraction)

so to train the traps all you need is an upper back row or kelso shrug. and optionally if you want to better train the upper trap you can do a lu raise (front delts will be worked in this)
