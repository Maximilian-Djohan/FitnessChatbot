# What is the best tricep attachment?

What is the best attachment for your tricep pushdown?

its often debated what the best tricep exercise is. usually based on stability or rom. the most popular tricep attachment would be the rope being said it helps increase the rom of your tricep pushdown. so is the rope the best attachment?

The rope pushdown allows for a greater range of motion and can isolate the triceps more effectively, while the straight bar pushdown allows for lifting heavier weights.

well what you are training in a tricep pushdown? its simple you're training elbow extension. this will be the same throughout all the attachments. but the rope is known to be the best for rom. this is false. the only extra range you would get is from moving your arm backwards at the shoulder. your elbow has no contribution to this and in turn doesn't have any benefit from this. so what is the best attachment?

besides armpath your wrist grip is also affected whether its neutral or pronated. in this case stability comes to play. being pronated against the bar in something like a bar pushdown will allow you to be more stable than something like a rope pushdown. also material of the attachments should be considered as you will have better grip on something like a bar compared to a rope.

what about the vbar? it is stable like the straight bar so how do they compare? its also a great option but it has the problem of wrist discomfort. as the attachment doesn't rotate throughout the movement it won't allow you to keep your wrists straight and ends up putting a lot of force on your wrists towards the bottom which will make you perform worse. so which out of the three is the best tricep attachment

realistically all of them will be great choices and if you prefer one it will be fine to go for it. but based on stability a straight bar will be a marginally better option compared the other options. (but besides these options cuffs will always be better as you are eliminating that wrist demand)
