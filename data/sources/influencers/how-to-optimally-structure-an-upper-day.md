# How to optimally structure an upper day

How to "optimally" structure an entire upper day

first we want to identify which muscle groups we want to train. then we train their main function. we do this by training their primary joint action.

MUSCLE GROUP PRIMARY JOINT ACTIONS
Horizontal adduction
chest Shoulder flexion
Shoulder adduction
Lats Shoulder extension
Upper Back Scapular retraction
Front/Side Delt Shoulder flexion
Shoulder abduction
Biceps/arms Elbow flexion
Triceps Elbow extension
Rear Delt Shoulder extension/Horizontal abduction
Quads Knee extension
Hamstring Knee flexion
Add/abductors Adduction/abduction
Glutes Hip extension
Calf Plantar flexion
Abs Trunk flexion

the chest has the functions of horizontal adduction and shoulder flexion. you can train this through a pec dec and low to high fly variation.

the chest has the functions of shoulder adduction and shoulder extension you can train this through a pulldown and close grip row/pullover

the upper back has the function of scapular retraction you can train this through an kelso shrug or upper back row

the elbow flexors (biceps/brachioradialis/brachialis) have the function of elbow flexion. you can train this through a curl variation. (do descending for biceps and ascending for brachioradialis)

the triceps have the function of elbow extension. you can train this through an pushdown/extension

perform these exercises in a lower rep range from 4-8 and for sets do what you can progress from. your sets per session will depend on your frequency of leg days.
