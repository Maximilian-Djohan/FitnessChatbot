# Should isometrics replace your normal training?

Should isometrics replace your
normal lifting (isometrics)?

Isometrics are when you contract the muscle while there is no movement at the joint. we know that they meet the requirements for muscle growth as you can perform them with a high degree of effort and mechanical tension so should you replace normal training with them?

Voluntarily increasing central motor command increases perception of effort, and the number of recruited motor units (and the number of activated muscle fibers)

if you can still keep progressing a good movement (good resistance and setup for targeted muscle) you should keep doing normal training. isometrics have a hard time gauging progression with which is a big flaw. It should be used on exercises where you've maxed out progression or on exercises where you can't normally progress with weight. e.g. adductors or something like finger training.
