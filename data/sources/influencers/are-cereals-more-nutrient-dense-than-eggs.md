# are cereals more nutrient dense than eggs?

Are cereals more nutrient dense then eggs? How can you determine if a food is good for hitting micronutrients?

some people argue whether some foods are nutrient dense or how some sources compare to a "natural" source. starting off we can look at the micronutrient profile of something like a fortified cereal e.g cocoa pebbles vs something like eggs. we can see that your folate, B & A vitamins are hit better in the cereal. but is the absorption actually the same?

another factor is the source of where the vitamins come from. in a "fortified" cereal these are synthetic vitamins that are added. this can cause problems as some sources arent as bioavailable as others. looking at the fortified B and A vitamins the bioavailability of their sources are very high in the cereal. though the iron has a lower bioavailability compared to animal products up to ~20% but it makes up for this by just adding more iron. (eggs have an even lower bioavailibility for iron)

Bioavailability is a pharmacological and nutritional term that describes the rate and extent to which an active substance (such as a drug, vitamin, or nutrient) is absorbed into the systemic circulation and becomes available at its intended site of action.

so what does this mean? while you cant take vitamins at face value due to the bioavailability of the source many fortified options have this in mind in their process. so yes something like cereal can be used to hit your micronutrients. (keep in mind that A,D,E,K vitamins are fat soluble so you will need to have a fat source to absorb it properly e.g milk)

Fortified foods have extra vitamins, minerals, and other nutrients added during processing to boost their nutritional value. This is a public health strategy to prevent nutrient deficiencies.
