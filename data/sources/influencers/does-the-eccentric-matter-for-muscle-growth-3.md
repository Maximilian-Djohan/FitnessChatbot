# Does the eccentric matter for muscle growth?

Does the eccentric matter for muscle growth?

Eccentric Concentric

another concept that is often regarded as important due to popular creators is the "eccentric" but is having an emphasized "slow and controlled" eccentric as important as they preach?

(one of these creators: dr mike israetel)

it actually isnt what they make it out to be as during the eccentric portion of the exercise muscle fibers are able to produce around 2x the amount force compared to the concentric due to passive mechanical tension. This means effort needed for the eccentric is halved meaning you are only able to recruit 50% of the muscle fibers compared to the concentric resulting in 50% less fibers experiencing any stimulus during it. so should you ignore the eccentric?

Passive mechanical tension during the eccentric phase of muscle contraction is the force generated by the stretch of elastic elements within the muscle, such as titin, when the muscle is lengthened.

the eccentric is still an important part to standardizing your form and you should have intent with it. this doesnt mean you need to go slow but have it controlled enough.
