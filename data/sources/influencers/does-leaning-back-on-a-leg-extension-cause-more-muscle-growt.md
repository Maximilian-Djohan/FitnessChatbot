the rectus femoris is a biarticular muscle meaning it crosses both the hip and the knee. so when you are leaned back it will lengthen it more at the hip. so in the study where they used untrained lifters they would make stretch mediated hypertrophy adaptations. this growth wont be long term and will be maxed out after youve made them. this means you also wont benefit from leaning back over a longer period of time. so should you lean back or not?

During eccentric training, fascicle lengths increase rapidly for the first two months and then plateau in the third month (while pennation angles do not change). This likely occurs due to the decreasing amount of passive tension generated over time.

there is no need to lean back during your leg extension. you will be giving up stability that can help increase motor unit recruitment for your long term growth for a short term adaptation. also training at a longer length will incur more muscle damage. so train how you normally would and if you do want to develop and maintain stretch mediated hypertrophy adaptations you can stretch after training.
