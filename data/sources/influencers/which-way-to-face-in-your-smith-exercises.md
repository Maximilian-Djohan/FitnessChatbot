# Which way to face in your smith exercises

Which way should you face on your chest pressing, rows and deadlift/squat variations on a slanted smith machine?

when deciding what way to face on your angle smith exercises we want to know what joint action we are trying to train. then with the exercise setup we want to face the way that will best allow you to best train that joint action.

| Muscle Group | Joint Actions |
|---|---|
| Lats | Shoulder extension |
| Upper Back | Scapular retraction |
| Front/Side Delt | Shoulder flexion, Shoulder abduction |
| Biceps/arms | Elbow flexion |
| Triceps | Elbow extension |
| Rear Delt | Shoulder extension/Horizontal abduction |
| Quads | Knee extension |
| Hamstring | Knee flexion |
| Add/abductors | Adduction/abduction |
| Glutes | Hip extension |
| Calf | Plantar flexion |
| Abs | Trunk flexion |

starting with your pressing you want to setup the bench aligned with the angle of the smith.

for your hinges i.e RDLS/SLDLS you also want to be aligned with the angle of the smith.

for your squat pattern this will depend on what your goal of the squat is. for a quad biased squat focusing on knee extension opposed to hip extension we want to be aligned with the angle. and vice versa focusing on hip extension for the glutes/adductor magnus you will want to face away from the angled smith.

and for your rowing you want to also face aligned with the smith to allow yourself to maintain the same elbow flare throughout the range of motion.

so as a summary facing aligned with the angle for chest pressing, hinges, quad biased
