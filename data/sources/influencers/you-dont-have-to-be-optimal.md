# you don't have to be optimal

Why you DON'T have to be optimal to make great progress in the gym.

This is why you see people promote sub optimal methods as they WILL work given the time. So is SBL useless? No. Training efficiently can speed up this process. This doesn't mean you will be growing faster than others. You will just be reaching your end goal quicker. Some people can do whatever and grow more than you ever will. But if you want to reach your goal faster, that is what SBL is for.

But there will never be some way of training that will definitively make a crazy difference over a long period of time. This is because any type of "decent" training where you are consistent and put in effort, you will make progress. This is also why you see people who train "unoptimally" make progress. The fact that you are actually training makes the biggest difference.

Unoptimal training refers to suboptimal or inefficient exercise programming.

With science based lifting or just gym advice in general, many people say to do something a certain way to grow better. Some people use other people's anecdotes, like popular bodybuilders, to why you should do another thing. But how should you know what will actually work?
