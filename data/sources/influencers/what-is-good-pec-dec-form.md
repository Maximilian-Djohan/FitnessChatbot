# What is good pec dec form?

What is good pec dec form? How should you setup your pec dec?

Some people struggle with pec dec form where the movement feels awkward or puts strain on other muscle groups but how should you set it up?

The main supporting muscles in a pec dec exercise are the anterior deltoids (front shoulders), which assist in bringing the arms together, and the biceps, which provide stabilization to the elbows.

Starting off with seat height. The seat height will determine where your arms are in relation to the handles. You want to have the seat height to allow your arms to be relatively straight across making sure your performing pure horizontal adduction. Another thing to keep in mind is that you should have your torso stacked above your pelvis maintaining an unbending posture.

Next is how far you set the handles back. The farther back represents where your rep will start/end. You want to set it so your rep starts at the most to the side of your torso. Letting it go farther back then this will put strain on the shoulder.

Next is exercise execution. On the eccentric as you get closer to your torso it is fine to bend your elbows a bit take some load of your biceps but on the concentric at the end of your rep your elbows should be locked out. If you find yourself bending your arms at the end of your reps to finish the rep you are using too much weight/ego lifting.

So as a quick summary: have your arms straight across, have your torso relatively straight against the pad, start the reps by the side of your torso, and dont actively bend your arms at the end of your reps.
