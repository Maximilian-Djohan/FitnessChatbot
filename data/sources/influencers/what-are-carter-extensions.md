# What are carter extensions?

What are carter extensions? Are they actually a good exercise?

Carter extensions are normal single arm tricep extensions except for the fact that they have support behind the upper arm. but are they actually good?

well while your upper arm doesnt have to be supported as there isnt a force pushing you a direction where you would need it. if you feel like it makes you more stable it will be beneficial. just the "feeling" of it being more stable will lower your perception of effort which will allow for more motor unit recruitment.

Motor unit recruitment is a measure of how many motor neurons are activated in a particular muscle, and therefore is a measure of how many muscle fibers of that muscle are activated.

so yes the carter extension will be a great exercise for the longhead of your tricep as it will be hardest in a more flexed elbow position at 90 degrees and if the support helps you then its even better. (other heads will still be worked)

Triceps brachii moment arm lengths change with elbow joint angle, increasing shoulder angle alters the proportional involvement of each head of the triceps brachii, towards full elbow extension.
