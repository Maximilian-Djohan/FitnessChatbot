# Why do people do low sets of exercises if it doesn't maximize growth.

Why do people do low sets per exercise if it doesn't maximize growth?

by training an exercise with more volume by allocating more sets to the function its training we will be able to get closer to maximizing growth for it. but then why do some people do something like one set on each exercise?

doing an exercise you will train muscle fibers that perform it. while you can think of an exercise training an entire muscle group you can go even further. e.g. your biceps/elbow flexors all you need is a curl variation. doing 3 sets of a curl can "maximize" the growth of muscle fibers required for that curl faster in the short term compared to 1 set. but say we split that volume into three different exercises: a biceps biased curl, brachioradialis curl, and a biceps supination. yes you will reach that end goal later but with the trade off of developing more muscle fibers that wouldn't have grown otherwise.

Greater training volumes are associated with more rapid hypertrophy. Yet, each set does not cause the same amount of muscle growth. Later sets in a workout are less effective.

To double the amount of muscle hypertrophy achieved by the low category (3 sets/week), subjects would need to do 18 sets/week.

it just depends on your goals looking in the short term or long term. you will make great progress regardless of what you do but some people like to be specific and enjoy it. and you can do more sets while being specific but there is only so many sets you can do in a session and a ton of muscle regions that you can train. so what should you do? pick the muscle regions you want to grow and do as much volume you can recover from training those muscle regions whether its 8 exercises or 20 exercises train towards the physique you want to build.

but another thing to keep in mind is diminishing return. yes the more sets you do the more stimulus you will get. but as you do more sets of an exercise you will get diminishing return as additional sets will provide less hypertrophy and more muscle damage. this is another reason people like to do 1 set of multiple exercises as they will just do the most stimulating set.

During strength training, each additional set performed stimulates progressively less hypertrophy but causes progressively more muscle damage. Early sets are efficient insofar as they stimulate a great deal of muscle growth but very little muscle damage.
