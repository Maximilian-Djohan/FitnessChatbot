# Just because you're pulling from above doesn't mean it's a pulldown

Just because you're pulling from above you doesn't mean it's a pulldown. In fact it can be redundant

Machine lat pullover

many people do some type of overhead pull or pullover instead of something like a pulldown with the methodology that if you are pulling from above you it will be the same as a pulldown. this is false. you can determine this with your arm path where these other type of pulls even if they start above you your arms are very tucked making you perform shoulder extension like a close grip row rather than adduction in a pulldown

shoulder extension: shoulder adduction:

in reality what you are performing is the same as what you are performing in your close grip row. and as you are going overhead in high degrees of shoulder extension you will have chest and triceps involvement alongside your lats.

ht (EZ bar) pull

so these other exercises will be performing the same task as what your close grip row. so if you want to incorporate them you should do them in place of the close grip row and limit the rom to below shoulder height.
