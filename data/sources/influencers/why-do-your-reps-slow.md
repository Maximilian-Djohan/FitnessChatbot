# Why do your reps slow?

Why do your reps slow towards the end of your set? What does it have to do with muscle growth?

We know that when training to failure your reps will involuntarily slow and this is important as those last 5 reps will be the most stimulating reps. but what causes this involuntary slowing of contraction speed? (involuntary slowing = rep on the concentric is slower even though you are maintaining max effort every rep)

when you hit failure on an exercise (your last rep) this happens due to you hitting your maximum tolerable perception of effort (your brain says you can't go anymore). this is shown with the slowing of your reps as you get closer to this maximum point your each rep is slower due to you not being able recruit as many muscle fibers.

The maximum tolerable perception of effort is the point where you stop an exercise set because the feeling of exertion becomes too great, and it is often the limiting factor for performance. Reaching this maximum perception of effort causes task failure.

so what does this mean? you should be training to failure / 0 rir and a way to tell you're getting closer to this point is if your reps are slowing but not due to you purposefully doing so.
