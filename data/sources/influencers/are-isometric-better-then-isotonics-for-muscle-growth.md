# are isometric better then isotonics for muscle growth?

Are isometrics better for muscle growth compared to normal reps? What are isometrics?

Isometrics are when you contract the muscle while there is no movement at the joint. you can think of this as pushing or pulling on a stationary object where the joint doesnt move. but can this actually be good for muscle growth?

Isometrics are a type of exercise where muscles contract and create tension, but their length doesn't change and no joint movement occurs.

first we need to see if isometrics fit the requirements for muscle growth which is a high degree of effort and mechanical tension. these two are both present when doing a max effort isometric. so should you implement them?

there is a main flaw of isometrics which is progression. the way you would progress an isometric is through producing more force but you cant really track that without specialized equipment. they also lack standardization of form and it will be hard to keep motivation on them compared to a normal isotonic exercise. so do they have their use?

Progressive overload with isometrics involves progressively increasing the challenge by increasing amount of force produced from set to set.

they still have their use whether it be training something where you have joint pain, maxed out a machine, lack of equipment, easier setup for less important muscle groups etc. and if you are wondering how to perform them you can do so through reps of 3-5 second isometric bursts to make a ~15 second set.
