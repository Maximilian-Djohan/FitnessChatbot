# Why do some people band their exercises?

Why do some people band their exercises? Should you band your exercises for more muscle growth?

(Give it a try)

Sometimes you can see people adding a band on their exercises but what purpose does this serve? are they just doing it to ego lift? no. they do this to alter the resistance profile of the exercise. you can use the band to change where the exercise is hardest to match up with the leverages of the muscle you are training. (this is often seen in a JM Press and Squat Patterns)

A resistance profile describes how the difficulty or force of an exercise changes throughout its range of motion.

why do people do this on a JM Press? when doing a JM Press you are training elbow extension for the triceps with a small degree of the chest and shoulder involvement. so when we band it we are making it easier at the bottom and harder at the top. this aligns better disadvantaging any other muscle group (chest at the bottom) that can possibly be active as well as it better matches the strength profile of the triceps harder towards lockout.

Triceps brachii moment arm lengths change with elbow joint angle, increasing towards full elbow extension.

why do people do this on a Squat Pattern? we know that when doing a squat pattern you will be training knee extension of the quads. but the deeper you go there will also be hip extension present. as you go deeper you will have more hip extension which will make your squat more adductors and glutes. so when you band it it evens it out the resistance making it less descending (less very hard at bottom) to make it more quads.

so should you band your exercises? you can if you want and wanna optimize it more but you will still be training your muscles effectively regardless.
