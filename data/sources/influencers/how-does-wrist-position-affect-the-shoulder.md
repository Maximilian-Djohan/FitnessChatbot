# how does wrist position affect the shoulder

how does wrist position affect your lateral raise? what is best for muscle growth?

many people take a neutral grip (palms down) when performing their side delts. but is this actually the best for the side delt?

well looking at activation data that compares a normal grip, externally rotated grip (thumbs up) and internally rotated grip (thumbs down) we see that a traditional grip has the best activation. we can further explain this outcome as IMAL and fiber orientation best aligns with the side delt in this variation. so what does this mean?

well for lateral raises this means you can just continue what youre doing. but some people like to train their side delts in a shoulder press. while this will be very effective for the side delts we can see that as you are externally rotated it will be worse for the side delts (besides that more muscles are involved taking away from the side delts). so to best train side delts you should have a palms down lateral raise variation.
