# Bent vs Straight arm pec dec

Bent Arm vs. Straight Arm on Pec Dec Which is better for muscle growth and whats the difference between them?

many people like to say having a bent arm is "wrong form" saying that it decreases the moment arm making the exercise easier or it will turn it into a press and thus involving the triceps making it counter productive. but is this actually true?

the exercise wont become easier as you bend the elbow. why is this? the torque on the shoulder will remain the same because its based on the cam of the machine which is fixed and a bend in your arm wont affect this. but what about turning it into a press?

turning it into a "press" can actually be beneficial. as you bend the arm you will be decreasing torque at the elbow. so normally with a straight arm your biceps will have some involvement but the bend can decrease/eliminate this involvement. and you dont have to worry about tricep involvement as there is not a moment arm for elbow extension.

another complaint is for people who keep a bent arm towards the end of the movement. since the chest performs horizontal adduction bringing the arm towards the mid line of the body people will say that you are losing range of motion on the chest. relatively you are only missing out on 10-20 degrees of range and will be a similar range to a chest press. as long as you dont actively bend your arms more at the end you wont lose out on rom.

so what should you do? well sometimes you can feel like your biceps are limiting you based on limb length or specific fly machine so having a bending/bent arm can help with this. if not then you can use either a bent or straight arm and both will be good there is no wrong choice both are effective.
