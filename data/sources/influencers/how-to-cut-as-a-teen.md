# How to cut as a teen?

How to cut as a teen? Should you be cutting and is it safe?

my client & friend @shaonuff minus 20 lbs

your teenage years are a very important period of growth. because of this the idea that you shouldnt be in a calorie deficit as energy is important to help "fuel" these changes. but does this actually have any merit?

The ages of 12-18 represent a major growth period defined by puberty, rapid physical spurts, and cognitive development. Teens experience significant changes in height, body composition, and voice

well its both true and false. as a teen you will be healthy and can maintain/gain at 20% bodyfat rather than my usual recommendation of getting to around 10-15% first. I would only actively recommend cutting when younger if you are at higher bodyfat ~30%+ as your health can be negatively impacted being at that body composition. but even with the case at being at 20% some people still want to be leaner. so how should you cut as a teen?

well our priority is health. first we do not want to push the cut too hard to limit any negative effects of deficit meaning I would recommend sticking to ~500 cal deficit per day in which you are not only subtracting food but also adding in activity to fulfill the deficit

A daily calorie deficit of ~500 calories (3,500 per week) generally leads to a weight loss of about 1 pound (0.45 kg) of fat per week.

besides this we want to make sure other factors are in check. the main ones being sleep and diet. for sleep you want to make sure to get at least 7-9+ hours of quality of sleep. and for your diet you should make sure to consume the correct proportions of protein 0.8g per lb, ~30-40g fats (supplementing omega 3s) w/ rest in carbs. and while this isnt the most "important" you should still try to achieve all your micronutrients as it will be

So as an overview. one cutting is not important for teens. but if you do choose to cut you should do it smart. dont be aggressive and be in control of all the factors that can influence your health such as your diet and sleep. there wont be an inherent risk if done correctly.
