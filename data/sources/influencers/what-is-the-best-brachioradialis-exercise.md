# What is the best brachioradialis exercise?

What is the BEST brachioradialis exercise?

to train the brachioradialis we want to bias it in comparison to the other elbow flexors. we do this with wrist position and resistance profiles. for the brachioradialis it gets advantaged with an ascending resistance profile (hard at top) and a pronated grip. what is a good exercise for this?

Elbow flexor moment arm lengths increase with increasing elbow flexion and are maximal in the contracted position. The brachioradialis has very poor leverage when lengthened and the best leverage when shortened.

Biceps brachii moment arm lengths are longer when the wrist is supinated (although only in the middle of the elbow joint angle range of motion). This means that we can therefore target the biceps brachii with supinated grip biceps curls, and the brachioradialis with pronated grip biceps curls.

the most "maximalist" exercise you can do for this is a cable top half cuffed reverse curl. by limiting the rom we can train where the brachioradialis has best relative leverage in elbow extension (above 70 degrees). it is also ascending as it has best leverage around 100 degrees which is towards the end of the rep.
