# Incline Dumbbell is overrated

Incline Dumbbell Is overrated. Incline Barbell is actually better for muscle growth.

incline dumbbell press is a very popular as a better alternative to incline barbell. but is it actually better? through incline pressing we are training shoulder flexion and horizontal shoulder flexion which both these exercises do. but the db variation is said to give a better stretch on the pec. but we know that the chest has better leverage within that mid/lengthened position and in turn that extra rom has no benefit defeating the db's purpose. but does that mean its worse then a barbell?

well we need to know that we want have the highest degree of motor unit recruitment as we can to recruit as many muscle fibers we can. some factors that will affect this will be stability/coordination demand which is a bigger requirement to using dumbbells as they are unstable especially as you get heavier. and in turn barbells will have less of this demand being more stable and in turn being able to have a higher degree of motor unit recruitment

Motor unit recruitment during exercise refers to the process of activating different motor units to produce varying levels of muscle force. As exercise intensity increases, the nervous system recruits progressively larger and faster motor units to meet the demand.

so is the incline dumbbell press bad? no its still good and if you enjoy doing it then you should. but you shouldnt do it over a barbell variant for the reason that you think its better as it isnt. and regardless a machine press or fly will be better.
