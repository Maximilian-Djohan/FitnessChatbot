to know if the muscle will benefit from unilateral training it has to overcome the bilateral force deficit. this means to benefit your unilateral exercise should be performing more than half the weight than the bilateral version of the exercise. this shows that you are actually recruiting more muscle fibers. but what would make it so you arent overcoming the bilateral force deficit?

The bilateral force deficit (BLD) refers to the phenomenon where the combined force produced by both limbs during a simultaneous, maximal bilateral contraction is less than the sum of the forces produced by each limb working independently.

things that would make it so an exercise doesnt overcome the bilateral force deficit would be if the unilateral exercise is unstable and/or uncomfortable. a common problem occurs is through lateral/rotational instability. this would happen as there is no counter force on the non working side causing your body to move during your uni exercise which will in turn lower MUR canceling out any benefit of doing an unilateral exercise.

In exercises like lunges or single-leg squats, counterforce is the action of the opposite limb (or core) to stabilize the working limb and maintain proper alignment and balance.
