# explaining the reasoning behind my triceps exercise tierlist

explaining the reasoning behind my triceps exercise tier list

starting off to preface just because something is lower on the list does not mean its not effective for muscle growth. rather based on different criteria there will be better options if your goal is to "maximize" growth for said muscle.

starting off with F tier these exercises have a bad progressive runway in progressive overloading weight. and the dumbbell kickback wont be loaded much due

at C tier i have presses and freeweight triceps exercises. the presses wont be as good as the other exercises on the list due more musculature involved with the chest and shoulders leading to less recruitment in the triceps. and the freeweight exercises while viable lack stability compared to the other exercises on the list.

in B tier i have the supinated pushdown and skull crusher. the supinated pushdown using your hands will be limited by your finger flexors during the movement especially as you get to higher weights. the skullcrusher is also a decent option but it just lacks stability compared to exercises higher on the list.

in A tier i have pushdowns using a rope and dips. pushdowns with a rope are very effective but are worse than other pushdowns on the list due to grip instability. for dips they are also very effective but due to the amount of shoulder flexion your front delt will be very active during the movement.

in S tier i placed the "refined" versions of previous exercises. pushdowns using a bar will have less grip demand compared to the other attachments. alongside this is the overhead extension which is also one of the best tricep exercises. then we have the machine dip which is much more stable and easier to load then the free weight variant and if i had the JM Press in the list i would also have it around here with the only thing holding it back is the chest/shoulder involvement but again they are great medial/lateral head exercises

lastly for S+ i have the tricep extension machine which is just a stable variation of your pushdown variants with a fixed arm path with more support being seated. then i have cuffed variations of pushdowns/overhead extensions which eliminate any wrist limitations. and any of these exercises will train all parts of the tricep with each leading to a slight bias in certain heads.

so yes most of these exercises on the list can be effective for growing your triceps but due to different criteria i ranked some exercises higher than others.
