# What is the post activation effect?

What is the Post Activation Potentiation Effect? How can we use it to increase muscle growth?

The post activation potentiation effect is a phenomenon by which the force exerted by a muscle is increased due to its previous contraction. We do this to improve force output for your working set. But what does it actually look like in practice?

Post-activation potentiation (PAP) refers to the short-term improvement in muscle performance following a previous bout of high-intensity muscular activity.

You will induce the post activation potentiation effect in your last warmup prior to your working set. This warmup set you will use a heavy weight around 80-90% of your working weight for 1 rep.
