# Does exercise order matter?

Does exercise order matter? Does it affect muscle growth?

Many people order exercises based on weight used having more heavier "compound" movements at the start of your workout. Is this actually how you should structure it?

Yes, the order of exercises in a workout does matter, especially when focusing on strength and muscle growth. Generally, you should prioritize compound exercises (those that work multiple muscle groups) and perform them at the beginning of your workout when you are freshest and have the most energy. This allows you to lift heavier and perform more repetitions.

There are a few things to look at when ordering exercises being muscle priority, cardiovascular demand, and coordination requirement. Starting off with priority it is the most important for exercise order. You should have your weak points at the start of your session as you will be more fresh for them. As you continue to do exercises there will be an intrasession fatigue build up which will hinder lifts later in the session.

Supraspinal CNS fatigue, a component of central fatigue, refers to a decline in the ability of the brain to effectively drive muscles, leading to reduced force production during exercise.

Next is the cardiovascular demand of an exercise. an example of this can be seen in leg training. exercises like your leg extension will be less cardiovascular demanding then something like a leg press. also something like a leg extension will cause less muscle damage so in comparison having something more demanding like a leg press earlier in your session it can hinder later movements.

Very heavy resistance training places significant cardiovascular demand on the body, primarily due to increased blood pressure and heart rate.

the last is the coordination requirement of an exercise. this is usually in relation when doing a heavier free-weight compound. while cardiovascular demand and muscle priority are important if you want to progress certain movements that have a higher coordination requirement you can also have those first in session. this can be seen in something like bench pressing having it earlier in session can make the task requirement easier as you will be the freshest at the start.
