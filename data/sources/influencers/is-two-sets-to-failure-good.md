# is two sets to failure good?

Is two sets to failure valid? What is the best for muscle growth?

In the past few years doing two sets to failure on exercises was popularized over doing other variations like 4x12. And while it is better than that, with the rising popularity of higher frequency training and even lower volumes, does it still have its use?

Training to failure with partial reps involves performing exercises until you can no longer complete a full range of motion repetition, then continuing with partial reps to further stimulate muscle growth.

Analyzing it, doing two sets to failure is useful compared to other higher volumes as you wont be doing something excessive, and while training with max effort you will recruit as many fibers as possible compared to sticking to a number of reps. And staying to a lower amount of sets you'll be able to recover from this high intensity training. So doesnt that mean its still good?

Voluntarily increasing central motor command increases perception of effort, and the number of recruited motor units (and the number of activated muscle fibers).

First, what is failure? There are two definitions of this. The 1st being you cant complete another full rep aka 0RIR or 2nd where you do partials until you cant move the weight anymore which was popular with 2x to failure. But training this 2nd way is worse. When you cant complete another full rep your brain has reached its maximum tolerable perception of effort. But if you continue with partials it wont be productive as your brain wont be able to recruit many muscle fibers and you will be generating exponentially more fatigue with very little benefit.

There are multiple types of fatigue mechanism present during a strength training set. Each type of fatigue mechanism accumulates at a different rate, and has different effects. Some fatigue mechanisms mainly affect single muscle fiber shortening velocity, others reduce motor unit recruitment, and others reduce single muscle fiber force.

Fatigue progression through the set. Proximity to failure (measured by reps in reserve).

So what does this mean? 2x sets to failure is good if you stop at 0RIR. But overall you shouldnt base volume by just giving two sets to everything. You need to take into account your training frequency with your split and your priorities. Depending on split, one, two, or even three sets to failure will be productive. But yes, 2 sets to failure (0RIR) is a good baseline.
