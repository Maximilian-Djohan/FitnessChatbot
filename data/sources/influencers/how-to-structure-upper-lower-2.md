# How to Structure Upper Lower

How to structure your workout split (upper lower edition)

The first thing you should do is make sure youre working out all of the main muscle groups. You do this by performing the joint actions of each muscle.

PRIMARY MUSCLE GROUP JOINT ACTIONS
Chest: Horizontal adduction, Shoulder flexion
Lats: Shoulder adduction, Shoulder extension
Upper Back: Scapular retraction
Front/Side Delt: Shoulder flexion, Shoulder abduction
Biceps/arms: Elbow flexion
Triceps: Elbow extension
Rear Delt: Shoulder extension, Horizontal abduction
Quads: Knee extension
Hamstring: Knee flexion
Add/abductors: Adduction/abduction
Glutes: Hip extension
Calf: Plantar flexion
Abs: Trunk flexion

next thing to keep in mind is exercise selection. you should order your exercises based on priority to bring up any weak points. many people say arms lack on upper but if thats the case just do arms before chest/back work

(if no weak points run your split for a few months until then then change exercise order to fix it)

next is to decide how much volume you should do. you should do 1-2 exercises per muscle group with 1-2 sets per exercise. your weak points should have more sets then your strong points. you should also utilize unilateral movements to further prioritize any weak points

(do what you can recover from everyone is different)

Example Upper Day

chest - 1x incline press 1x fly

lats - 1x wide grip pulldown 1x sagittal plane row

upper back 1x upper back row
front/side delt - 1x shoulder press 1x lateral raise

biceps/arms - 1x descending preacher curl 1x reverse curl (free-weight)
triceps - 1x pushdown 1x overhead extension

rear delt - (both rows will be good)

Example Lower Day

quads - 1x leg extension 1x leg press
hamstring - 1x hyperextension 1x hamstring curl

add/abductor - 1x adduction / 1x abduction

glutes - 1x hip thrust

calf - 2x straight knee calf variation
abs - 1x crunch var
