# Analyzing Jeff Nippards Arm Workout

He starts off with a bayesian curl for the biceps. This is a decent choice as there is data that shows having your arm behind your body in shoulder extension can help bias the biceps as well as being supinated will help disadvantage the other elbow flexors. But this variation will place load on the shoulder and lack the stability of something like a preacher or recline curl.

Next he does a "throat crusher" this will be good for the medial and lateral head of the triceps. Though they will disadvantage the longhead as it is biarticular and performs shoulder flexion and since the antagonistic joint action is occurring (shoulder extension) it will suffer from antagonistic inhibition causing it to not be trained well.

He then does one set of a preacher curl and while it is "redundant" with the bayesian curl as it is a very similar challenge it is fine if you are managing volume and progressing with it. You can also say that it will be more brachialis based on having your arm in more of a flexed shoulder to disadvantage the biceps.

He then follows this up with machine lateral raise. This is a very good exercise for the side delt as it is stable and easily progressible. Also since he performs it unilaterally where less overall musculature will be involved he will be able to recruit more muscle fibers in the side he is using.

He then follows this up with a tricep pushdown/extension. This will be good for the entire tricep as well as the longhead as your upper arm is stationary by your side which will cover what he was missing from the "throat crusher".

He then does an inverse zottman curl which is essentially pointless after the other two curl variations. The eccentric challenge while being supinated will have no good purpose for his biceps and if he wanted to disadvantage the biceps on the concentric he should've just stuck to a curl with a pronated grip.

He then ends this with a wrist curl and wrist extension for the forearms. These are stable exercises that train the functions of the forearm effectively.

So overall his workout is pretty good in terms of exercise selection and volume. Though the zottman curl could be scrapped or replaced with a better option. And for shoulders he only did a lateral raise meaning his front delt won't be getting any work and I would recommend that everyone have a top half shoulder press alongside this to train the front delt.
