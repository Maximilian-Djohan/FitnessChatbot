# Do you need to feel a muscle in order to grow?

Do you need to "feel" a muscle in order for it to grow?

many people often say they have the problem of feeling a given muscle during an exercise. this is often associated with lat training as many people struggle to feel their lats during a pulldown. is this an actual problem and what should you do if it is?

your main priority in this scenario is to check your technique. if your technique is perfect you will be targeting the muscle you are trying to train. you could infer this by thinking about your purpose when training the lats. you are trying to train shoulder adduction in which the primary mover for this joint action would be the lats.

The lats are key in pulling the arm towards the body (adduction), as seen in exercises like pull-ups, lat pulldowns.

another reason why you might struggle is because of tempo/rom. we know that training the lats in a pulldown they have best leverage at 60-75 degrees of adduction which is toward the middle/bottom portion of the rep. some people overemphasize the top portion of the movement aiming for the stretch (the top) where the lats have worse leverage or even worse the eccentric of the movement where there are less muscle fibers active.

The latissimus dorsi has three main regions (thoracic, lumbar, and pelvic) and they each have very different leverages for shoulder extension and adduction.
