# What is a Keenan Flap and is it a good exercise?

What is a "Keenan Flap" and is it a good exercise?

The "Keenan Flap" or a unilateral frontal plane pullover is a lower lat biased exercise training shoulder adduction which is comparable to a wide grip pulldown. But is this a better exercise than a pulldown, and if so why?

One of the reasons why is when looking at the range of motion. Eliminating the top part of the ROM is good as the chest has a better relative involvement in the contribution in shoulder adduction in that ROM. Also, staying below 90 degrees of shoulder adduction you will work the lat where it has best leverage, where it peaks between 60-75 degrees.

Also, as it is a single joint exercise it will improve motor unit recruitment, as in a pulldown there will be a slight degree of elbow flexion. It also has the benefit of keeping you purely in the frontal plane, making sure you are only doing shoulder adduction.
