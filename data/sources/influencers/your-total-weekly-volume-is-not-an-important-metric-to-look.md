# your total weekly volume is not an important metric to look at

Your total weekly volume does NOT matter. How should you know if you're doing enough volume?

It's commonly told that you need a certain amount of sets per muscle group in a week to grow. Is this true, is there a volume quota you need for hypertrophy?

How many sets do you need per muscle group per week?

This is false. Looking at this in a more extreme view, many people agree that doing 12 sets 1 day per week will not be good for muscle growth. They would say splitting it up throughout the week will be better. This is the importance of frequency. We know that each set in a session will have diminishing return. So how much volume should you have per week?

Volume will be dependent on frequency, with frequency being more important to have more effective/stimulating sets. Anything with a 2x or higher frequency will be sufficient, whether it is PPL, UL or FB. Doing something like 2-4 sets per muscle group split across the regions you want to train during your session will be great. Just start at a lower volume and see if you progress, adding more volume and you'll be set.

"2x weekly training frequency" means training a specific muscle group, or performing a full-body workout, two times within a seven-day period.

What should you take away from this? You can grow with "lower/moderate" volumes if you have good frequency. The recommendations are just a baseline. Do as what you can recover and progress the fastest from. Don't stress about getting a certain amount of weekly volume.
