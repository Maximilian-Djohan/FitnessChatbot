# Analyzing Jeremy Ethiers Full Body Day

Analyzing Jeremy Ethier's "Perfect" Full Body Workout

starting off is the incline dumbbell press, to cover your chest, shoulders and triceps. ignoring his incline recommendations and his recommendation for women to be in the 10-15 rep range its an effective exercise that you can continuously progress on. he also performs it more flared out which means it will be more of an mid/lower pec biased exercise as it lacks shoulder flexion for the upper pec bias (will still be trained) but is overall effective.

next he picks the barbell squat as an overall leg exercise for the quads, adductors, erectors and glutes. this will be very effective for the quads, adductor magnus and glutes as you are training both knee extension and hip extension. but unless you have a particular enjoyment in the exercise I would recommend a machine squat pattern. he also states that you can use a goblet squat if youre a beginner but there is no need to perform a worse harder to load/more unstable exercise just because you are a beginner.

next he picks an exercise for the back specifically the lats. this is a great exercise for the lats is relatively stable and has a good progression using a weight belt.

next he picks an RDL for the glutes and hamstrings. this will be a great exercise and everyone should have a hinge to train the biarticular hamstring muscles that cross the hip. it will also be a decent choice for the glutes.

for the upper back muscle the traps and rhomboids he recommends a cable wide grip row. while you are training scapular retraction the exercise choice is average at best. lacking a chest support will be a big limiting factor as you will have to rely on your erectors to stabilize the torso. you should pick any chest supported variation over this even if its dumbbells with a bench.

for his last exercise he recommends a shoulder super set via a dumbbell lateral raise and rear delt fly. the lateral raise is a great stable option for the side delt. ignoring the fact that its a super set which provides no benefit besides saving time the rear delt dumbbell fly will be decent though how he performs it will still be primarily a side delt exercise as he has the same arm path.

before we get into his bonus customizable exercise choices lets go over what he missed. there is no bicep exercise, no exercise to target the longhead of the tricep, no exercise to train, one part of the hamstring that only performs knee flexion, the rectus femoris of the quad that only performs knee extension, and lastly the front delt. but lets see if any of his bonus exercises cover these.

starting off he has the dead bug for the core which trains anything but any of the core muscles functions of torso rotation/flexion instead you are just flailing your arms around. after this he has a few mediocre recommendations. ignoring the misleading advice and some of his interesting workout choices its a decent workout that trains most of the bigger muscle groups to an extent but misses a lot.
