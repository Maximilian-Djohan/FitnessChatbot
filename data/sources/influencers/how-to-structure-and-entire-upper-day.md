# How to structure and entire upper day

How to "optimally" structure an entire upper day

first we want to identify which muscle groups we want to train. then we train their main function. we do this by training their primary joint action.

PRIMARY MUSCLE GROUP JOINT ACTIONS
Chest: Horizontal adduction, Shoulder flexion
Lats: Shoulder adduction, Shoulder extension
Upper Back: Scapular retraction
Front/Side Delt: Shoulder flexion, Shoulder abduction
Biceps/arms: Elbow flexion
Triceps: Elbow extension
Rear Delt: Shoulder extension, Horizontal abduction
Quads: Knee extension
Hamstrings: Hip extension, Knee flexion
Add/abductors: Adduction/abduction
Glutes: Hip extension
Calf: Plantar flexion
Abs: Trunk flexion

the chest has the functions of horizontal adduction and shoulder flexion. you can train these through presses or flys.

the back has the functions of shoulder adduction and shoulder extension. you can train this through a frontal pull and a sagittal pull.

the upper back has the the function of scapular retraction you can train this through an kelso shrug or upper back row.

the front and side delt have the function of shoulder abduction you can train this through a shoulder press and lateral raise.

the elbow flexors consisting of the biceps, brachialis and brachioradialis have the function of elbow flexion. you can train this through a curl variation (supinated for the biceps pronated for the brachioradialis.)

the triceps have the function of elbow extension. you can train this through an pushdown/extension.
