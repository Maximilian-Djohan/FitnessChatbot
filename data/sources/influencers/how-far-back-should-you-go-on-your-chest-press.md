# How far back should you go on your chest press?

How far back should you go back on your chest press?

many people think you need to go as deep as you can on your chest press to maximize chest growth but this isnt the case. so how far back should you go on your chest press?

through your chest press you are training horizontal adduction. if you look at the chests leverage during this it falls off after 0 degrees adduction (arms to your side). we also know that are strongest towards the mid/lengthened portion of the rep from 30-70 degrees. so when you go that far back its unessary as well as detrimental.

when you do go back that far you are just creating risk of injury as well as putting strain on your shoulders. you will also incur more muscle damage training the chest at length. but some might argue SMH adaptations but if you care about that you can just stretch after your workout.

so to train your chest in any exercise you should be going until your arms are by your side and youll be perfect
