# What is better a machine press or standing low to high fly?

What is better for the upper chest, a Machine Press or a Standing Low to High Fly?

Some people ask whether a machine press or standing low to high fly is better. This is because something like a seated low to high fly will be the "best" exercise for the upper chest due to it being single joint (no tricep). It requires setup unlike a standing variant. So which would be better?

Well, given the machine press and standing fly, I would say the machine press is better even though it has more musculature involved. This is because the instability of the weight pulling you back will be too much to effectively progress it.

Some might say you can use a single arm variant to get over this as there is less total weight to stabilize against. But since you've taken away the counter force, you instead have added lateral/rotational stability now pulling you to that specific side.

So unless you have a seated low to high fly setup, I will recommend you just do an incline press. It will be much more stable and will have a better progressive runway.
