# Why are some peoples maintenance calories higher than others?

Why are some peoples maintenance calories higher than others? Why can men eat more than women without gaining weight?

sometimes people say its unfair that someone can maintain or be in a deficit on calories that would put them into a surplus. this concept is straightforward its just due to their build and lifestyle. but what are the actual factors that contribute to this?

there are many different factors that contribute to this but the main factors are just off comparing builds. someone bigger/taller will have a higher maintenance. whether comparing a baby to the average person or a person to a bodybuilder. genetics, hormones, age will all affect this but to a smaller degree / still something you can work around even if it hinders you.

1. Muscle Mass
Why it matters: Muscle burns more calories at rest than fat does.
Example: A muscular person will have a higher resting metabolic rate (RMR), meaning they burn more calories even when not active.

2. Body Size (Height & Weight)
Why it matters: Larger people (taller, heavier) generally need more calories just to keep their bodies functioning.
Example: Someone who is 6'2" and 200 lbs will likely burn more calories than someone who is 5'5" and 130 lbs.

3. Gender
Why it matters: Males usually have more muscle mass and less body fat than females, on average.
Example: A male and female of the same weight and height may have different calorie needs because of differences in body composition.

4. Diet Composition
Why it matters: Protein has a higher thermic effect (it takes more energy to digest).
Example: A diet high in protein can slightly raise maintenance calories compared to a high-fat or high carb diet.
