# Should you train to failure? And how do you know you've reached failure?

Should you train to failure? And how do you know if you have reached failure?

you hit failure on an exercise when you hit your maximum tolerable perceived effort as your body prevents you from producing any additional effort. we can tell that you are reaching failure based on a slow contraction velocity (each rep gets slower) until you hit that failure point. but should you train to failure?

Muscular failure is reached when the maximum tolerable level of perceived effort is reached, since this prevents further increases in effort, and therefore stops further increases in the central motor command that lead to further motor unit recruitment. In this way, afferent feedback limits motor unit recruitment levels at muscular failure.

its important to train close to failure as the last 5 reps in a set are stimulating. but for practical application if you are a beginner its good to train to failure as you wont be able to gauge your rir. but if you can you should train to 0-2 rir. (getting almost all of the stimulus benefit while avoiding the most fatiguing parts of the set)

In strength training, "RIR" stands for "Reps in Reserve." It refers to the number of repetitions you could potentially perform with proper form after completing a set, before reaching failure.

Strength training volume can probably be best measured by recording the number of reps done with full motor unit recruitment and at slow bar speeds.
