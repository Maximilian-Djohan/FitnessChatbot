# How to Structure Push Pull Legs

How to structure your workout split (push pull legs edition)

The first thing you should do is make sure youre working out all of the main muscle groups. You do this by performing the joint actions of each muscle.

PRIMARY MUSCLE GROUP JOINT ACTIONS
Chest: Horizontal adduction, Shoulder flexion
Lats: Shoulder adduction, Shoulder extension
Upper Back: Scapular retraction
Front/Side Delt: Shoulder flexion, Shoulder abduction
Biceps/arms: Elbow flexion
Triceps: Elbow extension
Rear Delt: Shoulder extension, Horizontal abduction
Quads: Knee extension
Hamstring: Hip extension, Knee flexion
Add/abductors: Adduction/abduction
Glutes: Hip extension
Calf: Plantar flexion
Abs: Trunk flexion

next thing to keep in mind is exercise selection. you should order your exercises based on priority to bring up any weak points. similar to upper lower people say arms lack on ppl as they are often hit after chest/back but if thats your weak point just do them first

(if no weak points run your split for a few months until then then change exercise order to fix it)

next is to decide how much volume you should do. you should do 2 exercises per muscle group with 2 sets per exercise. with weak points at the start for priority. also use unilateral sets to further prioritize your weak points

(do what you can recover from everyone is different)

Example Push Day

chest - 2x low to high fly 2x fly

front/side delt - 2x shoulder press 2x lateral raise

triceps - 2x pushdown 2x overhead extension

(fly instead of press to not hinder triceps/delts but press if you want)

Example Pull Day

lats - 2x wide grip pulldown 2x sagittal plane row

upper back - 2x upper back row

biceps/arms - 2x descending preacher curl 2x reverse curl (free weight)

rear delt - (both rows will be good)

Example Leg Day

quads - 2x leg extension 2x leg press

hamstring - 2x hyperextension 2x hamstring curl

add/abductor - 2x adductor 1x abductor
glutes - 2x hip thrust
calf - 2x straight knee calf variation

abs - 2x crunch variati
