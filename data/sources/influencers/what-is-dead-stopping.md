# What is dead stopping?

What is deadstopping? should you do it? can it boost muscle

deadstopping is where at the end of each rep you come to a complete stop. this can be letting the cable go all the way back up on your tricep extension, letting the arms on your shoulder/chest press come to a complete stop at the bottom of your rep or locking out completely on your preacher curl. but is there any benefit in doing this?

another misconception is that after you finish the concentric you just drop the weight down with no control. to dead stop you can control the eccentric as deadstopping just means you are going all the way back to the starting point. but then why do people do a quick eccentric. the eccentric won't provide good muscle growth as you will have less motor unit recruitment during it and also it causes more muscle damage.

dead stopping on exercises can help standardize your form as you are starting each rep from the same point and it can stop someone from using momentum. it can also help manipulate an exercise's ROM to make it better fit a muscle's strength profile/leverages. and lower risk of injury as you won't be relying on stretch reflex to help move the weight.

"Deadstopping" in weightlifting refers to a technique where you pause and rest the weight completely on the floor or a support between repetitions, eliminating momentum and forcing the muscles to work harder from a dead stop.

Yes, the stretch reflex can help you move more weight, especially during dynamic movements. When a muscle is stretched quickly, the stretch reflex causes it to contract more forcefully, allowing for greater power output and the ability to lift heavier weights.

so should you deadstop? it's up to you. if you want to use it to help standardize your reps go for it. and you don't need to drop the weight each rep to do so.
