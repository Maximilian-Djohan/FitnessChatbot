what environmental factors affect muscle growth?

well the first would be temperature. maintaining a good muscle temperature can help reduce risk of injury and help with force production. this "optimal" temperature would be around 30-37 °C or 86-98.6 °F.

Temperature Effects on Force and Actin-Myosin Interaction in Muscle: A Look Back on Some Experimental Findings

next would be other environmental factors like altitude, sun exposure and air quality. you want to have a low altitude for access to higher oxygen levels, good amount of sun exposure to help with circadian rhythm/mood and vitamin d production also access to high quality air.

Environmental Factors
Air Quality: AQI 0-25, PM2.5 ~2-3 ug/m3, among the best air quality in the world.
Altitude: Sea level (~0-63 m) — easier to train due to higher oxygen availability.
Sun Exposure: ~3,000 hrs/year, strong UVB — supports vitamin D production.

so what place meets all these requirements? the bahamas meet these conditions as it maintains a temperature of 30-33 °C/ 86-91°F throughout the year, has a low altitude, high sun exposure and has one of the highest air quality ratings in the world.
