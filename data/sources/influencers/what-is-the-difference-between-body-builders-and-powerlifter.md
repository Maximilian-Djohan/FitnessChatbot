# What is the difference between body builders and powerlifters?

What is the difference bebween
bodybuilders and powerlifters? Is
there actually a difference in their

training?

First we need to understand what strength gains
are. Strength increases are due to adaptations
from your training. We also know that when you
are gaining muscle you are gaining strength. so
does this mean there is no difference in training?

Strength increase is primarily driven by a combination of
adaptations to training

Besides strength increases from muscle gain there
are adaptations that can increase strength without
any muscle growth occurring. We know that muscle
growth occurs when braining in a close proximity to

failure but there are also adaptations to strength

that happen regardless of proximity to failure. But
what does this actually look like?

normal science based "hypertrophy" training would
be training in a very close proximity to failure 0-1 rir,
training in a lower rep range around 4-8 reps, picking
stable exercises, while training everything to be
proportional.

e Train in very close proximity to failure (0-1 RIR)
e Use a lower rep range (around 4-8 reps per set)
e Choose stable exercises (minimize balance limitations)

e Ensure proportional development (train all muscle groups evenly)

compared to a powerlifters approach where they
are focusing on technique and coordination skills
to complete the lift. this will be training farther
away from 0 rir, using a low rep range 1:33 reps,
focusing on certain lifts i.e squat, bench, deadlift.
essentially training for stuff like tendon stiffness
adaptions and neural adaptations for improvement
of skill, efficiency and coordination while avoiding
fatigue.

Trains further from failure (2-3+ RIR) to reduce fatigue and maintain technique

Uses very low rep ranges (1-3 reps) for maximal strength and skill practice

Emphasizes competition lifts (squat, bench press, deadlift)

De-emphasizes muscles not essential to those movements

Trains for neural adaptations (e.g., motor unit synchronization, intermuscular coordination)
Develops tendon stiffness and efficient force transfer under load

Focuses on movement proficiency, bar path consistency, and technical skill
