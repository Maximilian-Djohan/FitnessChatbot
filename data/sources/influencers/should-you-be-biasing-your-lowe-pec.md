# should you be biasing your lower pec?

Should you be isolating your lower pec? Should you be implementing a dumbbell pullover to do so?

recently in the SBL community people have been talking about going out of your way to bias the lower pec. should you do this? and if so how would you do this?

recently popularized by kashi is the look of some golden age body builders that have a "protruding" or "hanging" low pec. if you wanted to chase this look you'd want your training to reflect this. but is it worth it? first are you willing to implement a specific exercise for this that you can do indefinitely? this is important as if you drop the exercise you will be letting those specific fibers atrophy. if your answer is yes and you want to chase this how can you do so?

while your normal mid chest biased exercise like a flat press or fly would train it you could train it better by specifically implementing an exercise for it. the entire chest performs horizontal adduction but the costal region of the chest will also be trained in shoulder extension/adduction. also seeing how the fibers run more vertical we want to keep that in mind when choosing the exercise.

shoulder extension & adduction:

well what would this look like? if you were to train horizontal adduction you can do something like a high to low fly or a very flared dip though other muscles like the lats can be active during these. but a more practical/easy to implement would be a dumbbell pullover which trains shoulder extension with a descending resistance profile where the lats can't contribute well.

so should this be something you implement? your training should be specific to your goals. so if you want to have this look then go for it and stick to it. and yes you should always have a horizontal adduction movement for your chest regardless of what you choose to do.
