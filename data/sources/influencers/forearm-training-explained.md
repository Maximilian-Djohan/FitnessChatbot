Forearm Training Explained

(dont actually read this)

Your forearm is made up of a ton of muscles with the main forearm muscles Flexor Carpi Radialis, Flexor Carpi Ulnaris, Palmaris Longus, Extensor Carpi Radialis Longus, Extensor Carpi Radialis Brevis, Extensor Carpi Ulnaris, Supinator, Pronator Teres, Pronator Quadratus, Flexor Digitorum Superficialis, Flexor Digitorum Profundus, Extensor Digitorum Communis and Brachioradialis

Your forearm has many different functions being:

What does this look like?

You can train these by performing the movement with a cable and bands. I dont recommend training all of these. Instead if you want to bring up your forearms you should just train wrist flexion and elbow flexion (brachioradialis biased) as those muscles cover the most area.

To train wrist flexion you can do a simple forearm supported wrist curl

to train your brachioradialis you can do an ascending resistance profile curl variation while being pronated like a reverse curl
