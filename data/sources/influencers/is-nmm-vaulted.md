# is NMM vaulted?

Is neuromechanical matching vaulted? Are muscle leverages useless?

Elbow flexor moment arm lengths increase with increasing elbow flexion and are maximal in the contracted position. The brachioradialis has very poor leverage when lengthened and the best leverage when shortened.

Neuromechanical Matching is the concept where you structure your exercise aligned with where a muscle has best leverage compared to other muscles that will be active. The idea behind this is that the brain is limited in recruitment so it will try to be efficient by recruiting the muscles which have best relative leverage to perform the exercise.

Neuromechanical matching is a principle suggesting the nervous system activates muscles in proportion to their mechanical advantage (leverage) for a given movement

some people say that NMM isnt absolute and there are other factors that determine what is being trained. and this is true. we can see this through shoulder extension where the rear delt has better leverage than the lats in shoulder extension. but we call shoulder extension exercises like a pullover or close grip row a lat exercise but we do acknowledge that the rear delt does get worked well.

PCSA stands for Physiological Cross-Sectional Area, which is a measure of the total cross-sectional area of all muscle fibers, perpendicular to the fibers themselves.

NMM isnt the only thing that will determine what muscle is contributing. but you shouldnt disregard it. i.e in elbow flexion the brachialis is the muscle with the largest cross sectional area but that doesnt mean we should just pick any random curl because they will all train the brachialis. we use NMM to try to advantage or disadvantage muscles further to better train them.

so NMM is not "vaulted" it is something you use in your training to better train a certain muscle through its joint action. you shouldnt claim that a muscle will only be trained because of leverage you are just trying to better train it by aligning it with what we know. yes there are other factors that determine a muscles contribution but these arent things that should conflict with NMM rather be something you consider alongside it.
