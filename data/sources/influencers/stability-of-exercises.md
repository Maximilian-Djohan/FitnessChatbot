# Stability of exercises

Would you get picked if you were stable? Does stability lead to more muscle growth?

when choosing exercises there is the option between doing free-weights, cables or machine versions of an exercise. some people say that machines are better because they are more stable but is this actually true?

Free weights and machines offer different benefits in strength training. Free weights, like dumbbells and barbells, allow for a wider range of motion and more natural movement patterns. Machines, on the other hand, provide a fixed path of motion and can be more stable.

machine variants where there isnt a stability demand will lead to more motor unit recruitment in the given muscle which will lead to more growth. a clear example can be shown comparing a chest supported row vs a barbell row. during a barbell row their will be a lot more stress on your erectors and even legs which takes away from the lats/upper back.

machines also allow for better standardization of form which makes tracking progress easier.
