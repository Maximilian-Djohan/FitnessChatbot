# Science Based Skincare

Science Based Skincare

to start off this will be a very general skincare guide as skincare will be very person dependent which varies on age/genetics and lifestyle factors. it will go through what most people can follow and the reasoning behind each part of it.

Skincare refers to the practices and products used to maintain and improve the health and appearance of the skin. It involves cleansing, moisturizing,

Cleanser AM/PM:

starting off your routine will be your cleanser. this has the purpose of removing any dirt/oil and buildup on your skin. you can use something that has gentler ingredients which you can base it off the reactions it has with your skin. if you struggle with acne you want to make sure it has salicylic acid if you dont struggle with acne alongside your normal cleanser you can use something with salicylic acid periodically throughout the week.

A cleanser is a skincare product designed to remove dirt, oil, makeup, and impurities from the skin. It is an essential first step in any skincare routine.

Toner (Optional) AM/PM:

next in some routines is a toner this isn't always needed but it can be used to further exfoliate your skin after your cleanser or to hydrate if you struggle with dry skin. but in most cases this won't be needed if your other products are good enough/if you have average skin.

Facial toner is a skincare product, typically a liquid, that's applied to the skin after cleansing. It helps to remove any remaining traces of cleanser, dirt, or makeup, and can also balance the skin's pH and hydrate it.

Serum AM:

your serums are also optional but it can be used to help protect the skin or help boost/brighten the skin. this is where something like a vitamin c serum would go. things to avoid would be products that have clear bottles as the product will lose effectiveness with light exposure or low dosed products which are essentially just water.

Serums are concentrated skincare products designed to target specific skin concerns with a high concentration of active ingredients.

Treatment PM:

treatments are also optional but can be used to "fix problems". this can be used to help with acne, skin quality/anti aging, hyperpigmentation, and texture. this would be things like retinoids or something like niacinamide or other acids. you want to avoid jumping to a high concentrations or mixing multiple treatments as they can have a bad reaction on your skin.

Skincare treatments encompass a range of procedures designed to address various skin concerns and improve overall skin health.

Moisturizer AM/PM:

your moisturizer is one of the last things in the routine. it will have the purpose to hydrate, improve the skin barrier and lock in moisture. you want to avoid having fragrances and alcohol in them to avoid skin irritation and dryness.

Moisturizer is a crucial part of any skincare routine, serving to hydrate the skin, restore its natural protective barrier, and improve its overall health and appearance.

Sunscreen AM:

your sunscreen has the purpose to shield against UVA and UVB rays to prevent aging and skin cancer. you want to get an SPF 50 sunscreen either mineral or chemical will be good for this.

Sunscreen is a crucial part of any skincare routine because it protects the skin from the harmful effects of ultraviolet (UV) radiation, preventing sunburn, premature aging, and skin cancer.

as an overview things you want in your morning routine are a cleanser, optionally a toner, serum, moisturizer, and sunscreen. for your night routine you want to have a cleanser, optionally a toner, treatment and moisturizer. and keep in mind to avoid things that will cause skin irritation and/or dryness like fragrances or alcohol.
