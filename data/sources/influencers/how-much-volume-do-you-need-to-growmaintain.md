# How much volume do you need to grow/maintain?

How much volume/sets do you need to grow? How much volume does it take to maintain?

first we need to understand what role our volume plays in relation to our training. working out causes stimulus which causes our muscle to grow. after training the stimulus will last ~33 hours. and we know a muscle is either growing or atrophying so after this stimulus ends it will be under atrophy. so we need to have enough volume that the growth of the stimulus will outweigh atrophy rates.

Muscle fibers grow in the post-workout stimulus period and then shrink in the later periods of time outside of those post-workout stimulus periods. They do not stay the same size for the whole week during maintenance.

and this is simple as we have data for this. to maintain muscle you need to 3 sets once a week. what about if you train twice a week how much volume do you need? if you train at least twice a week just 1 set will be enough to grow. so doing more than 3 sets per week or at least 1 set twice week will be enough to progress.

(frequency > volume)

Non-linear dose-response relationships of hypertrophy to workout volume vary substantially between meta-analyses, perhaps due to inclusion criteria.

but when programming you should aim higher than maintaining and obviously train with splits that have higher frequencies. and keep in mind not everyone can recover from the same amount of volume. so test volumes and see the higher end of volume you can recover and in turn progress from.

(start low and add sets with time to test)
