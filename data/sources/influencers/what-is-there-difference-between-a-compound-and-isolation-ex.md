# What is there difference between a compound and isolation exercises?

What is the difference between a
"compound" and "isolation" exercise.
what is bebter for muscle growth?

many people think a compound exercises are popular
exercises like bench and squat. in reality we determine
what is a compound by seeing what the exercise is
training. if it has more than one joint action it is an
compound while isolations you are training one.

(an example of this is bench having horizontal adduction/elbow extension vs a fly will have just
horizontal adduction)

what are the benefits of either? doing compounds
will be more time efficient, help you practice exercise
execution on that movement and reduce total
session volume. while comparing it to an "isolation"
they will have more motor unit recruitment towards
the given muscle as you wont have other muscles
involved. so what is better?

it depends on your goals. if you like doing compounds
like pressing go for it. if you struggle with too much
volume compounds can help with that (e.g. incline
press for chest and shoulders). if you have a weak
point that you want to bring up do an isolation. if you
really want to prioritize something do an isolation. if
you feel other muscles are limiting you during an
exercise do an isolation.
