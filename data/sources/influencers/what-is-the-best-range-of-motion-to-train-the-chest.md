# What is the best range of motion to train the chest?

What is the best range of motion to train the chest?

to train the clavicular head of the chest (upper pec) you want to perform shoulder flexion in an incline press or low to high fly from 20->120 degrees (with peak tension around 40-90 degrees)

why do we train that range? the upper pec has better relative leverage compared to the front delt (another shoulder flexor) as the front delt has better leverage at the very start of the movement as well as contributing a lot towards the end of that movement

The internal moment arm lengths of the shoulder flexors change relative to one another with increasing shoulder elevation angle

To train the sternal/costal head of the chest (mid/lower pec) you want to perform horizontal adduction/shoulder flexion with a flat press or fly with a full rom with your hands by your side till they are in front of you (peak tension in the mid/lengthened range)

why do we train that range? the mid/lower chest has good leverage throughout horizontal adduction and not limiting rom helps keep standardization.
