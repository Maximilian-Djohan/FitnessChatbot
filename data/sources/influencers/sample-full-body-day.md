# Sample Full Body Day

Sample Full Body Day

Exercises & Alternatives

To effectively structure an full body day we want to perform all the joint actions of the muscles and muscle regions we want to train. so what would this look like?

for the chest starting off with your main movement training horizontal adduction (bringing upper arm to midline) can be either any flat press or pec dec/fly. then a secondary movement training shoulder flexion will be an incline press or a low to high fly.

for your back starting with the lats is vertical adduction (bring upper arm down by side) is a pulldown. for shoulder extension (bringing arm back) is a close grip row or pullover. and for the upper back scapular retraction through a kelso shrug or upper back row.

for the shoulder you want to train shoulder abduction (bringing upper arm up to the side). for the front delts this can be through a shoulder press machine/variant. and for the side delt you can do any lateral raise variation I'd recommend machine or cable.

for the biceps you just want to pick any form of elbow flexion. for the biceps I'd recommend any variant where you are supinated like a preacher curl or recline curl. and alongside this if you want you can pick a variation where you are more pronated like a reverse curl for the brachioradialis.

for the triceps you just need to extend the elbow. you can do this through any pushdown variation. and if you want to add another exercise alongside you can do something like a JM press or dip.

Starting off with the quads is knee extension. the most important exercise for this is the leg extension training the entirety of the quad. then alongside this you can also have a squat pattern if you'd like.

for the hamstrings your main exercise will be knee flexion training the entirety of the hamstrings. this can be through a seated or lying hamstring curl. additionally you can also train hip extension through a hinge such as an SLDL/RDL or hyper extension.

next is your add/abductors. you can train this via adduction and abduction on the add/abductor machine. and if really needed you can even do this same motion on cables.

for your glutes your main exercise should be hip extension. this will be best done through a hip thrust. if you don't have access to this then something like an RDL can be sufficient.

lastly for the calves you want to perform plantar flexion (toes down) ideally with a straight knee. this calf raise can be done on a specific machine or on a leg press machine or any other straight leg variation.
