# How does grip width affect lat pulldowns?

How does grip width affect lat pulldowns. Is one better than the other for lat growth?

We know that to train the lats we want to perform their main functions of shoulder adduction and shoulder extension. this happens in both variations but recently wider grips for pulldowns have been popularized for lat growth but does this actually matter?

well we first have to look at what is the actual difference is between both variations. looking at the close grip pulldown as your arms come down they are in the sagittal plane (infront of you) and in a wide grip pulldown they are in the frontal plane (besides you). but what does this actually mean for the lats?

Frontal Sagittal

when you look at the arm path we can with a close grip pulldown pulling in the sagittal plane you are actually training shoulder extension (upper lats bias) while you are in the frontal plane you are training shoulder adduction (upper lats bias). so how should this affect your lat training?

you should consider them as two different exercises as they train different functions. the close grip pulldown is more similar to a close grip row. so when programming lats into your session you should have both functions whether it be a wide grip and close grip pulldown or a wide grip pulldown and a close grip row. but yes you will train the entire lat no matter what you choose
