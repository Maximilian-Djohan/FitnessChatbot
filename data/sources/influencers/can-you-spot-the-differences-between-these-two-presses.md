# can you spot the differences between these two presses?

can you spot the differences between these two pressing exercises? which is better for muscle growth?

at first glance these two chest presses might appear the same. they both train horizontal adduction. with the only difference with it being how its loaded? does any of this make a difference?

with the way the plate loaded press is set up it has an ascending resistance profile. this means it is hardest at the end of the movement and easiest at the start. when training the chest we want a descending resistance profile where it is harder by that lengthened to mid range and easiest at the end. but why is this and why does this matter?

chest pressing is a multi joint movement. having horizontal adduction (chest) and elbow extension (tricep). the resistance profile matters as the chest has better leverage at the start of the movement while the tricep will have better leverage towards the end of the movement. so when we have an ascending resistance profile where its hardest at the end it will essentially become a tricep press.

Pectoralis major and anterior deltoid both contribute to horizontal flexion (as in the pec deck) but the posterior deltoid is the main contributor to horizontal extension. However, there are differences between regions. Triceps brachii moment arm lengths change with elbow joint angle, increasing towards full elbow extension.
