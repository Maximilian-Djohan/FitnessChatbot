# Does the hamstring get worked in a squat pattern?

Does the hamstring get trained in a squat and/or a leg press?

some people say depending on foot placement. you can change your squat from primarily quads to also train the hamstring. we know that the hamstring has two functions to extend the hip and flex the knee which are both present in a squat pattern. does this mean that a squat pattern will train the hamstring?

no. the hamstring will not get effectively trained. this is due to antagonistic inhibition as the hamstring is a biarticular crossing both the hip and knee joint. so during a squat pattern it will be shortening at one joint and lengthening at the other so it wont be able to produce force to get trained

Antagonistic inhibition, in the context of muscle physiology, refers to the automatic relaxation or lengthening of one muscle (the antagonist) when the other muscle in a pair (the agonist) contracts.
