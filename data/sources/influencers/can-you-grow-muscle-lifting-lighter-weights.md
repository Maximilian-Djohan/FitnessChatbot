# Can you grow muscle lifting lighter weights?

Can you grow muscle just curling the 30s? And is it beneficial to use a lower weight?

some people say since the biceps are a small muscle and you dont need heavy weight to grow them. they also use the fact that in some instances you can see popular bodybuilders or just bigger individuals doing this while still having big arms. but is this true? should you be curling lighter weight

in reality the most important thing is training within proximity to failure with high degrees of effort. no matter what rep range you use the last ~5 reps to failure are the most stimulating no matter how many total reps you do in the set. We also know that you can have high degrees of effort regardless of rep range when the weight is challenging and taken to failure. so what should you do?

Strength training volume can probably be best measured by recording the number of reps done with full motor unit recruitment and at slow bar speeds

you should train to failure or close to failure (0-2 RIR) with a challenging weight. while you can do anywhere from 5-30 reps i would recommend to staying from 4-8 reps or even 4-12 reps if you really prefer. staying to lower rep ranges can help with any fatigue buildup or cardiovascular demand that might occur with higher reps.
