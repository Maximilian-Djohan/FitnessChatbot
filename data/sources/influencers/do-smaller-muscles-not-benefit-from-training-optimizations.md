Do smaller muscles not benefit from NMM and unilateral training? What is the voluntary activation deficit?

what is the voluntary activation deficit? voluntary activation is the level of recruitment in a given muscle. the deficit is where you cant fully recruit a certain muscle group. but what does this have to do with neuromechanical matching and unilateral training?

A voluntary activation deficit is the inability of the central nervous system to fully recruit and activate a muscle's motor units during a maximal voluntary contraction

what is the purpose of NMM and unilateral training? it is to increase recruitment of a given muscle compared to other contributing muscles. this concept is used as your central motor command will be limited to how much it can recruit. so we use them in training to increase recruitment in certain muscles to overcome this and be more efficient. so what does this have to do with voluntary activation?

Motor unit recruitment is a measure of how many motor neurons are activated in a particular muscle, and therefore is a measure of how many muscle fibers of that muscle are activated.
