# is range of motion vaulted?

Is range of motion vaulted? Does it result in less muscle growth?

the importance of range of motion is highly emphasized in resistance training with many people claiming that there are various benefits such as more muscle growth or decreased risk of injury. but is this actually true?

Range of motion (ROM) refers to the extent of movement a joint can achieve. It's a measure of how far you can move a joint in different directions

in reality while range of motion is important you dont need to train in what would normally be called a "full range of motion". you should train a muscle where it has best leverage to perform. This concept of neuromechanical matching is to train a muscle where it has best leverage rather than "full ranges" as sometimes you arent training the muscle you think you are. what would this look like?

Neuromechanical matching is a principle in human movement where the central nervous system directs motor commands to muscle units based on their mechanical advantage.

this can be seen in a shoulder press. we know that the side delt and front delt perform shoulder abduction (bringing arm up at the side). but below 90 degrees, even in a shoulder press the side delt will primarily be trained up until above 90 degrees where the front delt takes over.

so when doing an exercise you should train the muscle where it has good leverage rather than doing an arbitrarily decided "full" rom. but this also doesnt mean you need to just do partial reps for all muscle groups, you can also adjust the resistance profile of an exercise to match where the muscle has leverage such as a descending preacher curl for the biceps.
