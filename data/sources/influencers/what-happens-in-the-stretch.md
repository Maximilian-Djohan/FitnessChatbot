# What happens in the stretch

What actually happens in the
stretch? Is it actually beneficial
for muscle growth?

“We dont actually know"

Doing a "stretched" position exercise is to benefit
from stretch mediated hypertrophy. SMH happens
through a process called sarcomerogenesis. This
increases passive tension in the muscle promoting
growth so doesnt this mean it actually has a
benefit?

Sarcomerogenesis is the process of adding new sarcom-
eres in series within a muscle fiber, effectively increasing the
muscle's overall length.

SMH is "temporary" while you can benefit from it and
maintain it you can lose it within a few weeks of nob
maintaining it. SMH is easy to gain and lose and has
been overblown for its benefit. it is not something
that will always be "increasing" gains.

We know that we should train muscles where they have
best leverage for optimal muscle growth. (nueromechanical
matching. This conflicts with SMH in some cases. With
some muscles that best leverage at a shorter range so you
would be sacrificing long term gains for an adaptation.
another thing to also keep in mind is that you inflicts more
muscle damage at longer lengths.

Eccentric training to a long maximum muscle produces more post-workout fatigue
and muscle soreness than the same training to a short maximum muscle length.
