# Science Based Glute Training

Science Based Glute Training

The glute is made up three main gluteal muscles. the main function that the biggest glute muscle (glute max) performs is hip extension while they all (max,med,min) also contribute to hip abduction.

another thing you can optionally add is working your glutes at a longer length through a "deep" hinge for stretch mediated hypertrophy through an RDL/SLDL or through stretching. but as long as you have a hinge you will have this covered.

the first way to better train the glute is to consider antagonistic inhibition. while the glute doesn't suffer from this the hamstring which also contributes to hip extension does. to isolate the glute you can pick an exercise that has an antagonistic joint torque to the knee to "shut off" the hamstring allowing you to isolate the glute this would look doing a bent knee exercise. (I'd opt for a thrust over a RDL as the RDL will still have the hamstrings active alongside your erectors)

the next thing we can look at is how to "optimize" hip abduction. you can perform abduction multiple times once leaned forward to better train the upper glute maximus and once leaned back to better train the medius/minimus.

so for glutes to train hip extension you should ideally be performing a hip thrust then a hip abductor exercise leaned forward/backward, and optionally a lengthened challenge through a hinge or indirectly through your hamstring hinge.
