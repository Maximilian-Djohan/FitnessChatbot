# Why do people do a wide grip pulldown?

Why do people do a wide grip pulldown for the lats? Does going wider actually help with lat growth?

in a lat pulldown you are training the lats function of shoulder adduction. this happens whether you take a wider or a closer grip so why do people say a wider grip is better for the lats?

well what's changing between the different grip widths? the difference is how high your upper arm goes through shoulder ab/adduction. when you are wider your upper arm will be limited on how high it can go while on a closer grip you are able to go higher. so how does this affect the lats?

we can look at the leverage of muscles responsible for shoulder adduction. when you have a closer grip you are going higher up in adduction where your lats will lose leverage to your chest. then when you are using a wider grip you are limiting how high your arms can go which means you are avoiding the range where your chest gains leverage in adduction.

Latissimus dorsi muscle activation during maximal voluntary isometric contractions decreases with increasing shoulder elevation angle in both vertical (pulldown) and horizontal (pullover) exercises, but sternocostal pectoralis major activation increases.

so what does this mean? the "stretch" part of your pulldown is avoided. which means your lats will stay in the range where they have better leverage to be trained cutting out any unnecessary rom.
