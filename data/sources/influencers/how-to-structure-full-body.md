# How to structure Full Body

How to structure your own Full Body Split

first is to decide what muscles and muscle regions you want to train. after deciding this look at the functions of those regions so you can train them. so what does training all the important muscle regions look like?

PRIMARY MUSCLE GROUP JOINT ACTIONS
Chest: Horizontal adduction, Shoulder flexion
Lats: Shoulder adduction, Shoulder extension
Upper Back: Scapular retraction
Front/Side Delt: Shoulder flexion, Shoulder abduction
Biceps/arms: Elbow flexion
Triceps: Elbow extension
Rear Delt: Shoulder extension, Horizontal abduction
Quads: Knee extension
Add/abductors: Adduction/abduction
Glutes: Hip extension
Calf: Plantar flexion
Abs: Trunk flexion

next is exercise order. structure the order based on what muscle groups you need to bring up the most or want to bring up the most in your split. e.g arms first if you want to prioritize your arms or chest first if you want to bring up your chest etc.

Example Full Body Day:

Chest: Flat Press or Fly + Incline Press or Low to High Fly

Lats: Wide Grip Pulldown or Flap + Sagittal Plane Row or Sagittal Flap
Upper Back: Kelso or Upper Back Row

Front Delt/Side Delt: Shoulder Press + Lateral Raise

Biceps/Elbow Flexors: Supinated Descending Curl + Pronated Ascending Curl
Triceps: Pushdown + Triceps Press or Overhead Ext

Rear Delts: Your rows will train it well

Quads: Leg Extension

Hamstrings: Hamstring Curl + Hinge

Add/Abductor: Adduction + Abduction

Glutes: Shortened Focused Hinge/Hip Thrust

Calves: Straight Leg Calf Raise (Internal+External Optionally)
Optional: Abs/Obliques/Tibialis/Neck/Forearms

lastly is volume. this will vary from person to person. you should aim to train each muscle with around 2-3 sets per session splitting it among the regions you want to train. with priorities taking more volume. a general amount would be around 15-20 sets per session. and importantly train with intensity to failure / 0 RIR.
