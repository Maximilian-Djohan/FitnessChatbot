Having a high voluntary activation and if they are made up of more type 2 fibers. so what muscles actually take the longest to recover?

Type II muscle fibers, also known as fast-twitch fibers, are characterized by their rapid contraction and generation of greater force, but fatigue more quickly than type I fibers (slow-twitch).

A high voluntary activation level in a muscle indicates that the brain is sending a strong signal to the muscle, causing it to contract with a high degree of force. This means the muscle is recruiting more motor units and firing them more frequently, leading to a stronger contraction than if the activation level were lower.
