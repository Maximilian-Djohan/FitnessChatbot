# KNEE CHECKLIST DURING QUAD EXERCISES

Checklist to Avoid Knee Pain & Strengthen your Knees during your Quad Exercises

many people complain about knee pain developing. why can it develop and how can we avoid it or train to alleviate it? well one of the main issues is via exercise execution and tempo. many people don't have eccentric control on their reps dropping the weight down excessively, not performing the exercise with a full range of motion, and not keeping their limb pathing in mind.

so what should you do? first is rom. in a hypertrophy context a full rom isn't needed. but your joints/tendons strength is specific to the range of motion you work in. so using a wider range will make you stronger in a wider range, also the use of a higher load will help to prevent any form break down. in the context of your quad training you should be going till lockout.

for tempo you don't want to launch the weight on the first rep. you want to "accelerate" into it reaching your max effort slowly up until the end of your first rep then continuing as normal. for your eccentric you should utilize a 2-3 second eccentric for every rep and for the last rep make sure you're not dropping it. this is applicable for both your squat pattern and leg extension

next is your pathing of your legs as you flex/extend the knee. many people don't consider this as they just think about getting the weight from point a to point b. but you should make sure you aren't externally/internally rotating throughout your reps (very common). to avoid this you can place your legs closer together making sure they're completely aligned. (you can even use a block to hold during your reps to actively practice this)

Medial rotation

another thing you can try if you can't do any quad exercises are isometrics on a leg extension during a specific range. you can focus on the range where you feel the most discomfort and hold the isometric there to try to help strengthen that specific joint angle.

so as a Summary controlling your reps, using a full range of motion, avoiding internal/external rotation of the knee and even possibly implementing some isometrics. but keep in mind these are only possible fixes. if you're truly having enough pain over a long period of time where it's limiting your function you should get it checked out by a professional.
