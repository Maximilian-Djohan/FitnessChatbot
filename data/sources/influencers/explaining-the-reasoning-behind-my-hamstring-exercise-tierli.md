# explaining the reasoning behind my hamstring exercise tierlist

explaining the reasoning behind my hamstring exercise tierlist

starting off to preface just because something is lower on the list does not mean its not effective for muscle growth. rather based on different criteria there will be better options if your goal is to "maximize" growth for said muscle.

starting with F tier i have the squat patterns. as a majority of the hamstring is biarticular in a squat pattern where you are moving at the knee and hip there will be a high degree of antagonistic joint torque occurring causing the hamstring to not get worked effectively. then the other exercises have suboptimal resistance profiles, stability/loading progression issues.

in C tier there are hinges which still have some degree of antagonistic joint torque at the knee but to a much lower degree compared to a squat pattern meaning the hamstring will still gets worked but less compared to a SLDL / higher exercises on the list. (I would even say that an RDL can be "effective" for the hamstrings)

in B tier i have the glute kickback. many people think of it as a glute exercise but depending on how you perform it it will train the hamstrings very well. if there is no knee bend and you have a "far" ROM it will train the hamstrings well. and most setups will have a resistance profile hardest towards the middle of the rep favoring the hamstrings. but this still lacks stability.

in A tier i have the SLDL which is one of the best overall hinges if not the best. it will effectively train the biarticular hamstring muscles and will have little to no antagonistic joint torque with a straight knee and it is very easy to progress but its lower on the list since it doesnt train the biceps femoris short head of the hamstring as its not biarticular and only gets trained in knee flexion.

next in S tier i have the standing ham curl and the rack pull. a knee flexion exercise through hamstring curl is needed to train entire hamstring though in particular this one lacks some stability/comfortability holding it back from S+. and for the rack pull in theory it would be one of the best hamstring hinges if performed as a top half SLDL as the adductor magnus will be contributing towards the bottom rom which you have cut out.

in S+ tier i have both of the main hamstring curl variations. either of theses are great choices to have as you need to train knee flexion to train the entirety of the hamstring and these are very stable/easy to progress variations.

so yes most of these exercises can be effective for growing your hamstrings but due to different criteria i ranked some exercises higher than others.
