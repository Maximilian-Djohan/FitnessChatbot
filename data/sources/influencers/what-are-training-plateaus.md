# What are training plateaus

What are training plateaus? Why do they happen and how can we fix it?

OVERCOMING A STRENGTH PLATEAU

PLATEAU

What is a plateau? It's where your progress on a said exercise slows down or stalls from session to session. But why does this happen and how can we fix this?

A training plateau is a phase where progress in strength, endurance, or muscle growth stalls despite consistent effort.

First off, you realistically aren't plateaued. After running an exercise for a long period of time you won't constantly be improving coordination adaptations. Your main source of strength gain will be from muscle growth. But muscle growth only happens grams at a time. It's normal that just a few grams of muscle do not have an expressible increase in strength to the point where you're adding reps or weight every session.

Muscle grows at a relatively slow rate. It is normal to gain only 0.5 to 1 lb of muscle per month for men (and slightly less for women), even with optimal training and diet.

So what does this mean? It's normal for your progression to "stall". This is especially the case for smaller muscles like your biceps which have a small cross sectional area. When you're only putting on grams of muscle onto your body for a muscle that is already small, it's normal for this growth to not create much of a difference in force output. But is there anything you can do to solve this?

I wouldn't rush into anything, but if you're constantly plateauing weeks and weeks on end then you should make sure your external factors are in check, i.e. dieting/sleep. After that, make sure your volume is recoverable, lowering your volume if you have to. If this still doesn't work, the implementation of some higher weight lower rep sets to work on coordination/neural adaptations can help "boost" you out of a plateau (1-3 reps w/ RIR). But your main takeaway is that growth is slow. It's fine to not be constantly progressing.
