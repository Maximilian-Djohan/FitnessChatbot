# Should you train your forearms?

Should you train your forearms? How should you effectively train the forearm?

some people say it isnt worth training the forearm as it wont grow much while some will train it regardless. the forearms are made up of a ton of muscles so how can you train the forearms and should you?

there are many multiple actions that your forearm contributes to being elbow flexion, wrist flexion/extension, finger flexion/extension and pronation/supination. out of these the main muscles to train are the wrist flexors/finger flexors as they have the greatest cross sectional area (besides elbow flexion). but how should you train these?

I would recommend some type of wrist curl where you perform finger flexion at the start of the rep. while you might think you should isolate these two functions it is unnecessary as they will have a low voluntary activation deficit meaning you will be able to effectively recruit both during the one exercise. i would also recommend you to do it with a straight arm as some of the muscles are biarticular.

for the other functions it isnt worth the time training as they wont contribute much to your forearm size. but if you like the way the top of your forearm looks you can implement wrist extension/finger extension.

overall is it worth to train your forearm? its not very important as your forearms will get good stimulus isometrically through your curl variations. but if you decide to train them a wrist curl with finger flexion will be effective. (also the brachioradialis is also important but you will be training that through your elbow flexor exercises)
