# Can you bias the lats?

Can you bias the lats? Do you need multiple exercises when many people do a sagittal pull training shoulder extension (close grip row) and a frontal pull training shoulder adduction (wide grip pulldown) but is there actually a need to do both of them? can you bias regions of the lats?

shoulder extension: shoulder adduction:

well this idea behind this is that when youre training the lats by training two different functions there will different muscle fibers worked and in turn bias different regions. e.g a close grip row will bias the upper lats and a wide grip pulldown will bias the lower lats.

well first looking at the mechanisms/theory behind this. when training either function you technically will be training the entire lat. but the idea of biasing comes with the idea of voluntary activation. the lats will experience the voluntary activation deficit meaning you wont be able to maximally recruit them. so by training a different function where they have different leverages you have the potential to train muscle fibers that werent trained in just one action.

A voluntary activation deficit is the inability of the central nervous system to fully recruit and activate a muscle's motor units during a maximal voluntary contraction.

but when looking at the outcomes of this in practice this idea based on theory/mechanisms didnt make a statistically significant difference in outcomes for the lats when looking at emg data. so what should you do?

Latissimus Dorsi: ULD had the highest activation in IR at Zero-Position, significantly higher than IR at 0°ABD (p<0.05) followed by IR at 90°ABD. The activity of LUD and combined segments (ULD+LLD) was similar across exercises.

you can technically get away with just one lat exercise especially if you are a beginner. but i would recommend still training both functions of the lats. you will be able to handle the volume of doing two exercises, also the activation of other muscles in each movement are different i.e rear delt will be trained well in shoulder extension.
