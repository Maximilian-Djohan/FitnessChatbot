# Are pyramid sets effective?

Are Pyramids sets effective for muscle growth? Should you be doing them?

PYRAMID SETS

FOR MUSCLE AND STRENGTH

there are two common ways to do a pyramid set. the first is the more straightforward way of doing it increasing weight each set until you get to your top set or a reverse pyramid where you start with a top set and work your way down each set. but is this an effective way to workout?

Pyramid sets in exercise involve gradually increasing weight while decreasing repetitions, or vice versa, within a set of exercises.

now what about a reverse pyramid? you start off with your top set but decrease weight each set. it has the same problem but the later sets will be requiring less effort. but there is somewhat of a use. if you overshoot weight on your first set and have another set planned you can drop the weight a bit to make sure you arent doing something low like 3 reps.

in a normal pyramid you increase the weight with a varying rep range. this is not better than just doing normal straight sets. instead those first sets which are lighter wont require as much effort to lift the weight resulting in less muscle fibers being recruited. its just better to do your top set. (also do your warmups but not like this)

Voluntarily increasing central motor command increases perception of effort, and the number of recruited motor units (and the number of activated muscle fibers)

Strength & Conditioning Research
Author: Chris Beardsley

so what should you do? just do your normal warmups then your top set and keep the weight the same between your sets.
