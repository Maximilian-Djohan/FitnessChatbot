how long should you rest in between sets for the most muscle growth?

we rest because of the fatigue mechanisms that occur from your set. this occurs through central and peripheral fatigue. this occurs mainly through central fatigue consisting of supraspinal cns fatigue where your brain senses unpleasant sensations which increases your perception of effort and spinal cns fatigue due to repeated motor neuron firing where they get overworked and causes a reduction in this firing/excitability. then there is peripheral fatigue through metabolite and calcium ion related fatigue.

The maximum tolerable perception of effort is the point where you stop an exercise set because the feeling of exertion becomes too great, and it is often the limiting factor for performance. Reaching this maximum perception of effort causes task failure

through all these fatigue mechanisms your perception of effort will increase during a set. this will continue to a point until you reach your maximum tolerable perception of effort where you hit failure. you then use a rest period so this fatigue will dissipate for the next set. if you dont rest long enough this fatigue will still be present and will make you reach this maximum tolerable perception of effort faster in your set causing you to fail earlier on. so how long should you be resting for?

Most strength training workouts involve multiple sets. While fatigue contributes to reaching failure, fatigue also increases over successive sets (reducing the number of reps that can be done) so long as rest periods are sufficiently short. This accumulated fatigue can involve both peripheral and central nervous system (CNS) fatigue.

there wont be a set period for rest times and it can be person to person dependent. but a general recommendation will be 3 minutes but it can be anywhere from 1-5 minutes depending on your cardiovascular endurance and the demand of the exercise. but if you want a measurable way to determine how long you should rest you can wait until your heart rate goes to baseline after your set.
