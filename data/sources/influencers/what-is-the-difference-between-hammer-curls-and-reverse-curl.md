# What is the difference between hammer curls and reverse curls?

What is the difference between reverse curls and hammer curls? What is better for muscle growth?

In both of these curl variations you are training elbow flexion with the only difference being wrist position. But how does this differing wrist position affect muscle growth?

Well, in both of these curls they will be ascending hardest higher up in the range of motion. And as they stray away from being supinated, getting more and more pronated from a neutral grip to pronated grip, they are further disadvantaging the biceps. This results in them both being brachioradialis biased exercises, so what's the difference between the two?

Well, as you become more pronated you are further disadvantaging the biceps. This as a result means the reverse curl will be better for the brachioradialis. So it will be the more "optimal option", but I would only recommend this if using a cuff as it has a high demand on wrist extension without it. And if you're unable to use a cuff a hammer curl is a good alternative as it won't have the limiting factor of wrist extension.
