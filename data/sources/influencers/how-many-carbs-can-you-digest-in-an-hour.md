# How many carbs can you digest in an hour?

How many carbs can you digest in an hour? How does this affect preworkout/intraworkout carbs?

we know that carbohydrates are important as your bodies energy source or to help maintain blood glucose levels/reduce intrasession fatigue if used intraworkout but how many carbs should you consume?

Intra-workout carbohydrates refer to carbohydrates consumed during exercise or physical activity. They provide energy to muscles and help sustain performance during prolonged or intense workouts.

normally just eating carbohydrates from a glucose source i.e rice, bread, pasta, rice, oats, or cereals you will be able to digest around 60gs of carbs in an hour. but is there anyway to increase this?

High oxidation rates from combined carbohydrates ingested during exercise

Roy LP G Jentjens, Juul Achten, Asker E Jeukendrup
PMID: 15354037 DOI: 10.1249/01.mss.0000139796.07843.1d

you can increase this to 90g of carbs. this can be done through the use of different metabolic pathways to digest both glucose (starchy carbs) and fructose (fruits/honey). so you can have ~60gs of a carb source from glucose and ~30g from fructose. so how can you apply this?

High rates of exogenous carbohydrate oxidation from a mixture of glucose and fructose ingested during prolonged cycling exercise

Roy L PG Jentjens, Asker E Jeukendrup
PMID: 15946410 DOI: 10.1079/bjn20041368

if consuming a pre workout meal you should space it around ~60 minutes before hand having a glucose and fructose source. if using intraworkout using two different sources of carbs wont matter as much as in resistance training you wont really deplete your glycogen stores to any significant degree so you can just have a glucose source.
