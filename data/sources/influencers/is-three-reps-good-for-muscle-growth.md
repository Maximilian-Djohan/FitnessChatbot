# Is three reps good for muscle growth?

Is three reps good for muscle growth?

with lower rep ranges being popular some people go even lower doing something like three reps but is this effective for muscle growth?

three rep to failure will provide enough stimulus to be considered an "effective" set for muscle growth but you will be missing out. in any rep range the last ~5 reps to failure will be the most stimulating. so when doing 3 reps you will potentially be missing out on ~2 stimulating reps.

Strength training volume can probably be best measured by recording the number of reps done with full motor unit recruitment and at slow bar speeds

Hypertrophy is the result of single muscle fibers increasing in size. Single muscle fibers are stimulated to grow once they have detected mechanical tension. The mechanical tension detected by a single muscle fiber is determined by (1) whether a fiber is activated, and (2) the speed at which the fiber shortens, because of the force-velocity relationship. Only those reps that involve a large number of fibers being activated while shortening at a slow speed will stimulate hypertrophy.

Number of reps done with full motor unit recruitment and a slow bar speed, when training to failure

Non-stimulating reps do not involve high enough motor unit recruitment to activate the high-threshold motor units that control the majority of the most responsive muscle fibers

2 This analysis assumes that full motor unit recruitment is reached on each rep of a 5RM load (85-87% of 1RM). This number (5 reps) may differ between individuals and also between muscle groups

for muscle growth the best way to measure is to count reps that involve the recruitment of high threshold motor units while bar speed is slow. We can refer to these as "stimulating reps." When lifting heavy loads, extra sets need to be done to compensate for the smaller number of stimulating reps per set. When lifting lighter loads, the same number of sets to failure will cause similar muscle growth, regardless of the weight or volume of load.
