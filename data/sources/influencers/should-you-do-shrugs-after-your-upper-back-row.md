# Should you do shrugs after your upper back row?

Should you do kelso shrugs after you fail your upper back rows to maximize upper back growth?

(imagine this was on the same machine)

we know to train your upper back you want to perform scapular retraction. this happens in both an upper back row and kelso shrug. and the idea is that after you reach failure your upper back row you can get some extra reps in on kelso shrugs to get extra gains. but is this actually something you do?

Retraction

it won't be very beneficial to superset the two exercises. as you have already reached your maximum tolerable perception of effort from failing on the rows your brain will be limiting the amount of muscle fibers that are active in the shrugs but have exponential amounts of fatigue. if you want to have more volume just rest a few minutes and do it as a straight set so you can recruit more fibers during it and avoid excessive fatigue.

There are multiple types of fatigue mechanism present during a strength training set.

Voluntarily increasing central motor command increases perception of effort, and the number of recruited motor units (and the number of activated muscle fibers)

Fatigue progression through the set
