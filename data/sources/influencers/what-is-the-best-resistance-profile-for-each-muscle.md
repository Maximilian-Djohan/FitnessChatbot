# What is the best resistance profile for each muscle?

What is the best resistance profile for each muscle? Where do you want the exercise to be the hardest?

What is a resistance profile? The resistance profile of an exercise is the change of resistance throughout the range of motion of a movement. The most common resistance profiles are ascending where it starts off easier and gets harder and descending where it starts out harder and gets easier.

Why do resistance profiles matter? when doing an exercise where there are multiple joint actions/muscles active the resistance profile of the exercise can determine what muscle you're "biasing". an example of this is in chest pressing. at the bottom your chest has best leverage and your triceps get better leverage towards lockout. so what resistance profiles do you want for each muscle group?

hard bottom easy top

Resistance Profiles that different muscles prefer:

DESCENDING
- Chest
- Upper Back
- Biceps
- Triceps - Long Head
- Calves
- Adductors - Hip Extension

ASCENDING
- Lats
- Front Delt
- Triceps - Medial/Lateral Head
- Brachioradialis
- Glutes
- Quads in Pressing

Then for some muscles they have good leverage throughout their entire rom the resistance profile won't make any/little difference

Quads Leg Extension - any
Hamstrings Knee Flexion - any
Hamstring Hip Extension - midrange/bell
Add/Abduction - any
Side Delt - Descending
Rear Delt - Ascending

so basically when determining an exercise pick a stable one that matches up with the muscle's desired resistance profile.
