# Science Based Forearm Training

Science Based Forearm Training

the forearm is made up of a ton of muscles that contribute to elbow flexion, wrist flexion/extension, finger flexion/extension and pronation/supination, ulnar deviation and radial deviation.

(a) Radial deviation (b) Ulnar deviation

(c) Extension of wrist (d) Flexion of wrist

(e) Extension of fingers (f) Flexion of fingers (g) supination

but many people ask if there is even a point or if its "worth" to train the forearm. your forearm muscles won't have much growth potential but regardless if you want to grow it then it will be "worth" to you. in that case I would focus on exercises that cover cross sectional area of the forearm. looking at the muscle size of the differing forearm muscles finger flexion has the biggest PCSA with the next being wrist flexion and wrist extension after so how can you implement this?

you can cover both wrist flexion and finger flexion in the same exercise through a curling type exercise. you might think it would be better to isolate them but it wont matter much as they will have a low voluntary activation deficit due to their smaller size so you can effectively recruit them in a "compound" exercise. I would also recommend to do these with a straight arm since some of the muscles are biarticular.

after this you can choose to implement wrist extension though your main exercise will be the wrist curl. but if you want to add another I would recommend wrist extension as its next line in terms of cross sectional area.

so if you want to train your forearms go for it. I would mainly just implement finger flexion/wrist flexion curl and if you really want a wrist extension exercise. (also keep in mind your brachioradialis is a "forearm" muscle but it will be covered by biceps or brd biased curl)
