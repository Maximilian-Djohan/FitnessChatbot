# Training at your MRV is overrated

Training at your maximum recoverable volume is overrated. What is the best volume to train at?

With more people discussing that you can't maximize growth doing one set. Many people have stated that you should do your max recoverable volume so you can "maximize growth". But is this actually the best? How much volume should you do?

(me myself have also said that you should be close to your MVC)

Greater training volumes are associated with more rapid hypertrophy. Yet, each set does not cause the same amount of muscle growth. Later sets in a workout are less effective.

To check if you are recovering from volume you need to be progressing week to week on it. Your maximum recoverable volume is doing the upper limit of volume that you can progress from. But some people won't be progressing the fastest from this. Some people will progress faster from a middle ground of the minimum and maximum amount of volume they can handle. So what does this mean?

Muscle hypertrophy likely displays a non-linear dose-response relationship with workout volume that varies depending on the lifter and the muscle.

For example say you are progressing everything every 3 sessions doing a minimum volume of 10 sets. You raise the volume to your MVC progressing every 2 sessions on your MVC doing 20 sets a session. But say as you were slowly raising your volume to MVC at a middle ground of 15 sets you were progressing it every other session. This is an example of benefiting from less than the MVC but why is this?

You are both recovering from 20 and 15 sets but yet the one with lesser sets you progress faster with. This can happen as with more sets in a session you will have more intrasession fatigue which can hinder your progression on movements later in the session. Also realistically you might not be fully recovered from 20 sets by your session but enough to progress unlike something a little bit less like 15 sets which could also affect this.

During strength training, each additional set performed stimulates progressively less hypertrophy but causes progressively more muscle damage. Early sets are efficient insofar as they stimulate a great deal of muscle growth but very little muscle damage.

So what should you do? Test how much volume you can progress the fastest from. The total amount of sets will differ from person to person so do what works the best for you.
