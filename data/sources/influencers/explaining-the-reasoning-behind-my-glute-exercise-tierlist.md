# explaining the reasoning behind my glute exercise tierlist

explaining the reasoning behind my glute exercise tierlist

starting off to preface just because something is lower on the list does not mean its not effective for muscle growth. rather based on different criteria there will be better options if your goal is to "maximize" growth for said muscle.

starting off with F tier exercises these have very bad progressability as they are free weight exercises. and the single leg rdl can be good for the glutes but as you become more advanced using much heavier weights there will be too much lateral/rotational instability as you are on one leg causing it to be worse then any of the exercises higher up on the list

next in C tier these will all grow the glutes as you are training hip extension but due to how the exercise is it will involve a lot of other musculature which will be worse compared to exercises higher on the list.

in B tier i have pure hip extension hinges these will be great if performed with a bent knee to disadvantage the hamstrings via antagonistic inhibition but in general the hamstring and adductor magnus will still be contributing to a high degree causing other exercises to be better.

in A tier i have the cable kickback if performed with a very bent knee extending backwards while setting up peak resistance at the start of the movement youll be able to target the glutes effectively. but similar to the single leg rdl it will lack stability albeit a bit better since you can brace against the cable stack.

in S tier i have the hip abduction which is great for targeting the upper glute max, and glute medius/minimus unlike you would in hip extension. the only thing holding it back from S+ is due to "importance" as hip extension will contribute more towards your glute visual. but for maximizing glute growth it is needed.

in S+ i have the hip thrust which is the best exercise for the glutes due to setup and easy progression. the ghd will be very good for the glutes as well if performed like a back extension w/ barbell making it similar to a hamstring setup but with peak resistance at a shortened rom to better train the glutes.

so yes most of these exercises can be effective for growing your glutes but due to different criteria i ranked some exercises higher than others.
