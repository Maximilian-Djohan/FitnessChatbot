# How often should you be progressing in the gym?

How often should you be progressing in the gym?

we know that we need to be progressive overloading to make sure we are making progress in with muscle growth. but how often should you be progressing? the rate of progression will be different for everyone. there will also be many factors that contribute towards your rate of progression such as training advancement, sleep, diet, programming with exercise selection, frequency and volume and lifestyle. so how often should progression be?

Progressive overload is a training principle where you gradually increase the demands placed on your body during exercise to stimulate continued improvement in strength, endurance, or muscle mass.

you shouldnt worry about trying to progress within a certain amount of time. if all variables are in check then progression will come. you shouldn't try to force it or let outcomes ruin motivation. progression will come with good training and lifestyle choices but it will be different for everyone. if you are progressing within 1-2 weeks you will be fine.

what if you arent progressing over a period of time and regressing every session? if you arent progressing and seeing constant regression over a longer period of time then you should reevaluate whether your lifestyle choices are in check and also look over your programming. but many people make the mistake of trying to rush progression. while you should have the drive to progress as much as possible whether you are progressing over the long term is what matters.

Unintentionally regressing your lifts in weightlifting means experiencing a decrease in your strength or performance without actively choosing to decrease the difficulty of your workouts.
