# does you body fat change muscle growth?

Is muscle growth slower being at an unhealthy vs healthy bodyfat? Why and how much?

often in my dieting videos ive spread the idea that you should try to maintain a healthy bodyfat percentage around 10-15% for men and ~20% for women. why is this? well being at a healthier bodyfat percentage will help with better insulin sensitivity, better hormone balance, and better cardiovascular endurance while training etc. these are all factors that will only help with muscle growth. Better insulin sensitivity enhances muscle growth by optimizing nutrient partitioning, ensuring that glucose and amino acids are directed into muscle tissue for fuel and repair rather than being stored as fat. Improved sensitivity increases the efficacy of insulin as an anabolic hormone, which stimulates protein synthesis, reduces muscle catabolism

well I 100% agree with all the benefits and promote it when looking at data comparing muscle growth between overweight and normal weight individuals the difference are so minute they dont reach a statistical significance between groups. so what does this mean?

strictly for muscle growth you can very effectively if not maximally grow while being overweight. but in terms of positive health benefits and the reasoning of better insulin sensitivity, better hormone balance, and better cardiovascular endurance it would be better to be at a healthy body fat percentage also with the benefit of looking better being leaner. but if your higher than that or are younger it doesn't have to be something to over analyze.
